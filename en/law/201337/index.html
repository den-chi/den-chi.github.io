Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Tax Policies in the Nationwide Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries Cai Shui [2013] No. 37 (Expired)

2013/5/24

Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Tax Policies in the Nationwide Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries

Cai Shui [2013] No. 37

May 24, 2013

Finance departments, the state administrations of taxation, local taxation bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities directly under State planning, finance bureau of Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps:

According to the requirements of the State Council to further expand the pilot collection of value added tax in lieu of business tax (hereinafter referred to as BT to VAT) in the transportation industry and certain modern services industries, the relevant issues are hereby circularized as follows:

Article 1 The nationwide pilot BT to VAT in the transportation industry and certain modern services industries has been approved by the State Council, and will be carried out on August 1, 2013.The relevant regulations are hereby issued to you, and please comply with them.

Article 2 To carry out the nationwide pilot BT to VAT in the transportation industry and certain modern services industries, the involved works has wide range and the time is limited, but the task is heavy, which requires all the above mentioned departments to attach great importance to and effectively enforce the organization and leadership of the pilot with well organization, thorough arrangement, clear and definite duty allocation. Various effective measures shall be taken to make well all the preparations before the pilot and the monitoring and analysis, propaganda and interpretation during the pilot, so to ensure a steady, orderly and smooth reform. In case of any problem, please report to the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation in time.

Article 3 The Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries at Shanghai (Cai Shui [2011] No. 111), Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Zero Value Added Tax Rate and Tax Exemption Policies Applied to Taxable Services (Cai Shui [2011] No. 131), Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Several Tax Policies for Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries (Cai Shui [2011] No.133), Supplementary Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Several Tax Policies for Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries (Cai Shui [2012] No. 53), Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries at 8 Provinces and Cities such as Beijing etc. (Cai Shui [2012] No. 71), Supplementary Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Several Tax Policies for Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries and the Scope of Taxable Services (Cai Shui [2012] No. 86), Item 16 and Item 18 of Article 3 of the Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Several Policies for Business Tax (Cai Shui [2003] No. 16) shall be abolished since August 1, 2013.

Appendix I Implementing Measures for Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries

Chapter I Taxpayers and Withholding Agents

Article 1 Entities and individuals providing services of transportation and certain modern service industries (hereinafter referred to as the "taxable services") within the territory of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as "within the territory") are VAT taxpayers. Taxpayers providing taxable services shall pay VAT according to these Measures, and will no longer pay BT.

Entities refer to enterprises, administrative entities, institutions, military entities, social organizations and other entities.

Individuals refer to individual industrial and commercial households and other individuals.

Article 2 Where an entity conducts business in way of contracting, leasing, being subordinate to another entity, and the contractor, lessee or subordinate person (hereinafter referred to as the "contractor") conducts the external business in name of the employer, the lesser or the be-subordinated person (hereinafter referred to as the "employer"), and the employer shall bear the relevant legal liability, the employer shall be the taxpayer. Otherwise, the contractor shall be the taxpayer.

Article 3 Taxpayers are divided into general taxpayers and small-scale taxpayers.

Taxpayers with annual sales tax amount payable services that VAT shall be levied on (hereinafter referred to as "annual sales tax amount payable services") exceeding the standards provided by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation are general taxpayers, and those with annual sales tax amount payable services not exceeding the prescribed standards are small-scale taxpayers.

Other individuals with annual sales tax amount payable services exceeding the provided standards are not general taxpayers; non-enterprise entities, enterprises, individual industrial and commercial households not constantly providing taxable services may choose to pay the tax as small-scale taxpayers.

Article 4 Where a small-scale taxpayer has sound accounting and is capable of providing accurate tax information, it may apply to the competent tax authority for qualification recognition as a general taxpayer and become a general taxpayer.

Sound accounting refers to the accounting conducted according to legal and effective vouchers and the account books are set in accordance with the State uniform accounting system.

Article 5 Taxpayers meeting the conditions set for general taxpayers shall apply to the competent tax authority for qualification recognition as general taxpayers. The specific recognizing measures shall be provided by the State Administration of Taxation.

Except as otherwise regulated by the Administration of Taxation, once a taxpayer has been identified as a general taxpayer, it may not be re-identified as a small-scale taxpayer.

Article 6 Where an entity or an individual located outside the territory of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as "overseas") provides taxable services within the territory without establishing operating agency within the territory, their agent shall be the withholding agent for value-added tax; where there is no agent within the territory, the receiving party shall be the VAT withholding agent.

Article 7 Two or more taxpayers may be regarded as one taxpayer with approval from the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation and pay consolidated taxes. The specific measures shall be separately formulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation.

Chapter II Taxable Services

Article 8 Taxable services refer to services of land transportation, marine transportation, air transport, pipeline transportation, R&D and technical services, information technology services, culture creativity services, logistics supporting services, tangible personal property leasing services, identification and consulting services, radio, television and film services.

The specific scope of taxable services shall be carried out according to the Annotations of Taxable Services Scope enclosed in these Measures.

Article 9 Providing taxable services refer to the providing of paid taxable services, excluding the taxable services provided in non-operating activities.

Paid refers to acquisition of money, goods or other economic interests.

Non-operating activities refer to:

  1. Activities of receiving government funds or the administrative and institutional fees by non-enterprise entities for the performance of state administration and public service functions in accordance with the laws and administrative regulations.

  2. Taxable services provided by employees hired by entities or individual industrial and commercial households to the entities or the households.

  3. Taxable services provided by entities or individual industrial and commercial households to their employees.

  4. Other situations regulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation.

Article 10 Providing taxable services within the territory refers to the conditions that the provider or the receiving party of the taxable services is located within the territory.

The following conditions are not providing taxable services within the territory:

  1. Overseas entities or individuals provide taxable services to entities or individuals within the territory that are completely consumed overseas.

  2. Overseas entities or individuals lease tangible personal property to entities or individuals within the territory that are completely used overseas.

  3. Other situations regulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation.

Article 11 The following activities conducted by entities or individual industrial and commercial households are regarded as providing taxable services.

  1. Providing free services in the transportation industry and certain modern services industries to other entities or individuals, excluding those provided for public benefit or provided to the public.

  2. Other situations regulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation.

Chapter III Tax Rates and Percentage Charges

Article 12 VAT Rate

  1. To provide leasing services of tangible personal property, the tax rate shall be 17%.

  2. To provide transportation services, the tax rate shall be 11%.

  3. To provide services in modern services industry (with the exception of leasing services of tangible personal property), the tax rate shall be 6%.

  4. For the taxable services specified by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation, the tax rate is zero.

Article 13 The percentage charges of VAT is 3%.

Chapter IV The Calculation of the Tax Amount Payable

Section 1 General Provisions

Article 14 Tax methods of VAT include general tax methods and simple tax methods.

Article 15 General tax methods shall be adopted for the taxation on taxable services provided by general taxpayers.

Simple tax methods may be adopted for the taxation on particular taxable services prescribed by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation and provided by general taxpayers, and once the methods have been chosen, they may not be changed within 36 months.

Article 16 Simple tax methods shall be adopted for the taxation on taxable services provided by small-scale taxpayers.

Article 17 Overseas entities or individuals provide taxable services within the territory without setting up operating agent within the territory, the withholding agents shall calculate the tax withheld in accordance with the following formula:

Withheld tax amount = amount paid by the receiving party /(1 + tax rate) × tax rate

Section 2 General Tax Methods

Article 18 The tax amount payable calculated in accordance with general tax methods is the balance between the output VAT for the current period and the input VAT for the current period. Formula for the calculation of tax amount payable:

Tax amount payable = output VAT for the current period - input VAT for the current period

Where the output VAT for the current period is less than the input VAT for the current period and cannot be deducted, the insufficient part may be carried forward to the next taxation period to continue the deduction.

Article 19 The output VAT refers to the VAT amount calculated with the sales amount and the VAT rate of the taxable services provided by the taxpayers.

Formula for the calculation of output VAT:

Output VAT = sales amount × tax rate

Article 20 The sales amount used for the calculation of tax in accordance with general tax methods does not include the output VAT. Where a taxpayer make the pricing with consolidation of the sales amount and the output VAT, the sales amount shall be calculated in accordance with the following formula:

Sales amount = sale amount with tax included /(1+ rate)

Article 21 Input VAT refers to the VAT paid or borne by the taxpayers when they purchase goods or receive processing, repairs and replacement taxable services.

Article 22 The following input VAT are allowed to be deducted from the output VAT:

  1. The VAT amount indicated on the VAT special invoice (including VAT special invoices for freight transportation, tax-control uniform invoice for the sales of motor vehicles, the same below) obtained from the sellers or the providers.

  2. The VAT amount indicated on the special payment letter for custom import VAT obtained from the Customs.

  3. The input VAT calculated with the purchasing price indicated on the purchase invoice or sales invoice of agricultural products and the deduction rate of 13% when the agricultural products are imported, unless VAT special invoice or the special payment letter for custom import VAT is obtained.

The formula for calculation is:

Input VAT = purchase price × deduction rate

Purchase price refers to the price indicated on the purchase invoice or sales invoice of agricultural products obtain by the taxpayers when purchasing agricultural products and the Tobacco Leaf Tax paid according the regulations.

  1. The input VAT calculated with the amount of fees for the transportation indicated on the settlement voucher of transportation costs and the deduction rate of 7% where railway transport services are received.

Formula for the calculation of input VAT:

Input VAT = amount of transportation costs × deduction rate

Amount of transportation costs refers to the transportation costs (including transportation costs of railway near to pipeline and special railway), construction funds, excluding the handling charges, premium or other incidental expenses indicated on the settlement documents of railway transportation costs.

  1. The VAT indicated on the tax payment voucher of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the tax payment voucher) for the tax payment obtained from the tax authority or the agent within the territory where taxable services are received from overseas entities or individuals.

Article 23 Where the VAT credit documents obtained by the taxpayers do not comply with the laws, administrative regulations or the relevant provisions of the State Administration of Taxation, the input tax may not be deducted from the output tax.

The VAT credit documents refer to the VAT special invoice, special payment letter of custom import VAT, purchase invoice of agriculture products, sales invoice of agriculture products, settlement documents of railway transportation costs and tax payment certificate.

Where a taxpayer is to deduct the input VAT on the bases of tax payment voucher, it shall has written contract, proof of payment, account statement from overseas entities or invoice.

Where the documents are not completed, the input VAT may not be deducted from the output VAT.

Article 24 The input VAT of the following items may not be deducted from the output VAT:

  1. The items with tax calculated in accordance with simple tax methods, non-VAT taxable items, VAT exempt items, goods purchasing and the receiving of processing, repairing services or the taxable services dedicated to the collective welfare or personal consumption. The fixed assets, patents, non-patented technology, goodwill, trademarks, copyrights, tangible personal property leasing involved in the above items refer only to the fixed assets, patents, non-patented technology, goodwill, trademarks, copyrights, tangible personal property leasing dedicated to the above mentioned items.

  2. Goods purchasing with abnormal losses and the relevant processing and repairing services and transportation industry services.

  3. Goods purchasing (excluding fixed assets), processing and repairing services or transportation industry services consumed by goods in process and finished products with abnormal losses.

  4. The received passengers' transportation services.

Article 25 Non-VAT taxable items refer to the non-VAT taxable services, transferring of intangible assets (excluding patents, non-patented technology, goodwill, trademarks, and copyrights), sales of real estate and real property under construction.

Non-VAT taxable labor services refer to the BT taxable labor services of items not listed in the Annotations of Taxable Services Scope.

Real estate refers to immovable property or the property the moving of which will result in the change of the nature, shape, including buildings, structures and other land attachments.

The real estate newly constructed, reconstructed, expanded, repaired, decorated by the taxpayers are all real estate under construction.

The personal consumption includes taxpayers' consumption for entertainment.

Fixed assets refer to the machines, machinery, transportation facilities and other production- and business-related equipments, tools, appliances, etc. with service life of more than 12 months.

Abnormal losses refer to the losses from being stolen, losing, mildewing and deterioration caused by ill management, and the losses of goods resulted from the confiscation or the forced self-destruction imposed by the law enforcement departments.

Article 26 Where a taxpayer applicable to tax calculated in accordance with general tax methods concurrently engages in business with tax calculated with simple tax methods, non-VAT taxable services, VAT free business, and it is impossible to divide the non-deductible input VAT, the non-deductible input VAT shall be calculated in accordance with the following formula:

Non-deductible input VAT = the total non-deductible input VAT for the current period × (sales amount of items with taxes calculated in accordance with simple tax methods for the current period + turnover of non-VAT taxable labor services + sales amount of the items free of VAT) / (total sales amount for the current period + total business turnover for the current period)

Tax authorities may, in accordance with the above formula and base on the annual data, clear the amount of non-deductible input VAT.

Article 27 In case the purchasing of goods, receiving of processing and repairing services or taxable services fall in the scope as stipulated in Article 24 (excluding items with taxes calculated in accordance with simple tax methods, non-VAT taxable services and VAT free items) and the input VAT have already been deducted, the above input VAT amount shall be deducted from the input VAT amount for the current period; where the input VAT amount cannot be decided, the input VAT amount shall be calculated on the basis of actual costs for the current period.

Article 28 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services with tax calculated according to general tax methods, the VAT amount returned to the buyer due to service suspension or allowance shall be deducted from the output VAT for the current period; the amount of VAT recovered for services suspension, withdrawal of imported goods or allowance shall be deducted from the input VAT for the current period.

Article 29 In any of the following situation, the tax amount payable shall be calculated with the sales amount and the VAT rate, and the input VAT may not be deducted, and the VAT special invoice may also not be used.

  1. Where the accounting system of a general taxpayer is not sound or a general taxpayer cannot provide accurate taxation information.

  2. Where a taxpayer shall apply for the qualification recognition as a general taxpayer and it fails to conduct the application.

Section 3 Simple Tax Methods

Article 30 The tax amount payable calculated in accordance with simple tax methods refers to the VAT amount calculated with sales amount and the VAT percentage charges, where the input VAT may not be deducted.

Formula for the calculation of tax amount:

Tax amount payable = sales amount ×percentage charges

Article 31 The sales amount in the calculation of tax in accordance with simple tax methods does not include the tax amount payable. Where the tax payer adopt the method of consolidating the sales amount and the amount of tax amount payable, the sale amount shall be calculated according to the following formula:

Sales amount = sales amount with tax included /(1+ percentage charges)

Article 32 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services with tax calculated according to simple tax methods, the sales amount returned to the buyer due to service suspension or allowance shall be deducted from the sales amount for the current period.

The overpaid tax results from the balance after the deduction of the sales amount for the current period may be deducted from the later tax amount payable.

Section 4 Determination of Sales Amount

Article 33 Sales amount refers to the total price and other charges obtained by the taxpayers for providing of taxable services.

Price excluding costs refer to the other costs of various natures charged other than the price, excluding the governmental funds or the revenue from administrative and institutional fees collected on behalf.

Article 34 The sales amount shall be calculated in RMB.

Where a taxpayer settles the sales amount in currencies other than RMB, the sales amount shall be converted into RMB, and the RMB exchange rate median price of the same day or on the first date of the current month when the sales occur may be chosen as the conversion rate. A taxpayer shall determine the conversion rate to be adopted in advance which may not be changed for 12 months after the determination.

Article 35 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services for which the tax shall be calculated with different tax rates or percentage charges, the sales amount shall be calculated separately with different rates or percentage charges; the tax calculation not conducted separately shall be calculated with the higher tax rate.

Article 36 Where a taxpayer is concurrently engaged in business taxable of BT, the sales amount of the taxable services and the business turnover of the taxable items of BT shall be calculated separately; for those not accounted separately, the sales amount shall be evaluated and determined by the competent tax authorities.

Article 37 Where a taxpayer is concurrently engage in tax free, tax reduction business, the sales amount shall be accounted separately; for those not accounted separately, the taxes may not be exempt or reduced.

Article 38 After providing the taxable services and issuing the VAT invoices, if the services providing has been suspended, allowance is given or the invoice is wrongly issued or other circumstances occur, a taxpayer shall issue the VAT special invoices in red in accordance with the provisions of State Administration of Taxation; in case VAT special invoices in red is not issued, the output VAT or sales amount may not be deducted in accordance with Article 28 and Article 32 of these Measures.

Article 39 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services and indicates the price and the discount separately in the same invoice, the sales amount shall be the price after discount; where the price and the discount are not separately indicated on the same invoice, the price shall be the sales amount and the discount may not be deducted.

Article 40 Where the price of the taxable services provided by the taxpayers is significantly lower or higher and there is no reasonable commercial purpose, or the situations set out in Article 11 of these Measures that shall be deemed as providing taxable services without sales amount occur, the competent tax authorities have the right to determine the sales amount in the following order:

  1. Determine according to the average price of the taxable services of the same kind provided by the taxpayer at resent time.

  2. Determine according to the average price of the taxable services of the same kind provided by other taxpayers at resent time.

  3. Determine according to the composite assessable price. Formula for composite assessable price is:

Composite assessable price = Cost × (1 + rate of cost profit)

The rate of cost profit shall be determined by the State Administration of Taxation.

Chapter V Occurrence Time of Tax Payment and Tax Withholding Obligation and Place of Tax Payment

Article 41 Occurrence time of VAT tax obligations is:

  1. The day on which the taxpayers provide taxable services and receive the sales proceeds or credentials to obtain the sales proceeds; In case the invoice is issued in advance, it is the day of the invoicing.

Receiving of sales proceeds refers to that the taxpayers receive payment while providing taxable services or after the completion of the services providing.

The day on which the sales proceeds credentials are obtained refers to the date of payment as determined in the written contract; Where there is no written contract or no date of payment is determined in the contract, it refers to the day on which the taxable services are completed.

  1. Where a taxpayer provides tangible personal property leasing services and receive advance payment, the occurrence time for the tax payment obligations is the same day on which the payment in advance is received.

  2. Where a taxpayer is regarded as providing taxable services as stipulated in Article 11 of these Measures, the tax payment obligations occur on the same day on which the taxable services are completed.

  3. VAT withholding obligations occur on the same day when the taxpayers' obligations to pay VAT arise.

Article 42 Place of VAT payment shall be:

  1. An entity with fixed business place shall make tax declaration with the competent tax authority where its agent or residence is located. Where the head office and branches are not located in the same county (city), the tax declaration shall be made with their respective local competent tax authorities; Upon the approval from the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation or their authorized financial and tax authorities, the tax declaration may be made in consolidation by the head office with the competent tax authorities where the head office is located.

  2. An entity without fixed business place shall make the tax declaration with the competent tax authorities where the taxable services occur; Where the tax declaration is not made, the tax authorities where its agency or the residence are collected shall collect the overdue taxes.

  3. A withholding agent shall make declaration with the competent tax authorities where its agents or residence are located and pay the withheld taxes.

Article 43 The terms for VAT payment are one day, three days, five days, ten days, fifteen days, one month or a Quarter respectively. The specific term of tax payment of the taxpayers shall be determined by the competent tax authorities according to the amount of the taxpayers' tax amount payable. The provision that the tax payment term is a quarter shall apply to small-scale taxpayers as well as the other taxpayers prescribed by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation. Where the taxes cannot be paid according to the fixed terms, they may be paid time-to-time.

Where a tax payment term of a taxpayer is a month or a quarter, the taxpayer shall make the tax declaration within 15 days after the expiration date; Where a tax payment term is one day, five days, ten days or 15 days, the taxpayer shall pay the taxes in advance within five days after the expiration date, and make the tax declaration and settlement of tax amount payable of the last month within 15 days from the first date of the next month.

The term for the withholding agent to deliver the withheld tax shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of the preceding two paragraphs.

Chapter VI Tax Deduction and Exemption

Article 44 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services for which the VAT may be exempted or deducted according to the relevant regulations, it may waiver the exemption or deduction right and pay the VAT according to the provisions of these Measures. When the deduction or exemption rights are waived, a taxpayer may not apply for deduction or exemption within 36 months.

Where a taxpayer provides taxable services for which the rules of exemption and zero tax rate shall apply, the zero tax rate shall be applied in priority.

Article 45 Where the sales amount of tax payable services provided by individuals does not reach the VAT threshold, the VAT shall be exempted; When the sales amount reaches the VAT threshold, the VAT shall be fully calculated and paid.

The rules of VAT threshold may not apply to the individual industrial and commercial households that are recognized as general taxpayers.

Article 46 The range for VAT threshold is as follows:

  1. When the VAT is paid in fixed period, it is CNY5,000-CNY20,000 (including the number mentioned) of the taxable sales amount of one month.

  2. When the VAT is paid in different times, it is CNY300-CNY500 (including the number mentioned) of the sales amount for one time (day).

The adjustment of the thresholds shall be stipulated by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation.

Finance departments (bureau) and the state administrations of taxation of all provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government shall, within the prescribed range and in consideration of the actual conditions, determine the thresholds that shall be applied in the local regions and make report to the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation for filing.

Chapter VII Administration on Taxation

Article 47 The State Administration of Taxation shall be responsible for the collection of the VAT collection in lieu of BT.

Article 48 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services with zero tax rate, it shall make declaration with the competent tax authorities in time and manage the tax returning (exemption), where the specific measures shall be stipulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation.

Article 49 Where a taxpayer provides taxable services, it shall issues VAT special invoices to the receiving parties who ask for VAT special invoices and indicate the sales amounts and the output VATs on the VAT special invoices.

In any of the following situation, the VAT special invoice may not be issued:

  1. Providing taxable services to individual consumers.

  2. Providing taxable services that the rules of VAT exemption shall apply to.

Article 50 Where providing taxable services and the recipient asks for VAT invoices, a small-scale taxpayer may apply to the competent tax authorities to issue the invoices on behalf.

Article 51 The collection and administration of VAT shall be carried out in accordance with these Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Levying and the current regulations related to collection and management of VAT.

Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 52 Taxpayers shall conduct the accounting of VAT in accordance with national unified accounting system.

Article 53 These Measures shall come into effect on August 1, 2013.

Appendix: Annotations of Taxable Services Scope

I. Transportation industry

Transportation industry refers to the business of transporting goods or passengers to the destination and making their spatial locations change, including land transport services, marine transportation services, air transport services and pipeline transport services.

  1. Land transport services.

Land transport services refer to the business of transporting goods or passenger by road (on the ground or underground), including road transport, cable cars transport, cableway transportation and other land transport, but temporarily excluding rail transport.

The management fees collected by the taxi companies from taxi drivers using the company's own taxi cabs shall be imposed VAT as land transportation services.

  1. Marine transportation services.

Marine transportation services refer to the business of transporting goods or passenger by natural or artificial waterways of rivers or lakes, etc. or maritime waterways.

The voyage charter, time charter businesses in ocean transportation are waterway transport services.

Voyage charter business refers to the business where the ocean shipping companies complete a particular transport task for the charterers and receive rental fees.

Time charter business refers to the business where the ocean shipping companies lease ships equipped with operators to others for a certain time, in the leasing period the ships are under the lessee's disposal whether or not in operating, the companies will charge rental fee to the lessee by days, and all the fixed costs occurred are borne by the ship owners.

  1. Air transport services.

Air transport services refer to the businesses of transporting goods or passengers by airway.

Wet lease business in air transport is air transport service.

Wet lease business refers to the business where the air transport enterprises lease airplanes equipped with flight crew to others for a certain time, in the leasing period the ships are under the lessee's disposal whether or not in operating, the companies will charge rental fee form the lessee by certain standards, and all the fixed costs occurred are borne by the lessees.

  1. Pipeline transport services.

Pipeline transport services refer to the transporting of gases, liquids, solids by pipeline facilities.

II. Certain Modern Services Industries

Certain modern services industries refer to the business of providing technical, informative services in respect of manufacturing, cultural industries, modern logistics industry etc., including R&D and technical services, information technology services, cultural creativity services, logistics supporting services, tangible personal property leasing services, attestation consulting services, radio and television services.

  1. R&D and technical services.

R&D and technical services include research and development services, technology transfer services, technology consulting services, contractual energy management services, engineering reconnaissance and exploration services.

(1) Research and development services refer to the business of research and experimental development conducted in respect of new technologies, new products, new handicraft or new materials and the system thereof.

(2) Technology transfer services refer to the businesses of transferring the ownership or use right of patent or non-patent technologies.

(3) Technology consulting services refer to the business of providing projects feasibility studies, technical forecast, specialized technical investigation, analysis and evaluation report and expertise consulting etc. for specific technical objects.

(4) Contractual energy management services refer to the business where the energy service companies and energy using entities stipulate energy saving targets in contracts, the energy saving service companies provide the necessary services, and the energy using entities based on the energy-saving effect pay the investment of the energy service companies and the reasonable remuneration.

(5) Engineering reconnaissance and exploration services refer to the on-site investigation on topography, geological structure, and underground resource endowments conducted before the mining and the construction

  1. Information and technology services.

Information and technology services refer to the business of production, collection, handling, processing, storage, transportation, retrieval and utilization of information by means of computers and communication networks, etc., and providing of information services. The services include software services, circuit design and testing services, information system services and business process management services.

(1) Software services refer to the business of providing software development services, software consulting services, software maintenance services and software testing services, etc.

(2) Circuit design and testing services refer to the business of providing integrated circuits and electronic circuit design, testing and related technical supporting services, etc.

(3) Information system services refer to the business of providing information systems integration, network management, desktop management and maintenance, information systems applications, basic Information and Technology management platform integration, Information and Technology infrastructure management, data center, hosting centers, security service. The services include the network operating services provided by the network to the non-owned online games.

(4) Business process management services refer to the business of providing human resources management, financial economic management, financial payment services, internal data analysis, call center services and e-commerce platform, etc. relying on computer information technology.

  1. Culture creativity services.

Culture creativity services include design services, trademark and copyright transfer services, intellectual property services, advertising services, and convention and exhibition services.

(1) Design services refer to the business of passing out plans, programs and ideas through visual, text and other forms, including industrial design, shape design, costume design, environmental design, graphic design, packaging design, animation design, exhibition design, website design, mechanical design, engineering design, advertising design, creative planning, and printing blueprint and so on.

(2) Trademarks and copyrights transfer services refer to the business of transferring trademarks, goodwill and copyrights.

(3) Intellectual property services refer to the activities dealing with intellectual property matters, including the services of agency, registration, identification, assessment, certification, consulting, search for patents, trademarks, copyrights, software, integrated circuit design.

(4) Advertising services refer to the business of publicizing and providing related services for the matters such as goods, business services, sports programs or notices, declaration, etc. entrusted by the clients through books, newspapers, magazines, radio, television, films, slides, signs, posters, windows, neon signs, light boxes, Internet and so on, including the advertising agencies and the publishing, broadcasting, publicity and display etc. of advertisement.

(5) Convention and exhibition services refer to the business of holding or organizing various exhibitions and conferences for the circulation of commodities, promotion, exhibition, trade talks, civil communication, corporate communication, international activities and so on.

  1. Logistics supporting services. Logistics supporting services include air services, port services, freight and passenger stations services, salvage services, freight agent services, customs clearance agency services, warehousing services and loading, unloading and carrying services.

(1) Air services include aviation ground services and general aviation services.

Aviation ground services refer to the business of ground services such as navigation and so on provided by the airlines, airports, civil aviation authorities, terminals, etc. to overseas aircrafts or any other aircrafts flying or staying within the territory, including passenger security screening services, apron management services, airport boarding launch management services, aircraft cleaning and disinfection services, air flight management services, aircraft taking off and landing services, flight communications services, ground signal services, aircraft security services, runways management services, air traffic management services etc.

General aviation services refer to the business of providing flight services for professional works, including aerial photography, aerial survey, aviation exploration, aerial forest protection, aviation hanging spraying, aviation rainfall etc.

(2) Port terminal services refer to the business of providing services for vessels such as port ship scheduling services, ship communications services, channel management services, dredging services, lighthouse management services, beacon management services, pilotage services, cargo handling services, untwisting services, berthing and shifting berth services, offshore oil spill removal services, water transportation management services, professional cleaning, disinfection and inspection services of the vessel and oil spill prevention service of the vessel, etc.

VAT for the port facility security fees charged by the port facility operators shall be collected as "port terminal services".

(3) Freight and passenger station services refer to the business of providing cargo stowage services, transport organization services, transit transfer service, vehicle scheduling, ticketing services and car parking services etc. by the cargo passenger stations (excluding rail transport).

(4) Salvage services refer to the business of providing ship personnel aid, vessel property salvage, water rescue and sunken objects salvage services.

(5) Freight agent services refer to the business where an agent accepts the entrustment of the goods consignee, consignor, ship owner, charterer or ship operators to go through the business procedures related to cargo and shipping agency for the clients such as cargo shipment, port entrance and exit of the ships, arrangement of pilotage, berthing, loading and unloading, etc. in the client's name or in its own name without providing cargo transportation services directly.

(6) Customs clearance agency services refer to the business of agency in customs clearance procedures entrusted the consignee, consignor of the imported and exported cargo.

(7) Warehousing services refer to the business of deposit and keep cargo in warehouse, garages or other places.

(8) Loading, unloading and carrying services refer to the business of loading, unloading and carrying goods using material handling tools, human power or animal power between the means of transport, between loading and unloading sites or between means of transport and loading and unloading sites.

  1. Tangible personal property leasing services.

Tangible personal property leases include tangible personal property finance leases and operating leases of tangible personal property.

(1) Tangible personal finance lease refers to the leasing of tangible personal property with financing nature and characteristics of the transfer of ownership, namely the lessor shall, according to the lessee's required specifications, models, function and other conditions, purchase tangible personal property and lease it to the lessee, where the ownership of the equipment belongs to the lessor, the lessee has only the right of use within the contract period, after the expiration of the contract and the rent is paid, the lease is entitled to purchase the tangible personal property at the left value, to take the ownership. No matter the lessor will sell the tangible personal property to the lessee, such leases are classified as finance leases.

(2) Operating lease of tangible personal property refers to the business of transferring tangible personal property items, equipments within the agreed time to others without changing the ownership of the leased property.

Bareboat charter business of ocean transport and dry lease business of air transport are operating leasing of tangible personal property.

Bareboat charter business refers to the business where ocean shipping companies lease ships within the agreed time to others without operator, do not assume various expenses that occur during the transportation, and only charge a fixed rental fee.

Dry lease business refers to the business where air transport enterprises lease aircrafts within the agreed time to others without crew, do not assume various expenses that occur during the transportation, and only charge a fixed rental fee.

  1. Identification and consulting services.

Identification and consulting services include certification services, identification services and consulting services.

(1) Certification services refer to the businesses conducted by entities with professional qualifications to proof products, services and management systems in conformity with the relevant technical specifications, mandatory requirements or standards of the relevant technical specifications by means of testing, inspection, measurement etc.

(2) Identification services refer to the businesses conducted by entities with professional qualifications to verify the economic activities and related documents of the clients, and publish opinions that has probative force, including accounting identification, tax identification , legal identification, engineering cost identification, asset assessment, environmental assessment, real estate land evaluation, examination and approval of construction drawing, identification of medical malpractice, etc.

(3) Consulting services refer to the business of providing and planning information or advice on financial, tax, legal, internal management, business operations and process management, etc.

Bookkeeping in agent shall be imposed VAT as "consulting services".

  1. Radio, film and television services.

Radio, film and television services include the production services, distribution services, and broadcast (including screenings, the same below) services for radio, film and television programs (works).

(1) Radio, film and television programs (works) production services refer to the producing services of radio, film and television programs and works such as special topics (special programs), columns, varieties, sports, cartoons, radio plays, TV plays, movies, etc., specifically including radio and television programs and work-related planning, editing, filming, sound recording, production of audio and video text picture material, scene layout, post clips, translation (compilation), subtitling, titles, credits, trailers, producing of special effects, film restoration, cataloging, the right confirmation and other business activities.

(2) Radio, film and television programs (work) distribution services refer to the business of distributing radio and television programs (works) to entities and individuals such as cinema, radio, television, websites, etc. by ways of sharing payment, buy-out, commission, agency, etc., and transfer of coverage and broadcasting rights of sporting events and other activities.

(3) Radio, film and television programs (work) broadcasting services refer to business of broadcasting the radio and television programs (work) at the cinema, theater, video halls and other places, as well as through radio station, television station, satellite communications, internet, cable television and other broadcast wireless or wired devices.

Appendix II Provisions on Matters Concerning the Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries

I. Relevant policies for pilot taxpayers [refer to the taxpayers who shall pay the VAT in accordance with the Implementing Rules for the Pilot Collection of Value Added Tax in Lieu of Business Tax in the Transportation Industry and Certain Modern Services Industries (hereinafter referred to as the "Implementing Measures for Pilot")

  1. Mixed Operation.

Where a pilot taxpayer sales goods, or provides processing, repairing labor or taxable services with different tax rates or percentage charges, the sales amount that different tax rates or percentage charges shall be applied to shall be accounted separately, and for those not accounted separately, the tax rate or percentage charges shall be applied in accordance with the following methods:

(1) For the sale of goods, providing of processing, repairing and replacement services or taxable services with different tax rates, the higher tax rate shall be applied to.

(2) For the sale of goods, providing of processing, repairing and replacement services or taxable services with different percentage charges, the higher percentage charge shall be applied to.

(3) For the sale of goods, providing of processing, repairing and replacement services or taxable services with different tax rates and percentage charges, the higher tax rate shall be applied to.

  1. Enterprises operating oil and gas field.

The VAT for taxable services provided by oil and gas field operating enterprises shall be accounted with the VAT rate regulated in the Implementing Measures for Pilot, and the VAT rate regulated in the Circular of the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation on the Printing and Issuing of the Administrative Measures for the Value Added Tax for Enterprises Operating Oil and Gas Filed (Cai Shui [2009] No. 8) may no longer be applicable.

  1. Air transport enterprises.

(1) VAT shall not be levied on the air transportation services provided by air transport enterprises to the passengers who have exchanged the air transportation services with mileage credits.

(2)The free air transport services provided by air transport enterprises according to the national order are the services provided for public welfare as regulated in Article 11 of the Implementing Measures for Pilot, where VAT shall not be levied on.

(3) The VAT taxable sales amount of the air transport enterprises does not include the airport construction fee collected on behalf and the amount collected and transferred by selling of tickets for other air transport enterprises.

(4) The income for late ticket that have already been sold without providing services yet are not the VAT taxable income, where VAT shall not be levied on.

  1. Sales amount.

For the tangible personal property financial leasing services provide by the pilot taxpayers engage in financial leasing after being approved by the People's Bank of China, the Ministry of Commerce, China Banking Regulatory Commission, the sales amount shall be the balance of the total price and expenses (including residual value) after deduction of the loan interest (including foreign currency loan and RMB loan interest), customs duties, import taxes, installation fees, insurance borne by the lessor.

For the deduction of price from the total price and the price excluding costs, a taxpayer shall obtain valid vouchers in compliance with the relevant laws, administrative regulations provisions of the State Administration of Taxation, otherwise, it may not be deducted.

The above vouchers refer to:

(1) For the payment paid to the entities or individuals within the territory, the invoices are the valid vouchers.

(2) For the paid amount of taxes, the payment certificate is the legally valid voucher.

(3) For the payment paid to entities or individuals overseas, the documents signed by the entities or individuals are the legal and valid vouchers. In case the tax authority doubt about the signed documents, it may ask for the providing of confirmation of overseas notary.

(4) Other credentials prescribed by the State Administration of Taxation.

  1. The settlement documents issued for transport costs (excluding settlement documents of rail transport costs) acquired by the pilot taxpayers after August 1, 2013 (inclusive) may not be used as tax deduction voucher.

  2. Qualification recognition of general taxpayers.

The annual sales standard of taxable services regulated in Article 3 of the Implementing Measures for Pilot is CNY5 million (including the number mentioned).

The Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation may adjust the annual sales standard of taxable services according to the pilot situation.

  1. Tax methods.

(1) The VAT of public transport services provided by general taxpayers among the pilot taxpayers may be calculated in accordance with simple tax method. Public transport services include passenger ferry, public transport, rail transportation (including subway and urban light rail), taxis, long-distance passenger transport, and shuttle, among which, the shuttle is a kind of land transport that transporting passengers on fixed route, fixed-time and stop at fixed-stations.

(2) For the operating lease services provided by the general taxpayers among the pilot taxpayers with tangible personal property purchased or homemade before the implementation of the pilot, the VAT may be accounted in accordance with the simple tax method during the pilot period.

(3) Where a taxpayer among the pilot taxpayers sales goods and provides processing and repairing services concurrently, and there is no regulation that general tax method may be chosen to calculate the VAT, the VAT of all the sales amount shall be calculated in accordance with general tax method.

  1. Business occurred before the pilot.

(1) Where a pilot taxpayer sign lease contract before the date of the implementation of the pilot in the same region and the execution has not been completed, before the expiry date of the contract, the BT shall continue to be paid in accordance with existing business tax policies.

(2) Where a pilot taxpayer provides taxable services, and the BT is differently collected in accordance with relevant provisions of the business tax policies of the state, and the total price and price excluding costs are not enough to offset the amount of allowed deduction, the amount which has not been deducted until the date for the pilot implementation may not be deducted when calculating the sales amount of the pilot taxpayer after the implementation of the pilot in the region, and application shall be made to the original competent local tax bureau for the refund of BT.

This provision does not apply to the tangible personal property leasing services for which the pilot taxpayer continues to pay the BT in accordance with paragraph 8 item 1 of this Article.

(3) Where a pilot taxpayer has paid the BT for the taxable services provided before the pilot has been implemented in the region, and refund occurs to deduct the turnover after the date (including the day mentioned) of implementation of the pilot in the region, it shall apply for a refund to the competent tax authorities for the already paid business tax.

(4) For the taxable services provided by the pilot taxpayer before the implementation of the pilot in the region, if supplementary tax shall be paid due to taxation inspection, the supplementary BT shall be paid in accordance with existing BT policies.

  1. Sales of used fixed assets.

Where a general taxpayer identified in accordance with the Implementing Measures for Pilot and these provisions sells self-used fixed assets purchased or homemade after the date (including the day mentioned) of the pilot carried out in the region, VAT shall be collected in accordance with the applicable tax rate; for selling of self-used fixed assets purchased or homemade before the date (including the day mentioned) of implementation of the pilot in the region, VAT shall be collected at halved rate of 4%.

Used fixed assets refer to the fixed assets that have been depreciated by the taxpayer in accordance with financial accounting system.

  1. Tax rate applicable to withheld VAT.

Where the agents or the recipients within the territory withhold taxes for overseas entities and individuals, VAT shall be withheld in accordance with the applicable tax rate.

II. Policies related to the original VAT payers [refer to the taxpayers who pay VAT in accordance with the Interim Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Value-added Tax (hereinafter referred to as the "Interim Regulations on VAT").

  1. Input VAT.

(1) Where a general taxpayer of the original VAT accepts the taxable services provided by the pilot taxpayer, the VAT amount indicated on the obtained invoices is the input tax and is allowed to be deducted from the output tax.

(2) In respect to motorcycles, cars, yachts for which consumption tax shall be collected for personal use by the original VAT general taxpayers, the input tax is allowed to be deducted from the output tax.

(3) Where an original VAT general taxpayer accepts taxable services provided by the overseas entities or individuals and VAT shall be withheld in accordance with the provisions, the input tax that is allowed to be deducted from the output tax is the amount of VAT indicated on the tax payment receipt of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "tax payment receipt") obtained from the tax authority or the agent.

When deducting the input tax on the basis of the tax payment receipt, the above mentioned taxpayer shall have a written contract, proof of payment and offshore statement of account or invoice. Otherwise, the input tax may not be deducted from the output tax.

(4) Where the original general VAT taxpayers purchase goods or receive processing repairing and installation labor services for the items listed in the Annotations of Taxable Services Scope which are not non-VAT taxes items as stipulated in Article 10 of the Provisional Regulations on VAT, the input tax is allowed to be deducted from the output tax.

(5) Where an original general taxpayer accepts the taxable services provided by the pilot taxpayer, the input tax of the following items shall not be deducted from the output tax:

A. Tax items calculated in accordance with simple tax method, non-VAT taxable items, VAT exempt items, collective welfare or personal consumption, where the patented technology, non-patent technology, goodwill, trademarks, copyrights, tangible real estate leasing involved in only refer to the patented technology, non-patent technology, goodwill, trademarks, copyrights, tangible personal property leases used for the above mentioned items.

B. The accepted passenger transportation services.

C. Transportation services related to goods purchasing with abnormal losses.

D. Transportation services related to goods purchasing consumed by goods in process and finished products with abnormal losses.

The above mentioned non-VAT taxable items refer to the non-VAT taxable items regulated in Article 10 of the Provisional Regulations on VAT, but excluding items listed in the Annotations of Taxable Services Scope.

E. The settlement documents issued for transport costs (excluding settlement documents of rail transport costs) acquired by the general taxpayer of original VAT after August 1, 2013 (inclusive) may not be used as tax deduction voucher.

Where an original VAT general taxpayer obtains the VAT special invoices issued by the tax authorities on behalf pilot small-scale taxpayers, the input tax is to be deducted in accordance with the amount indicated on the VAT special invoices.

  1. Qualification recognition of general taxpayer.

Where an original VAT general taxpayer engages in taxable services concurrently, and shall apply for the recognition as general taxpayer in accordance with the Implementing Measures for Pilot and paragraph 6 of Article 1 of these Regulations, there is no need to go through the general taxpayer recognition formalities again.

  1. Remaining tax credit at the end of VAT term.

Where an original VAT general taxpayer provides taxable services, the remaining credit at the end of VAT term occurred before the pilot implementation date shall not be deducted from the output tax of the taxable services.

Appendix IV Provisions on VAT Zero Rate and Tax Exemption and Abatement Policy applicable to taxable services

Article 1 Where entities and individuals within the People's Republic (hereinafter referred to as "within the territory") provide international transport services or research and development services and design services to overseas entities, the VAT shall be calculated with zero rate.

  1. International transport services refer to:

(1) Transporting passengers or cargo form within to outside the territory;

(2) Transporting passengers or cargo from outside to within the territory;

(3) Transporting passengers or cargo outside the territory;

  1. Where entities and individuals provide international transport services by waterway within the territory with VAT zero rate, they shall obtain the International Shipping Business license; provide international transport services in land, it shall obtain the Road Transport Business License and the International Motor Transport Driving License, and the business scope indicated in the Road Transport Business License shall include "international transportation"; provide international transport services by air, it shall obtain the Public Air Transport Enterprise Operating License and the scope of its business shall include "international air passenger, cargo and mail transportation business".

  2. Design services provided to overseas entities exclude design services provided for real estate located in the territory.

Article 2 The VAT of transport services from and to Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan as well as transport services in Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan (hereinafter referred to as "Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan Transportation Services") provided by entities and individuals within the territory shall be calculated with zero rate.

Where entities and individuals within the territory applicable to VAT zero rate provide transport services to Hong Kong in land, Macau, it shall obtain Road transport Business License and have transport vehicles with Road Transport Permit straightly to Hong Kong and Macao ; Where they provide transport services by waterway to Taiwan, they shall acquire Waterway Transport License on Taiwan Strait and has vessels with the Operating Permit on Taiwan Strait; Where they provided transportation by sea to Hong Kong, Macao traffic transport services, they shall have vessels with Hong Kong and Macao Line Operating License; Where they provide above mentioned transport services by air , they shall obtain the Public Air Transport Enterprise Operating License and the scope of its operations shall include "international and domestic (including Hong Kong and Macao) air passenger and mail transportation business".

Article 3 Where entities and individuals within the territory provide charter, voyage charter and wet lease services, if the leased means of transport are used for international transport services and Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan Transportation Services, the VAT zero rate shall not apply. The lessee shall apply for zero rate in accordance with the provisions.

Article 4 If the zero tax rate applicable taxable services provided by the entities and individuals within the territory are calculated with general VAT tax methods, the VAT will be returned with credit. The return rate is the VAT rate applicable according to Article 12 paragraphs 1 - 3 of the Pilot Implementing Measures; If simple tax method is applicable thereby, the VAT shall be exempted. If foreign trade enterprises concurrently provide taxable services with zero tax rate, the measures for tax refund and exemption shall be unified implemented.

Article 5 Where entities and individuals within the territory provide the taxable services with VAT zero rate, they may give up the zero tax and choose the tax exemption or pay the VAT in accordance with the regulations. When the zero rate is given up, zero rate application shall not be conducted within 36 months.

Article 6 Where an entity or individual within the territory provides taxable services with zero tax rate, it shall, on a monthly basis, apply to the competent authority for the tax refund or VAT exemption. Specific administrative measures shall be separately formulated by the State Administration of Taxation in consultation with the Ministry of Finance.

Article 7 The following taxable services provided by entities and individuals within the territory shall be exempt from VAT, except for those regulated by the Ministry of Finance and State Administration of Taxation with zero tax rate:

  1. Engineering survey services with the engineering, mineral resources outside the territory.

  2. Conference and exhibition services with conference and exhibition place outside the territory.

  3. Warehousing services with storage location outside the territory.

  4. Tangible personal property leasing services with the subject used outside the territory.

  5. Radio, film and television programs (work) issuing and broadcasting services provide outside the territory.

  6. International transport services in line with the conditions provided in Article 1 paragraph 1 but not in accordance with the conditions in Article I paragraph 2 of these Provisions.

  7. Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan Transportation Services in line with the conditions provided in Article 2 paragraph 1 but not in accordance with the conditions in Article 2 paragraph 2 of these Provisions

  8. The following taxable services provided to the entities outside the territory:

(1) Technology transfer services, technical consulting services, contractual energy management services, software services, circuit design and testing services, information system services, business process management services, transfer services of trademarks and copyright, intellectual property services, logistics and supporting services (excluding warehousing services), identification services, forensic services, consulting services, radio, film and television programs (works) production services, charter services, voyage charter services and wet lease services. But does not include: the subject matter of the contract in the territory of the contract energy management services, domestic goods or real estate certification services, authentication services, and consulting services.

(2) Advertising services with advertising places outside the territory.
