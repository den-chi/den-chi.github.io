Circular of the State Administration of Taxation on Printing and Distributing the Implementing Measures for Special Tax Adjustments (for Trial Implementation)

Guo Shui Fa [2009] No. 2

January 8, 2009

To the state taxation bureaus and local taxation bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government and cities specifically designated in the state plan:

In order to implement the Law of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise Income Tax and its Implementation Regulations and regulate and strengthen the administration of special tax adjustments, the State Administration of Taxation has formulated the Implementating Measures for Special Tax Adjustments (Trial Implementation). These Measures are hereby issued to you for implementation.

Attachment: Implementating Measures for Special Tax Adjustments (for Trial Implementation)

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 In order to regulate the administration of special tax adjustments, these Measures are formulated in accordance with the Law of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise Income Tax (hereinafter referred to as the "Income Tax Law") and the Regulations on the Implementation of the Enterprise Income Tax Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law"), the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Levying (hereinafter referred to as the "Tax Administration Law") and the Implementing Rules of the Law of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Tax Levying (hereinafter referred to as the "Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law") and relevant provisions in agreements (or arrangements) for avoidance of double taxation entered into by and between the government of China and the government of any relevant country(or region) (hereinafter referred to the "tax treaties").

Article 2 These Measures shall apply to administration by taxation authorities over special tax adjustments such as transfer pricing, arrangements for advance pricing, agreements for cost apportion, controlled foreign enterprises, thin capitalization, and general anti-tax-avoidance.

Article 3 The term "administration related to transfer pricing" as used herein is a general term for work such as review and evaluations on, investigations over or adjustments to a transaction between an enterprise and any of its associated parties (hereinafter referred to as the "related-party transaction") carried out by a taxation authority in accordance with the relevant provisions of Chapter 6 of the Income Tax Law and Chapter 36 of the Tax Administration Law in order to determine whether or not such a transaction complies with the arm's length principle.

Article 4 The term "administration related to arrangements for advance pricing" as used herein is a general term for work such as review and evaluations by a taxation authority in accordance with the provisions of article 42 of the Income Tax Law and article 53 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law on pricing principles and calculation methods proposed by an enterprise for related-party transactions in the years to come and the conclusion of arrangements for advance pricing after negotiations by the taxation authority with the enterprise.

Article 5 The term "administration related to agreements for cost apportion" as used herein is a general term for work such as such as review and evaluations on, investigations over or adjustments to a agreement for cost apportion between an enterprise and any of its associated parties carried out by a taxation authority in accordance with the provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 41 of the Income Tax Law in order to determine whether or not such an agreement complies with the arm's length principle.

Article 6 The term "administration of controlled foreign enterprises" as used herein is a general term for work such as review and evaluations on and investigations over the non-distribution of or a reduction in the distribution of any controlled foreign enterprise's profits carried out by a taxation authority in accordance with the provisions of Article 45 of the Tax Law and adjustments made by such a taxation authority to any portion of any such enterprise's income attributed to any resident enterprise in China.

Article 7 The term "administration of thin capitalization" as used herein is a general term for work such as review and evaluations on, investigations over or adjustments to the ratio between debt investments and equity investments that an enterprise accepts from any of its associated parties carried out by a taxation authority in accordance with the provisions of Article 46 of the Income Tax Law in order to determine whether or not such ratio meets the required one and complies with arm's length principle.

Article 8 The term "general anti-tax avoidance administration" as used herein is a general term for work such as review and evaluations on, investigations over or adjustments to, by a taxation authority in accordance with the provisions of Article 47 of the Income Tax Law, a reduction in taxable income or earnings made by an enterprise for the implementation of any arrangement for other unreasonable commercial purposes.

Chapter II Associated Filing

Article 9 The term "associated relationship" as used in Article 109 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law and Article 51 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law mainly refers to any of the following relationships between an enterprise and another enterprise, organization or individual:

  1. Either party directly or indirectly holds 25% or more in aggregate of the shares of the other party; a third party directly or indirectly holds 20% or more in aggregate of the respective shares of both parties; where either party holds the shares of the other party through an intermediary, as long as such party holds 25% or more of the shares of the intermediary, the percentage by which such party holds the shares of the other party is the one by which the intermediary holds the shares of the other party;

  2. Borrowings by either part from the other party (with the exception of any independent financial institution) account for 50% or more of the actually paid-in capital of such party or 10% or more of the total amount of loans borrowed by such party is secured by the other party (with the exception of any independent financial institution);

  3. A majority of senior officers (including directors and managers) or no less than one senior director of either party who controls the board of directors is appointed by the other party or a majority of senor officers (including directors and managers) or no less than one senior director of both parties who controls the board of directors is appointed by a third party;

  4. A majority of senior officers (including directors and managers) of either party simultaneously acts as senior officers of the other party (including directors and managers), or no less than one senior director of either party who controls the board of directors simultaneously acts as a senior director of the other party;

  5. The normal production and operation activities of either party is unable to be conducted without the provision of industrial property, proprietary know-how or other franchises by the other party;

  6. The purchase and sales activities of either party are controlled by the other party;

  7. Labor services accepted or provided by either party are controlled by the other party; or

  8. Any other relationship in which the production and operation or transactions of either party is materially controlled by the other party or the interests of both parties are associated, including any relationship in which either party, without reaching the shareholding percentage specified in Item 1 hereof, is entitled to basically the same economic interest as the substantial shareholders of the other party, family relationships, kinships and other relationships.

Article 10 related-party transactions mainly include the following types:

  1. The purchase, sale, transfer and employment of tangible assets, including the business of purchasing, selling, transferring or leasing buildings, transport vehicles, machinery and equipment, tools, commodities, products and other tangible assets;

  2. The transfer and employment of intangible assets, including the business of transferring titles in franchises such as land-use rights, copyrights (author's rights), trademarks, lists of clients, distribution channels, license numbers, business secrets, and proprietary know-how and in industrial property such as industrial designs or utility models and of providing the rights to employ such franchises and industrial property;

  3. Financing funds, including all types of short and long-term loans and guarantees, all types of interest-bearing advance payments and delayed payments, and any other business; and

  4. The provision of labor services, including the provision of services such as market surveys, marketing, management, administrative affairs, technology services, maintenance, designs, consultancy, agency, scientific research, legal services, and accounting affairs.

Article 11 Any resident enterprise subject to audit collection and any non-resident enterprise which has establishments or offices in China and honestly report and pay enterprise income tax shall, in filing a tax return for the annual enterprise income tax with a tax authority, attach thereto a Statement of Enterprises of the People's Republic of China on Annual related-party transactions, including a Statement on Associated Relationships, a Statement on the Summary of related-party transactions, a Statement on Purchase and Sale, a Statement on Labor Services, a Statement on Intangible Assets, a Statement on Fixed Assets, a Statement on Financing Funds, a Statement on External Investments, and a Statement on Overseas Payments.

Article 12 Where it is really difficult for an enterprise to submit statements provided in Article 11 hereof within the required period of time and it is necessary to extend the required period, such extension shall be handled in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Tax Administration Law and the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law.

Chapter III Management of Same-period Documents

Article 13 An enterprise shall, in accordance with Article 114 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law, prepare and preserve same-period documents for its related-party transactions according to taxable years and submit such documents in compliance with requirements of a taxation authority.

Article 14 Same-period documents shall mainly include the following matters:

  1. Organizational structure

(1) The relevant organizational structure and equity structure of the group with which the enterprise in question is affiliated;

(2) Information on annual changes in the enterprise's associated relationships;

(3) Any associated party with which the enterprise enters into transactions, including any such associated party's name, legal representative, composition of senior officers such as directors and managers, registered address, actual business address as well as any associated individual's name, nationality, domicile, family members and other information, and any associated party that may have a direct impact on pricing with respect to the enterprise's related-party transactions is indicated ; and

(4) Categories of taxes with the nature of income tax applicable to all associated parties, their tax rates and applicable preferential tax treatments.

  1. Production and operation

(1) An overview of the enterprise's business, including an overview of the development and transformation of the enterprise, an overview of the sector in which the enterprise is and of the development of such sector, general economic and legal issues that have impact on the enterprise and such sector such as industry policies and industry restrictions, and the chain of business lines of the group with which the enterprise is affiliated and its position in such chain;

(2) The composition of the enterprise's principal business, income derived from the principal business, and the percentage of such income in the total income of the enterprise, profits derived from the principal business and the percentage of such profits in the total profits of the enterprise;

(3) The position of the enterprise in the sector in which the enterprise is and an analysis of relevant market competition conditions;

(4) The internal organization structure of the enterprise, relevant information on roles played by the enterprise and its associated parties in related-party transactions, assets employed, risks assumed, and other matters and a completed Form for the Analysis of the Capabilities and Risks of Enterprises;

(5) The consolidated financial statement of the group which may, depending on the financial year of the group, be deferred in preparation to no later than December 31 of the year following the one in which related-party transactions are entered into.

  1. related-party transactions

(1) Types of, parties to, time of, amount involved in, settlement currency for, transaction conditions for, and other aspects of related-party transactions;

(2) Trade modes adopted for related-party transactions, annual changes in such modes and reasons therefor;

(3) Business processes for related-party transactions (including the flow of information, goods, and funds for different stages) and similarities and difference between them and those for non-related-party transactions;

(4) Intangible assets involved in related-party transactions and the impact of such intangible assets on pricing;

(5) Counterparts of contracts (agreements) entered into in connection with related-party transactions and a statement on their performance;

(6) An analysis of main economic and legal factors which have impact on pricing with respect to related-party transactions;

(7) Information on the attribution of income, cost and expenses, and profits to related-party transactions and non-related-party transactions by a reasonable ratio and a completed Form for the Analysis of the Financial Conditions of Enterprises Related to related-party transactions.

  1. Comparable analysis

(1) Factors taken into account for a comparable analysis, including the characteristics of assets or labor services in transactions, roles played by all parties, risks assumed by all parties, terms and conditions of the contracts involved, business strategies, economic conditions, and other factors;

(2) Roles played, risks assumed, and assets employed by a comparable enterprise and other relevant information;

(3) A statement on comparable transactions, for example, the physical characteristics, quality and usefulness of tangible assets; normal interest rate level, amounts, types of currency, terms, guarantees, the creditworthiness of financiers, ways of repayment, and interest calculation methods in the financing business; the nature and extent of labor services; the types of intangible assets, manners of transactions for intangible assets, rights to employ intangible assets obtained through transactions, and earnings gained from the employment of intangible assets;

(4) Conditions for the selection of comparable information and reasons therefor; and

(5) Difference adjustments to comparable data and reasons therefor.

  1. Selection and employment of transfer pricing methods

(1) Selection and employment of transfer pricing methods and reasons therefore and in selecting the profit method, an enterprise must make a statement on any contribution made by the enterprise toward the total profits or surplus profits of the group;

(2) How comparable information supports the selected transfer pricing method;

(3) Assumptions and estimations made in the course of determining prices or profits for comparable non-related-party transactions;

(4) Prices or profits for comparable non-related-party transactions determined by using reasonable transfer pricing methods and comparable analysis results and a statement on the observation of the arm's length principle; and

(5) Other materials which support the selected transfer pricing method.

Article 15 Any enterprise that falls under any of the following circumstances may be immune from preparing, preserving and submitting same-period documents;

  1. Where the annual amount involved in associated purchases and sales( in the case of processing business, the amount shall be calculated on the basis of customs clearance price for annual import and export) is no more than CNY200,000,000 and the amount involved in other related-party transactions (in the case of associated financing funds , the amount shall be calculated on the basis of the amount of interest collected) is no more than CNY40,000,000 , with the foregoing amounts non including related-party transaction amount involved in the performance of agreements for cost apportion or arrangements for advance pricing by the enterprise within the current year;

  2. Where related-party transactions are involved in the performance of arrangements for advance pricing; or

  3. Where the foreign party owns no more than 50% of the shares and the foreign party enters into related-party transactions with any domestic associated party.

Article 16 Except as otherwise provided in Chapter VII hereof, an enterprise shall, before May 31 of the year following the one in which related-party transactions are accomplished, complete the preparation of same-period documents for the current year and shall provide such documents within 20 days of the date on which a requirement is made by a taxation authority. Any enterprise which is not able to provide same-period documents within the specified time due to force majeure shall provide such documents within 20 days of the end of the force majeure.

Article 17 Same-period documents that an enterprise provides as required by a taxation authority shall be stamped with the seal of the enterprise and signed or stamped by its legal representative or any representative authorized by the legal representative. Sources for any information that is incorporated by reference into same-period documents shall be stated expressly.

Article 18 Where an enterprise changes or cancels its tax registrations due to reasons such as a merger or split-up, the enterprise existing after such a merger or split-up shall preserve same-period documents.

Article 20 Same-period documents shall be written in Chinese. Where the original materials are in a foreign language, a Chinese translation shall be enclosed.

Article 20 Same-period documents shall be preserved for a period of ten years from June 1 of the year following the one in which an related-party transaction is entered into by an enterprise.

Chapter IV Transfer Pricing Methods

Article 21 An enterprise in entering into an related-party transaction and taxation authorities in reviewing and evaluating the related-party transaction shall observe the arm's length principle and select and employ a reasonable transfer pricing method. As provided in Article 111 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law, transfer pricing methods include the comparable uncontrolled price method, the resale price method, the cost plus method, the transactional net margin method, the profit split method, and other approaches that are in compliance with the arm's length principle.

Article 22 A comparable analysis shall be conducted in selecting and employing a reasonable transfer pricing method. A comparable analysis shall mainly include the following five aspects:

  1. The characteristics of assets or labor services involved in a transaction, mainly including the physical characteristics, quality, quantity, and other aspects of tangible assets, the nature and scope of labor services, the types of intangible assets, its modes of trade, term and scope, expected earnings and other aspects;

  2. The capabilities and risks of all parties to the transaction; the capabilities mainly include research and development, design, purchase, processing, assembling, manufacturing, stock management, distribution, after sales service, advertising, transportation, warehousing, financing, financial, accounting, legal and human resources management abilities; in comparing capabilities, attention shall be paid to the extent of similarity between assets employed by the enterprises concerned in playing their respective roles; the risks mainly include research and development risk, purchase risk, production risk, distribution risk, marketing promotion risk, management risk and financial risk and other risks;

  3. The terms and conditions of the contract involved, mainly including the subject matter of the transaction, the quantity and price of goods involved in the transaction, the manners and conditions of payment, the conditions of delivery, the scope and conditions of after sales services, stipulations for the provision of additional labor services, the right to change or modify the contract, the term of the contract, and the right to terminate or renew the contract;

  4. The economic environment, mainly including an overview of the sector involved, geographic location, market size, market hierarchy, market share, the extent of market competition, consumer purchasing power, the alternativity of the goods or labor services involved, prices for elements involved in the production, the cost of transportation, government regulations, and others; and

  5. Business strategies, mainly including innovation and development strategies, multiple-sector business strategies, risk avoidance strategies and market share strategies and other strategies.

Article 23 Under the comparable uncontrolled price method, a price charged between non-associated parties for business activities equal or similar to related-party transactions is regarded as the arm's length price.

A comparable analysis shall pay special attention to differences between related-party transactions and non-related-party transactions in terms of the characteristics of the assets or labor services involved in the transactions, the terms and conditions of the contracts involved and the economic environment and contain specifically the following matters depending on different types of transactions:

  1. Purchase and sale or transfer of tangible assets

(1) Processes for such purchase and sale or transfer, including the time and place of the transaction involved, the conditions of delivery, delivery procedures, the conditions of payment, the quantity of the goods involved in the transaction, the time and place of after sales services, and other matters;

(2) Stages involved in such purchase and sale or transfer, including the ex-factory stage, the wholesaling stage, the retailing stage, the export stage, and other stages;

(3) Goods involved in such purchase and sale or transfer, including the name, brand, specifications, size, function, structure, shape, package and other aspects of the goods; and

(4) Environment for such purchase and sale or transfer, including the local customs involved, consumer preference, the extent of political stability, financial, taxation and foreign exchange policies and other matters.

  1. Employment of tangible assets

(1) The function, specifications, size, structure and type of such assets and a depreciation method for such assets;

(2) The time of, term for and place of access to such assets;

(3) Investment expenditure, maintenance cost and other expenses on such assets paid by the owner of such assets.

  1. Transfer and employment of intangible assets

(1) The type and purpose of such intangible assets, any industry to which such intangible assets is fit, and its expected earnings; and

(2) Conditions for the development of such intangible assets, any investment in it and its transfer, the exclusivity of such intangible assets, the extent to which and term for which it is protected under relevant laws, its transfer cost and expenses, particulars on its functions and risks, its alternativity, and other matters.

  1. Financing: the amount of financing, the type of currency involved, the term of such financing, any guarantee for such financing, the creditworthiness of the financier concerned, the manners of repayment, and interest calculation methods, and other matters.

  2. Provision of labor services: the nature of such services, technical requirements for such services, the professional level of such services, the assumption of responsibilities, the conditions and manners of payment, the direct and indirect cost of such services, and other matters. Where an related-party transaction differs substantially from a comparable non-related-party transaction in the aforesaid aspects, reasonable adjustments shall be made to the effect of such difference on the price involved. Where it is impossible to make reasonable adjustments thereto, another reasonable transfer pricing method shall be selected in accordance with provisions of this Chapter.

The comparable uncontrolled price method may apply to all types of related-party transactions.

Article 24 Under the resale price method, a price at which an associated party resells to any non-associated party goods purchased by the associated party and from which the gross profits of an comparable non-related-party transaction is subtracted shall be regarded as the arm's length price for purchasing the goods. The formula for such arm's length price is set out as follows:

Arm's length price=price at which a resale is made to a non-associated party x (1-the gross profit margin of a comparable non-related-party transaction)

The gross profit margin of a comparable non-related-party transaction=the gross profits of the comparable non-related-party transaction/the net earnings of the comparable non-related-party transaction x 100%

A comparable analysis shall pay special attention to any difference between an related-party transaction and a non-related-party transaction in terms of functions, risks, and the terms and conditions of the contracts involved and to other factors which have impact on the gross profit margin involved, specifically including the functions of sales, advertising and services, inventory risks, the value and useful life of the machinery and equipment involved, the employment and value of the intangible assets involved, the wholesaling or retailing stage, business experience, the efficiency of accounting and management, and other factors.

Where an related-party transaction differs substantially from a comparable non-related-party transaction in the aforesaid aspects, reasonable adjustments shall be made to the effect of such difference on the gross profit margin involved. Where it is impossible to make reasonable adjustments thereto, another reasonable transfer pricing method shall be selected in accordance with provisions of this Chapter.

The resale price method generally applies to simple processing business in which a reseller does not carry out any substantial value-added processing activities such as modifying the shape, function, structure of the goods involved or changing the trademark of the goods or to pure purchase and sale business.

Article 25 Under the cost plus method, the arm's length price shall be reasonable cost incurred for an related-party transaction plus the gross profit of a comparable non-related-party transaction. The formula for such arm's length price is set out as follows:

Arm's length price= reasonable cost for an related-party transaction x (1+ the cost plus rate of a comparable non-related-party transaction)

The cost plus rate of a comparable non-related-party transaction= the gross profits of the comparable non-related-party transaction/the cost of the comparable non-related-party transaction x 100%

A comparable analysis shall pay special attention to any difference between an related-party transaction and a non-related-party transaction in terms of functions, risks, and the terms and conditions of the contracts involved and to other factors which have impact on the cost plus rate involved, specifically including the functions of manufacture, processing, installation, and testing, market and exchange rate risks, the value and useful life of the machinery and equipment involved, the employment and value of the intangible assets involved, the wholesaling or retailing stage, business experience, the efficiency of accounting and management, and other factors.

Where an related-party transaction differs substantially from a comparable independent transaction in the aforesaid aspects, reasonable adjustments shall be made to the effect of such difference on the cost plus rate involved. Where it is impossible to make reasonable adjustments thereto, another reasonable transfer pricing method shall be selected in accordance with provisions of this Chapter.

The cost plus method generally applies to related-party transactions involving the purchase and sale, transfer and employment of tangible assets, the provision of labor services, or financing.

Article 26 Under the transactional net margin method, the net profits of an related-party transaction shall be determined on the basis of the profit margin indicators of a comparable non-related-party transaction. The profit margin indicators of a comparable non-related-party transaction include return on assets, sales margin, total cost plus rate, Berry ratio and other margin indicators.

A comparable analysis shall pay special attention to any difference between an related-party transaction and a non-related-party transaction in terms of functions, risks, and the terms and conditions of the contracts involved and to other factors which have impact on the business profits involved, specifically including executive functions, the assumption of risks, the employment of assets, the conditions of the sector and market involved, the size of business, economic cycle, product life cycle, the apportion of cost, expenses, income and asset among all parties to the transactions, the efficiency of accounting and management, and other factors.

Where an related-party transaction differs substantially from a comparable independent transaction in the aforesaid aspects, reasonable adjustments shall be made to the effect of such difference on the business profits involved. Where it is impossible to make reasonable adjustments thereto, another reasonable transfer pricing method shall be selected in accordance with provisions of this Chapter.

The cost plus method generally applies to related-party transactions involving the purchase and sale, transfer and employment of tangible assets, the transfer and employment of intangible assets and the provision of labor services.

Article 27 Under the profit split method, the amount of profits that shall be distributed respectively to an enterprise and its associated party shall be calculated on the basis of contribution to the consolidated profits of the associate transaction involved made respectively by them. The profit split method consists of the general profit split method and the surplus profit split method.

Under the general profit split method, profits that shall be distributed to all parties to an related-party transaction shall be determined on the basis of roles played, risks assumed and assets employed by all parties.

Under the surplus profit split method, the surplus profits shall be the sum remained after normal profits distributed to all parties to an related-party transaction are subtracted from the consolidated profits and all parties shall distribute the surplus profits on the basis of their respective contribution.

A comparable analysis shall pay special attention to roles played, risks assumed and assets employed by all parties to the transaction in question, the apportion of cost, expenses, income and asset among all parties, accounting, and the reliability of information and assumptions employed to ascertain contribution made by all parties to the surplus profits.

The surplus profit split method generally applies to situations in which related-party transactions are so closely integrated that it is difficult to make separate evaluations on the results of transactions for all parties thereto.

Chapter V Transfer Pricing Investigations and Adjustments

Article 28 Taxation authorities shall have the power to identify, in accordance with provisions of the Tax Administration Law and its Detailed Implementation Rules for tax inspections, enterprises to be investigated in order to make transfer pricing investigations and adjustments. Any enterprise under investigation must genuinely report particulars on its related-party transactions and provide relevant information and shall not refuse to do so or conceal any information.

Article 29 For transfer pricing investigations, an emphasis shall be placed on following enterprises:

  1. Any enterprise involved in associated business transactions involving a large amount of money or in multiple types of associated business transactions;

  2. Any enterprise incurring a long-term loss, making a small profit for a long time or with an unstable profit-making pattern;

  3. Any enterprise with the level of profits below the average level of profits made by other enterprises in the same sector;

  4. Any enterprise that fulfils such functions and assumes such risks as are explicitly inconsistent with the level of its profits;

  5. Any enterprise that enters into business transactions with any associated party of a tax haven;

  6. Any enterprise that fails to make associated filings or prepare same-period documents in accordance with relevant provisions;

  7. Any other enterprise that conspicuously violates the arm's length principle.

Article 30 For transactions between domestic associated parties that actually bear identical tax burden, as long as such transactions do not directly or indirectly result in any decline in the total tax revenue of the State, no transfer pricing investigation and adjustment shall be made in principle.

Article 31 Taxation authorities shall, in light of day-to-day tax administration work, carry out desk audit activities to identify enterprises to be investigated. In desk audit activities, a comprehensive evaluation and analysis of the production and operation conditions of an enterprise under investigation, its related-party transactions and other matters shall be conducted on the basis of annual income tax filings made and statements on associated business transactions submitted by the enterprise for previous years and other tax payment documents. Enterprises may, in the course of desk audit activities, provide same-period documents to taxation authorities.

Article 32 For an identified object of investigation, taxation authorities shall, in accordance with Chapter 6 of the Income Tax Law and Chapter 6 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law, Chapter 4 of the Tax Administration Law, Chapter 6 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law, carry out on-site investigations.

  1. Two or more on-site investigators must be present;

  2. In conducting on-site investigations, investigators shall produce a Paper of Tax Investigations and serve a Notice of Transfer Pricing Investigations;

  3. Inquiries, transfer of account books and relevant documents for inspection purposes and on-the-spot checks and other means may be adopted in accordance with statutory procedures for on-site investigations in light of actual needs;

  4. Where any party is questioned, a designated person shall prepare a Written Record of Inquiries (Investigations) and any such party shall be informed of the legal liability to be assumed by any such party in the case of dishonesty in answering questions. The Written Record of Inquiries (Investigations) shall be taken to any such party for check and confirmation;

  5. Where it is necessary to transfer account books and relevant documents for inspection purposes, a Notice of Transferring Account Books and Relevant Documents, a List of Account Books and Relevant Documents to Be Transferred, and relevant statutory formalities shall be completed in accordance with the provisions of Article 86 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law so as to transfer the account books and relevant documents, and the account books and relevant documents shall preserved in a proper manner and be returned in full within a statutory time limit;

  6. Investigators shall complete a Written Record of Inquiries (Investigations) for any issues and problems discovered in the course of on-the-spot checks. The Written Record of Inquiries (Investigations) shall be signed by two or more investigators and checked and confirmed by the enterprise under investigation depending on actual needs. Where the enterprise under investigation refuses to do so, two or more investigators may make appropriate notes on the Written Record of Inquiries (Investigations) for filing purposes;

  7. Information related to the case in question may be obtained by way of taking a record, making sound recordings, making video recordings, taking photographs, and reproducing, provided that the preserver and source of the original must be specified; the preserver or provider of the original shall make verification and write the words "a certified true copy" thereon and affix a seal or fingerprint thereon; and

  8. Where it is necessary to call on any witness to testify, any such witness shall be informed in advance of the legal liability to be assumed in the case of dishonesty in giving a testimony. The testimony of a witness shall be signed and fingerprinted by the witness.

Article 33 As provided in Paragraph 2 of Article 43 of the Income Tax Law and Article 114 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law, a taxation authority shall, in conducting transfer pricing investigations, have the power to require an enterprise, its associated parties and any other enterprise related to the related-party transaction investigations (hereinafter referred to as the "comparable enterprise") to provide relevant information and serve upon them a Notice of Taxation Matters.

  1. The enterprise shall provide the relevant information within the period specified in the Notice of Taxation Matters and, in the case of a failure to do so due to any special reason, shall submit to the taxation authority a written request of extension and may, after the approval of the taxation authority, delay in providing such information for a maximum period of up to thirty days. The taxation authority shall give a reply within fifteen days upon receiving the request of extension submitted by the enterprise and a reply given beyond the foregoing time or a failure to give a rely shall be deemed that the taxation authority has approved such request; and

  2. The enterprise's associated parties and the comparable enterprise shall provide the relevant information within a period of generally up to sixty days agreed upon with the taxation authority.

The enterprise, the associated parties and the comparable enterprise shall, as required by the taxation authority, provide genuine and complete relevant information.

Article 34 Taxation authorities shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of Chapter II hereof, verify the filing information of enterprises and require the enterprises to complete a Form for the Analysis of Comparable Factors for Enterprises.

Taxation authorities shall, on the basis of associated filings made and information provided by enterprises, fill out a Form for the Recognition of Associated Relationships, a Form for the Recognition of related-party transactions, and a Form for the Analysis of Comparable Factors for Enterprises, and cause them to be checked and confirmed by the enterprises under investigation.

Article 35 Where transfer pricing investigations involve gathering evidence from an associated party and a comparable enterprise, a taxation authority shall serve upon the relevant enterprise a Notice of Taxation Inspections before gathering the evidence.

Article 36 A taxation authority may adopt methods such as conducting on-the-spot investigations, issuing letters for assistance in investigations, and making inquiries into publicly available information to verify relevant information provided by an enterprise, its associated parties and a comparable enterprise. Where it is necessary to obtain relevant information from abroad, a taxation authority may gather the relevant information by going through information exchange procedures specified in a tax treaty in accordance with relevant provisions or through institutions of China stationed abroad. Where relevant information from overseas associated parties is involved, a taxation authority may require the relevant enterprise to provide the certification of a notary public office.

Article 37 Taxation authorities shall select and employ any of the transfer pricing methods specified in Chapter IV hereof to make an analysis and evaluation on whether or not related-party transactions of an enterprise comply with the arm's length principle. An analysis and evaluation may use publicly available information or non-publicly available information.

Article 38 In analyzing and evaluating related-party transactions of an enterprise, taxation authorities in principle shall not make adjustments to any difference in business profits arising as a result of discrepancy in the use of operating capital by the enterprise and a comparable enterprise. Where it is really necessary to make adjustments, the matter shall be reported to the State Administration of Taxation for approval.

Article 39 Any enterprise that carries out processing and manufacturing activities pursuant to the order form of its associated party and does not perform the functions of decision-making, the research and development of products, the sales of products and others shall not be responsible for any risk and loss incurred as a result of any error in decision-making, under capacity operation, unmarketable products, or any other reason and shall generally maintain a certain level of profit margin. For any enterprise that incurs a loss, taxation authorities shall, on the basis of an economic analysis, select an appropriate comparable price or enterprise to determine the level of profit margin for any such enterprise.

Article 40 Where a transaction involving the collection of a price between associated parties is offset by another transaction involving the payment of another price between them, a taxation authority shall, in conducting a comparable analysis and making tax adjustments, shall restore both transactions.

Article 41 Where an enterprise's profit level is below the median of the profit range of a comparable enterprise, in analyzing and evaluating the profit level of the enterprise by way of the quartile method, a taxation authority shall in principle make adjustments as if the enterprise's profit level is not below such median.

Article 42 Where an related-party transaction entered into by an enterprise complies with the arm's length principle upon investigations, a taxation authority shall reach a conclusion for transfer pricing investigations and serve upon the enterprise a Notice of the Conclusion of Special Tax Investigations.

Article 43 Where, upon investigations, an related-party transaction entered into by an enterprise fails to comply with the arm's length principle, thereby leading to a reduction in its taxable income or earnings, a taxation authority shall make adjustments to transfer pricing in accordance with the following procedures:

  1. The taxation authority shall draw up a proposal for preliminary adjustments to special tax investigations on the basis of an estimation, discussion and comparable analysis;

  2. The taxation authority shall, pursuant to the proposal for preliminary adjustments, conduct negotiations with the enterprise; both the taxation authority and the enterprise shall designate a principal negotiator and investigators shall prepare a Record of Particulars on Negotiations and cause such a Record to be signed and confirmed by both principal negotiators; where the enterprise refuses to do so, two or more investigators may make appropriate notes thereon for filing purposes.

  3. Where the enterprise raises objections to the proposal for preliminary adjustments, the enterprise shall provide relevant information within a period specified by the taxation authority. The taxation authority shall, after receiving such information, examine it and make a review decision in a timely manner;

  4. The taxation authority shall serve on the enterprise a Notice of Preliminary Adjustments to Special Tax Investigations in accordance with the review decision. Where the enterprise raises objections to the preliminary adjustments, the enterprise shall do so within seven days of receipt of such a Notice. The taxation authority shall arrange for additional negotiations and review after receiving the objections of the enterprise. A failure by the enterprise to raise any objection within the specified period shall be deemed as consent to the preliminary adjustments; and

  5. The taxation authority shall decide on a proposal for final adjustments and serve upon the enterprise a Notice of Adjustments to Special Tax Investigations.

Article 44 After receiving a Notice of Adjustments to Special Tax Investigations, an enterprise shall pay its tax and interest thereon within the specified period.

Article 45 Where a taxation authority makes adjustments to transfer pricing with respect to an enterprise, the taxation authority shall conduct follow-up administration activities within five years from the year following the one in which such adjustments are made. The enterprise shall, before June 20 of the year following the one in which the follow-up administration is conducted, provide the taxation authority with same-period documents for the year in which the follow-up administration is conducted. The taxation authority shall focus on analyzing and evaluating the following matters on the basis of the same-period documents and tax filing information:

  1. The investments and business conditions of the enterprise and changes therein;

  2. Changes in the amount of tax reported by the enterprise;

  3. Changes in the business results of the enterprise; and

  4. Changes in related-party transactions and other matters.

For any unusual issue with respect to the enterprise's transfer pricing discovered within the follow-up administration period, the taxation authority shall communicate with the enterprise in a timely manner to require the enterprise to make its own adjustments, or make adjustments to transfer pricing investigations in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Chapter.

Chapter VI Arrangements for Advance Pricing

Article 46 An enterprise may, in accordance with Article 42 of the Income Tax Law, Article 113 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law and Article 53 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law, enter into with a taxation authority an arrangement for advance pricing with respect to the pricing principle and calculation method for related-party transactions in the years to come. To conduct negotiations for, enter into and carry out an arrangement for advance pricing, the stages of preparatory discussions, formal application, review and evaluation, negotiations, conclusion of the arrangement, and monitoring and control on the implementation of the arrangement shall be gone through. Arrangements for advance pricing include unilateral, bilateral and multi-lateral arrangements.

Article 47 Arrangements for advance pricing shall be handled by taxation authorities at the level of cities divided into districts and autonomous prefecture or above.

Article 48 An arrangement for advance pricing generally applies to any enterprise which meets all of the following requirements:

  1. Amount involved in related-party transactions entered into annually by the enterprise are CNY40,000,000 or more;

  2. The enterprise performs the obligation of making associated filings in accordance with the law; and

  3. The enterprise prepares, preserves, and provides same-period documents in accordance with the relevant provisions.

Article 49 An arrangement for advance pricing applies to related-party transactions entered into within three to five consecutive years of the year following the one in which an enterprise files a formal written application.

The conclusion of an arrangement for advance pricing does not affect transfer pricing investigation adjustments made by a taxation authority for related-party transactions in the year in which the enterprise files a formal written application for the arrangement for advance pricing or in years previous to such year.

Where related-party transactions in the year in which an application is filed or in years previous to such year are identical with or similar to those in years to which the arrangement for advance pricing is applicable, pricing principles and calculation methods specified in the arrangement for advance pricing may, after the approval by the taxation authority of an application filed by the enterprise for such purpose, be applied to evaluations on and the adjustment of those related-party transactions in the year in which an application is filed or in years previous to such year.

Article 50 An enterprise shall, before formally applying for an arrangement for advance pricing, propose to a taxation authority in writing the intent to conduct negotiations for and enter into such an arrangement for advance pricing. The taxation authority may, on the basis of the written request of the enterprise, carry out preparatory discussions with the enterprise on the relevant contents of the arrangement for advance pricing and the feasibility of the conclusion of the arrangement for advance pricing and complete a Record of Discussions over Arrangements for Advance Pricing. Participants may anonymously take part in preparatory discussions.

  1. Any enterprise which applies for a unilateral arrangement for advance pricing shall propose to a taxation authority the intent to conduct negotiations for and enter into such an arrangement for advance pricing. During the preparatory discussions, the enterprise shall provide information about the following matters and discuss the matters with the taxation authority:

(1) Years to which the arrangement for advance pricing applies;

(2) Associated parties and related-party transactions involved in such arrangement;

(3) The production and operation conditions of the enterprise in previous years;

(4) A statement on the functions and risks of all associated parties involved in such arrangement;

(5) Whether or not to apply methods specified in such arrangement to address transfer pricing issues in previous years; and

(6) Other circumstances which it is necessary to explain.

  1. Any enterprise which applies for a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing shall propose to both the state administration of taxation and the competent taxation authority conduct negotiations for and enter into such an arrangement for advance pricing. The State Administration of Taxation shall make arrangements for preparatory discussions with the enterprise. In addition to Item 1 of this Article, the discussions shall in particular include the following matters:

(1) Particulars on any application for preparatory discussions submitted to the competent taxation authority of the other party to the tax treaty in question;

(2) The production and operation conditions and related-party transactions in previous years of associated parties involved in such arrangement; and

(3) Pricing principles and calculation methods proposed to be adopted in an arrangement for advance pricing presented to the competent taxation authority of the other party to the tax treaty.

  1. Where an agreement is reached in the preparatory discussions, the taxation authority shall, within fifteen days of the date on which the agreement is reached, notify the enterprise to conduct formal negotiations with respect to matters relating to the arrangement for advance pricing and serve upon the enterprise a Notice of Formal Discussions over Arrangements for Advance Pricing. Where no agreement is reached in preparatory discussions, the taxation authority shall, within fifteen days of the date on which the final preparatory discussion session concludes, notify the enterprise, serve upon the enterprise a Notice of Rejecting the Application by Enterprises for Arrangements for Advance Pricing, rejects the application by the enterprise for the arrangement for advance pricing and state any reasons therefor.

Article 51 An enterprise shall present to a taxation authority a written application for an arrangement for advance pricing within three months of the date on which a notice of formal discussions is received and submit a Formal Application for Arrangements for Advance Pricing. Any enterprise which applies for a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing shall submit to both the State Administration of Taxation and the competent taxation authority a Formal Application for Arrangements for Advance Pricing and an Application for the Commencement of Mutual Negotiation Procedures.

  1. A written application for an arrangement for advance pricing filed by an enterprise shall include the following matters:

(1) The organizational structure of any group related to the enterprise, the internal structure of any company related to the enterprise, relevant associated relationships and relevant related-party transactions;

(2) The financial and accounting statements and documents of the enterprise for the previous three years and information on the functions of its products and its assets (including intangible assets and tangible assets);

(3) The types of related-party transactions and taxable years involved in the arrangement for advance pricing;

(4) The allocation of functions and risks between associated parties, including institutions, personnel, expenses, assets etc. on which such allocation is based;

(5) Transfer pricing principles and calculation methods applicable to the arrangement for advance pricing and a function and risk analysis, a comparable analysis and assumptions etc. in support of such principles and methods;

(6) A statement on market conditions, including the development trend of the sector in question and the environment of competitions;

(7) A forecast on the annual business size and business performance of the enterprise for the period during which the arrangement for advance pricing is in force and its business plans;

(8) Information on related-party transactions, business arrangements, and profit level related to the arrangement;

(9) Whether or not issues such as double taxation are involved; and

(10) Relevant issues involving relevant domestic and foreign laws, tax treaties and other matters.

  1. Where an enterprise can not file a written application within the specified period due to any of the following special reasons, the enterprise may file an application for an extension of the specified period to a taxation authority and submit an Application for the Deferred Submission of Formal Application for Arrangements for Advance Pricing:

(1) It is necessary to prepare in particular information on certain areas;

(2) It is necessary to process any information technically, for example, translating any such information, etc; or

(3) Any other external reasons.

The taxation authority shall give a written reply with respect to the extension matter within fifteen days of receipt of the written application for an extension of the specified period and serve upon on the enterprise a Reply to Application for the Deferred Submission of Formal Application for Arrangements for Advance Pricing. A failure to give a reply within the specified period shall be deemed as consent by the taxation authority to the extension application of the enterprise.

  1. The enterprise and the taxation authority shall preserve in a proper manner documents, information, and statements involved in the foregoing application, including all documents and information that can support pricing principles and calculation methods proposed to be selected and employed or are proved to meet the requirements of the arrangement for advance pricing.

Article 52 A taxation authority shall, within five months of the date on which the formal written application for an arrangement for advance pricing and necessary documents and information submitted by an enterprise are received, make a review and evaluation. The taxation authority may, depending on the specific circumstances of the review and evaluation, require the enterprise to provide additional relevant information so as to reach a review and evaluation conclusion.

Where it is necessary to extend the review and evaluation period due to any special reason, the taxation authority shall notify in writing the enterprise in a timely manner and an extension so made shall not exceed three months.

A taxation authority shall mainly review and evaluate the following matters:

  1. Previous business conditions: making an analysis and evaluation of documents and information such as the business plans of the enterprise, its development trend, and business scope, with an emphasis on reviewing feasibility study reports, investment budgets (final accounts), resolutions of the board of directors, and other matters; making a comprehensive analysis of relevant information and documents reflecting the business results of the enterprise such as financial or accounting statements and audit reports;

  2. Function and risk conditions: making an analysis and evaluation of shares owned respectively and functions fulfilled by the enterprise and its associated parties in stages such as supply, manufacture, transportation, and sales and in areas such as the research and development of intangible assets and of risks assumed by them in inventory, credit, foreign exchange, market and other areas;

  3. Comparable information: making an analysis and evaluation of domestic and foreign comparable price information provide by the enterprise, giving a statement on any material difference between a comparable enterprise and the applicant enterprise, and making appropriate adjustments; in the case of inability to confirm the reasonableness of a comparable transaction or business activity, the taxation authority shall require expressly the enterprise to provide additional relevant information and document so as to prove that transfer pricing principles and calculation methods selected and employed by the enterprise reflect equitably related-party transactions under review and its current business conditions and are validated by relevant financial and business information;

  4. Assumptions: making an analysis and evaluation of factors affecting the profitability of the sector in question and the production and operation of the enterprise and of the extent of such effect and defining in a reasonable manner assumptions applicable to the arrangement for advance pricing;

  5. Transfer pricing principles and calculation methods: making an analysis and evaluation of whether or not transfer pricing principles and calculation methods selected and employed by the enterprise in the arrangement for advance pricing are applicable to related-party transactions and relevant financial and business information for the previous years, the current year and the years to come, how to actually so apply them, and whether or not they comply with provisions of laws and administrative regulations; and

  6. Expected arm's length price or profit range: arriving at a price or profit range acceptable to both the taxation authority and the enterprise by making a further analysis and evaluation of a comparable price, profit margins and a comparable transaction that have been fixed and other circumstances.

Article 53 A taxation authority shall, within thirty days of the date on which a review and evaluation conclusion for an unilateral arrangement for advance pricing is reached, conduct negotiations with the enterprise concerned with respect to the arrangement for advance pricing. Where an agreement is reached through such negotiations, the taxation authority shall report level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for examination and approval a preliminary version of the arrangement for advance pricing together with a review and evaluation report.

For a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing, the State Administration of Taxation shall conduct negotiations with the competent taxation authority of the other party to the tax treaty in question. Where an agreement is reached through such negotiations, a preliminary version of the arrangement for advance pricing shall be drawn up on the basis of the memorandum of negotiations in question.

A preliminary version of the arrangement for advance pricing shall include the following matters:

  1. Basic information on associated parties such as their names and addresses;

  2. related-party transactions involved in such arrangement and years in which such arrangement applies;

  3. The comparable price or transaction, transfer pricing principles and calculation methods, and expected business results, among others, which have been selected in such arrangement;

  4. The definitions of terms related to the application of transfer pricing methods and the calculation basis;

  5. Assumptions

  6. Notification of any changes in the annual reports of the enterprise, records it preserves, and its assumptions and other obligations;

  7. The legal force of such arrangement and the confidentiality of information and documents;

  8. Mutual responsibility clause

  9. Modification of such arrangement

  10. Methods and channels of dispute resolution

  11. Effective date; and

  12. Supplementary provisions.

Article 54 After a taxation authority and an enterprise reach an agreement on the contents of an preliminary version of an unilateral arrangement for advance pricing, the legal representatives of both parties or any representative authorized by such legal representatives shall formally enter into the unilateral arrangement for advance pricing. After the State Administration of Taxation reach an agreement on the contents of an preliminary version of a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing with the competent taxation authority of the other party to the tax treaty in question, representatives authorized by the competent taxation authorities of both parties or all parties shall formally enter into the bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing. The competent taxation authority shall enter into an implementation agreement with an enterprise on the basis of such bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing.

Article 55 After the commencement of formal negotiations with respect to the arrangement for advance pricing and before the conclusion of the arrangement for advance pricing, a taxation authority and an enterprise may discontinue or terminate such negotiations. Where a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing is involved, such negotiations may be discontinued or terminated only after consultations between the competent taxation authorities of all parties to the tax treaty in question. Where negotiations are terminated, both parties shall return to each other all documents provided to each other in the course of negotiations.

Article 56 Taxation authorities shall establish a management system to monitor and control the implementation of arrangements for advance pricing.

  1. During the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, an enterprise shall preserve in their entirety documents and information related to such an arrangement (including account books, relevant records, etc.) and shall not lose, destroy or transfer such documents and information; the enterprise shall, within five months after the close of a taxable year, submit to the taxation authority concerned an annual report on the implementation of the arrangement for advance pricing. Such a report shall state the business conditions of the enterprise during the reporting period and the compliance of the enterprise with the provisions of the arrangement for advance pricing, including all matters required by such arrangement and whether or not there are any requirements as to the modification or cancellation of such arrangement. In the case of any pending issue or any issue expected to arise, the enterprise shall make a statement thereof in the annual report so as to carry out consultations with the taxation authority as to whether or not to modify or terminate such arrangement.

  2. During the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, the taxation authority shall conduct inspections on the implementation of such an arrangement by the enterprise on a periodic basis (generally half a year). Matters included in such inspections are mainly as follows: whether or not the enterprise complies with the provisions and requirements of such an arrangement; whether or not information and annual reports provided to conduct negotiations for and enter into such an arrangement reflect the actual business conditions of the enterprise; whether information and calculation methods on which transfer pricing methods are based are correct; whether or not assumptions described in such an arrangement continue to be valid; whether or not the application of transfer pricing methods by the enterprise is consistent with the assumptions; and other matters.

Where a common violation by the enterprise of the provisions of such arrangement is discovered, the taxation authority may at its discretion deal with it by imposing a penalty up to the cancellation of such arrangement. Where it is discovered that the enterprise conceals any information or refuses to implement such arrangement, the taxation authority shall hold such arrangement to be invalid ab initio.

  1. Where, during the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, the actual business results of the enterprise fail to fall within the price or profit range expected in such arrangement and such a case does not violate the provisions and requirements of such arrangement, the taxation authority shall, after making a report to the taxation authority at the next higher level for approval, adjust the actual business results to fall within the price or profit range defined by such arrangement. Where a bilateral or multi-lateral arrangement for advance pricing is involved, the taxation authority shall make a report level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for approval.

  2. Where, during the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, any material changes occur that affect such arrangement (e.g. any changes in assumptions), the enterprise shall, within thirty days after the occurrence of any such changes, submit a written report to the taxation authority in which to make a detailed statement on the effect of any such changes on the implementation of such arrangement and attach thereto relevant information. Such a report may be deferred due to any external reasons, provided the period of such deferment shall not exceed thirty days.

The taxation authority shall, within sixty days of receipt of the written report submitted by the enterprise, review and deal with any such changes, including reviewing particulars on any such changes, conducting consultations with the enterprise as to the modification of clauses and relevant conditions contained in such arrangement or taking measures such as the modification or termination of such arrangement on the basis of the extent of the effect of any such changes on the implementation of such arrangement. Where the implementation of such arrangement is terminated, the taxation authority may, in accordance with procedures and requirements of this Chapter, carry out negotiations with the enterprise with respect to a new arrangement for advance pricing.

  1. During the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing entered into with the enterprise jointly by a state taxation bureau and a local taxation bureau is implemented, the enterprise shall submit an annual report on the implementation of such arrangement and a report on material changes respectively to the state taxation bureau and the local taxation bureau. The state taxation bureau and the local taxation bureau shall carry out joint inspections and reviews on the implementation of such arrangement by the enterprise.

Article 57 An arrangement for advance pricing shall automatically become ineffective upon the expiration of its term. Any enterprise which needs to renew such arrangement shall, ninety days before the expiration of the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, file a renewal application to a taxation authority, submit an Application for the Renewal of Arrangements for Advance Pricing and provide reliable proof to prove that no material change occur in facts and relevant conditions described in the current arrangement for advance pricing and that all provisions and stipulations contained in such arrangement have been complied with. The taxation authority shall, within fifteen days of receipt of the renewal application, give a written reply as to whether or not to accept the renewal application and serve upon the enterprise a Rely to Application for the Renewal of Arrangements for Advance Pricing. The taxation authority shall complete a review and evaluation of the renewal application materials, draw up a preliminary version of a new arrangement for advance pricing after consultations with the enterprise, and renew such arrangement with the enterprise at the time and place agreed upon by both parities.

Article 58 The State Administration of Taxation shall be solely responsible for making arrangements for and carrying out coordinative work with respect to the negotiations, conclusion or implementation of arrangements for advance pricing that involve the taxation authorities of two or more provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government and cities specifically designated in the state plan. An enterprise may propose directly to the State Administration of Taxation the intention to conduct negotiations for and enter into an arrangement for advance pricing.

Article 59 The state taxation bureaus and local taxation bureaus of all regions shall recognize an arrangement for advance pricing entered into by and between a taxation authority and an enterprise as long as the enterprise complies with all provisions and requirements contained in such an arrangements.

Article 60 A taxation authority and an enterprise shall both have the obligation to keep confidential all information and documents obtained or acquired in the entire course of preparatory discussions, formal negotiations and conclusion, reviews, analyses, among others, with respect to an arrangement for advance pricing. For each discussion session between the taxation authority and the enterprise, both parties shall make a written record of matters that are discussed in which to set out the number and contents of documents provided to each other in such session and such a record shall be signed or stamped by the principal negotiators of both parties.

Article 61 Where a taxation authority and an enterprise fail to enter into an arrangement for advance pricing, the taxation authority shall not thereafter apply non-factual information obtained or acquired in the course of discussions and negotiations to tax investigations into related-party transactions involved in such arrangement.

Article 62 In the case of any conflict between a taxation authority and an enterprise during the period in which an arrangement for advance pricing is implemented, both parties shall carry out negotiations; Where any such conflict fails to be resolved through negotiations, it shall be reported to the taxation authority at the next higher level for mediation; where a bilateral or multilateral arrangement for advance pricing is involved, any such conflict must be reported level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for mediation. The taxation authority shall implement a mediation result or decision issued by the taxation authority at the next higher level or the State Administration of Taxation. However, where the enterprise continues to be dissatisfied, the taxation authority shall terminate the implementation of such arrangement.

Article 63 A taxation authority shall, within ten days after the formal conclusion of a unilateral, bilateral or multilateral arrangement for advance pricing with an enterprise or within twenty days of the occurrence of the modification or termination of such arrangement or other circumstances in the course of its implementation, report level by level the formal text of such a unilateral arrangement, an implementation agreement for such a bilateral or multilateral arrangement, and a statement on any changes in such arrangement to the State Administration of Taxation for record.

Chapter VII Agreements for Cost Apportion

Article 64 Where an enterprise enters into an agreement for cost apportion with its associated party in accordance with Paragraph 2 of Article 41 of the Income Tax Law and Article 112 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law in order to jointly develop or own intangible assets or jointly provide or receive labor services, the provisions of this Chapter shall be complied with.

Article 65 All parties to agreement for cost apportion shall be entitled to beneficiary rights to intangible assets developed by them or transferred to them or labor services participated in by them and be responsible for appropriate activity cost. Such cost shall be equal to that a non-associated enterprise pays in order to obtain the foregoing beneficial rights under comparable conditions.

For intangible assets developed or received by applying an agreement for cost apportion, it is not necessary for any parties thereto to pay royalties for employing such intangible assets.

Article 66 An enterprise shall have a reasonable and measurable expected earnings with respect to the beneficiary rights in intangible assets or labor services involved in an agreement for cost apportion and base such earnings on reasonable business assumptions and normal business conditions.

Article 67 Agreements for cost apportion involving labor services are generally applicable to group purchases and group marketing plans.

Article 68 An agreement for cost apportion shall mainly include the following matters:

  1. The names of all parties thereto, countries in which such parties reside, associated relationships among such parties, the rights and obligations of such parties in the agreement;

  2. The particulars and scope of intangible assets or labor services involved in the agreement and any party specifically responsible for research and development activities or labor services involved in the agreement and the duties and task of any such party;

  3. The term of the agreement;

  4. A calculation method and assumptions for expected earnings to be obtained by all parties thereto;

  5. The amounts of initial investments and subsequent cost payments made by all parties thereto, manners in which such investments and payments are made, a value validation method for such investments and payments, and a statement on the observance of the arm's length principle;

  6. The application of accounting methods by all parties thereto and a statement on any changes in such methods;

  7. Procedures for accession into or withdrawal from the agreement by all parties thereto and provisions for the treatment of such accession or withdrawal;

  8. Conditions for any indemnification payment among all parties thereto and provisions for the treatment of such payment;

  9. Conditions for the modification or termination of the agreement and provisions for the treatment of such modification or termination; and

  10. Provisions for the employment of achievements generated as a result the agreement by an outside party.

Article 69 An enterprise shall, within thirty days of the conclusion of an agreement for cost apportion, report such an agreement level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for record. A determination by a taxation authority on whether or not an agreement for cost apportion complies with the arm's length principle must be reported level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for review.

Article 70 For an agreement for cost apportion the performance of which has commenced and from which certain assets have been created, if any change in the parties thereto occurs or such performance is terminated, the matter shall on the basis of the arm's length principle be dealt with as follows:

  1. Accession payment, namely, reasonable accession payment made by a new party acceded into the agreement for the acquisition of beneficiary rights to any existing achievements generated as a result of the agreement;

  2. Withdrawal indemnification, namely, reasonable indemnification obtained by any party thereto who withdraws from the agreement for transferring beneficiary rights to any existing achievements generated as a result of the agreement to any other parities thereto;

  3. After any change in the parties thereto, corresponding adjustments shall be made to benefits obtained and portion of cost assumed by all parties thereto; and

  4. Upon the termination of the agreement, all parties thereto shall make a reasonable distribution of any existing achievements generated as a result of the agreement.

Where an enterprise fails to deal with the foregoing circumstances on the basis of the arm's length principle, thereby leading to a reduction in its taxable income, a taxation authority shall have the power to make appropriate adjustments.

Article 71 Where, during the period in which an agreement for cost apportion is performed, the portion of cost assumed by any party thereto is inconsistent with benefits obtained by any such party, indemnification adjustments shall be made in light of actual circumstances.

Article 72 For any agreement for cost apportion that complies with the arm's length principle, relevant taxation treatments are set out as follows:

  1. Any portion of cost assumed by an enterprise in accordance with any such agreement may be deducted before the assessment of tax for years specified in any such agreement;

  2. Where any such agreement involves any indemnification adjustments, taxable income shall be included in the year in which any such indemnification adjustments are made;

  3. Where any such agreement involves intangible assets and achievements generated as a result of any such agreement are distributed when any accession payment or withdrawal indemnification is made or any such agreement is terminated, relevant matters shall be dealt with in accordance with relevant provisions for the purchase or disposal of assets.

Article 73 An enterprise may, in accordance with the provisions of Chapter VI hereof, enter into an agreement for cost apportion by way of an arrangement for advance pricing.

Article 74 During the period in which an agreement for cost apportion is performed, an enterprise shall prepare and preserve the following same-period documents in addition to complying with the provisions of Chapter III hereof:

  1. A counterpart of the agreement for cost apportion;

  2. Any other agreement entered into by all parties thereto for the performance of such agreement for cost apportion;

  3. The employment of any achievements generated as a result such agreement by any outside party and the amount of payment made by such party;

  4. Information on any accession into or withdrawal from such agreement for cost apportion, including the names of any party who has been acceded into or has withdrawn from such agreement, country in which any such party resides, any associated relationship involved, the amount of any accession payment or withdrawal indemnification, and manners in which such payment or indemnification is made;

  5. Information on any modification to or the termination of such agreement, including reasons for any such modification or such termination, the disposal or distribution of any existing achievements generated as a result such agreement;

  6. The total amount of cost incurred in connection with such agreement for cost apportion for the current year and the composition of such cost;

  7. Information on the portions of cost assumed by all parties thereto, including the amount of cost payments, manners in which such payments are made, parties to which such payments are made, the amount of indemnification payments made or received, manners in which such indemnification payments are made, and parties to which such indemnification payments are made; and

  8. A comparison between expected earnings derived from such agreement for the current year and actual results and adjustments made accordingly.

During the period in which an agreement for cost apportion is performed, regardless of whether or not the agreement for cost apportion is concluded by way of an arrangement for advance pricing, an enterprise shall, by June 20 of the following year, provide to a taxation authority same-period documents with respect to the agreement for cost apportion.

Article 75 Where an agreement for cost apportion entered into by an enterprise with its associated party falls under any of the following circumstances, the portion of cost assumed by the enterprise shall not be deducted before tax:

  1. Where the agreement for cost apportion is for unreasonable commercial purposes and without economic substance;

  2. Where the arm's length principle is not complied with;

  3. Where the portion of cost assumed by the enterprise is inconsistent with benefits received by the enterprise;

  4. Where the enterprise fails to put on records or prepare, preserve and provide same-period documents with respect to the agreement for cost apportion in accordance with relevant provisions hereof; or

  5. Where the enterprise has an operation period of no more than twenty years as of the date on which agreement for cost apportion is signed.

Chapter VIII Administration of Controlled Foreign Enterprises

Article 76 The term "controlled foreign enterprise" as used herein means any foreign enterprise established in a country (region) with an actual tax burden below the tax rate of 50% specified in Paragraph 1 of Article 4 of the Income Tax Law which is controlled by a resident enterprise or both a resident enterprise and an individual resident (hereinafter referred to as "Chinese resident shareholders", including Chinese resident enterprise shareholders and Chinese individual resident shareholders) in accordance with the provisions of Article 45 of the Income Tax Law and which does not distribute its profits or reduces the distribution of the profits for unreasonable business requirements.

Article 77 The term "control" as used in article 76 hereof refers to actual control which is formed in areas such as shares, funds, operation, purchase and sales. The term "control over shares" means a Chinese resident shareholder holds 10% or more of the voting shares of a foreign enterprise on any day during a taxable year by way of direct single-class shareholding or indirect multi-class shareholding and more than 50% of the foreign enterprise's shares are held by all Chinese resident shareholders.

For a Chinese resident shareholder that controls a controlled foreign enterprise by indirect multi-class shareholding, the percentage of shares held by such a shareholder in such an enterprise shall be calculated by multiplying shareholding percentages for each class of the shares of such an enterprise, and if a middle-level shareholder holds more than 50% of a class of shares, such a shareholder shall be deemed as holding 100% of such a class of shares.

Article 78 A Chinese resident enterprise shareholder shall, in make a tax return for its annual enterprise income tax, provide information on its external investments and attach thereto a Statement on External Investments.

Article 79 A taxation authority shall summarize, examine and verify information on external investments reported by Chinese resident enterprise shareholders, serve a Notice of Confirmation for Chinese Resident Shareholders of Controlled Foreign Enterprises upon any Chinese resident enterprise shareholder of a controlled foreign enterprise. Tax shall be levied in accordance with relevant provisions on any Chinese resident enterprise shareholder who is subject to the requirements for tax imposition specified in Article 45 of the Income Tax Law.

Article 80 Income included in the income of a Chinese resident enterprise shareholder for the current period that is deemed as dividend equivalents distributed by a controlled foreign enterprise shall be calculated according to the following formula:

Income of a Chinese resident enterprises shareholder for the current period=income deemed as dividend equivalents x the number of actual shareholding days/the number of days in a taxable year for a controlled foreign enterprise x the shareholding percentage of the shareholder

Where a Chinese resident shareholder holds the shares of a controlled foreign enterprise by indirect multi-class shareholding, the percentage of shares held by such a shareholder shall be calculated by multiplying shareholding percentages for each class of the shares.

Article 81Where a controlled foreign enterprise differs from a Chinese resident enterprise shareholder in taxable years, income that is deemed as dividend equivalents shall be included in a taxable year within which the Chinese resident enterprise shareholder is on the date when the taxable year for the controlled foreign enterprise is closed.

Article 82 Tax credits may be used in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Income Tax Law or tax treaties against enterprise income tax paid abroad for income included in the income of a Chinese resident enterprise shareholder for the current period.

Article 83 Where profits actually distributed by a controlled foreign enterprise to a Chinese resident enterprise shareholder have been assessed for tax in accordance with Article 45 of the Income Tax Law, such profits shall not be included in the income of the Chinese resident enterprise shareholder for the current period.

Article 84 Any Chinese resident enterprise shareholder that is able to provide evidence that a controlled foreign enterprise under the control of any such shareholder meet any of the following criteria may be immune from treating profits not distributed or reduced in distribution by the controlled foreign enterprise and include such dividend equivalents in the income of any such shareholder for the current period:

  1. The controlled foreign enterprise is in a country (region) with a high tax rate designated by the State Administration of Taxation;

  2. The income of controlled foreign enterprise are mainly derived from active business activities; or

  3. Annual total profits made by the controlled foreign enterprise are less than CNY5,000,000.

Chapter IX Administration of Thin Capitalization

Article 85 Interest expenses not allowed to be deducted in calculating taxable income as described in Article 46 of the Income Tax Law, shall be calculated according to the following formula:

Interest expenses not allowed to be deducted=annual interest actually paid to all associated parties x (1-the standard ratio/associated liability-asset ratio )

In the formula, the standard ratio is the ratio specified in the Circular of Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation Concerning Issues of Relevant Tax Policies on Criteria for the Pre-tax Deduction of Interest Expenses Paid by Enterprises to Associated Parties (Cai Shui [2008] No. 121).

The associated liability-asset ratio is the percentage by which debt investments accepted by an enterprise from all of its associated parties (hereinafter refereed to as associated debt investments) in accordance with the provisions of Article 46 of the Income Tax Law and Article 119 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law account for equity investments accepted by the enterprise (hereinafter referred to as equity investments). Associated debt investments include debt investments for which associated parties provide security by way of various manners.

Article 86 The specific calculation method for the associated liability-asset ratio is as follows:

The associated liability-asset ratio =the sum of average associated debt investments for each month within a year/ the sum of average equity investments for each month within the same year

In the foregoing formula:

Average associated debt investments for each month= (book balance for such associated debt investments at the beginning of each month+ book balance for such associated debt investments at the end of each month)/2

Average equity investments for each month= (book balance for such equity investments at the beginning of each month + book balance for such equity investments at the end of each month)/2

Equity investment is the amount of owner's equity set out on the balance sheet of an enterprise; where the owner's equity is less than the sum of paid-in capital(capital stock) and capital surplus, the equity investment is the sum of paid-in capital (capital stock) and capital surplus; where the sum of paid-in capital (capital stock) and capital surplus is less than the paid-in capital(capital stock) , the equity investment is the amount of the paid-in capital(capital stock).

Article 87 The term "interest expenses" as used in Article 46 of the Income Tax Law includes any interest, guarantee fee, mortgage fee, and other expenses with the nature of interest actually paid by an enterprise for direct or indirect associated debt investments.

Article 88 Interest expenses not allowed to be deducted in calculating taxable income, as described in Article 46 of the Income Tax Law, shall not be carried over to subsequent taxable years and shall be distributed among all associated parties according to percentages by which interest actually paid to each associated parties accounts for the total amount of interest paid to all associated parties; interest distributed to any domestic associated party whose tax burden is heavier than the enterprise concerned is allowed to be deducted. Interest paid directly or indirectly to overseas associated parties shall be deemed as distributed dividends and enterprise income tax shall then be levied on such interest by the difference between income tax rates applicable respectively to such dividends and interest. Where the amount of income tax withheld exceeds that of income tax levied on such dividends, such excess shall not be refunded.

Article 89 Where an enterprise, in calculating taxable income, seeks to deduct interest expenses by which the associated liability-asset ratio exceeds the standard ratio, the enterprise shall, in addition to complying with the provisions of Chapter III hereof, prepare, preserve and, as required by a taxation authority, provide the following same-period documents to prove that the amount, interest rate, term, and financing conditions of its associated debt investments as well as its liability-assets ratio, among others, comply with the arm's length principle:

  1. An analysis of the ability of the enterprise to repay debts or obtain loans;

  2. An analysis of the ability of the group with which the enterprise is affiliated to obtain loans and of the financing structure of the group;

  3. A statement on any change in equity investments made by the enterprise such as its registered capital;

  4. The nature and purposes of associated debt investments and the market conditions at the time when such investments are obtained;

  5. The types of currency, amount, interest rate, term and financing requirements of associated debt investments;

  6. Information on security provided by the enterprise and the requirements for the provision of such security;

  7. The status of the guarantors concerned and the requirements for the provisions of the guarantees;

  8. Interest rates for loans of the same type and period and financing requirements for such loans;

  9. Conversion requirements for convertible company bonds; and

  10. Other documents which can prove that the arm's length principle is complied with.

Article 90 Where an enterprise fails to prepare, preserve and provide same-period documents to prove the amount, interest rate, term, and financing conditions of its associated debt investments as well as its liability-assets ratio, among others, comply with the arm's length principle, interest expenses paid by the enterprise in excess of the standard ratio shall not deducted in calculating its taxable income.

Article 91 The term "interest actually paid" as used in this Chapter refers to interest included by an enterprise in relevant cost and expenses based on the accrual basis.

Where there is any transfer pricing issue in connection with interest actually paid by an enterprise to its associated parties, a taxation authority shall first make adjustments to transfer pricing investigations in accordance with the relevant provisions of Chapter V hereof.

Chapter X General Anti-tax Avoidance

Article 92 A taxation authority may, in accordance with Article 47 of the Income Tax Law and Article 120 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law, launch general anti-tax avoidance investigations into any enterprise which has any of the following tax avoidance arrangements:

  1. The enterprise misuses any tax preferential treatment;

  2. The enterprise misapplies any tax treaty;

  3. The enterprise misuses the form of corporate organization;

  4. The enterprise avoids tax by using tax havens; or

  5. The enterprise enters into any other arrangements for non-reasonable commercial purposes.

Article 93 A taxation authority shall, in compliance with the principle of substance over form, examine whether or not an arrangement entered into by an enterprise is a tax avoidance arrangement and take into account comprehensively the following matters:

  1. The form and substance of the arrangement;

  2. The time at which the arrangement is entered into and the period in which the arrangement is implemented;

  3. The manner in which the arrangement is fulfilled;

  4. All stages involved in the arrangement or relationship between the integral parts of the arrangements;

  5. Changes in the financial conditions of all parties involved in the arrangement; and

  6. Tax results arising from the arrangement.

Article 94 A taxation authority may, based on the economic substance doctrine, ascertain anew the nature of a tax-avoidance arrangement and take back tax benefits obtained by the enterprise concerned from such a tax-avoidance arrangement. For any enterprises which do not have economic substance, especially enterprises established within tax havens and which result in the avoidance of tax for their associated or non-associated parties, a taxation authority may deny the existence of any such enterprises.

Article 95 A taxation authority shall, in launching general anti-tax avoidance investigations into an enterprise, serve a Notice of General Anti-tax Avoidance Investigations upon the enterprise in accordance with relevant provisions of the Tax Administration Law and the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law. The enterprise shall, within sixty days of receipt of such a Notice, provide documents to prove that the arrangement in question is entered into for reasonable commercial purposes. Where the enterprise fails to provide the documents within the specified period or documents provided by the enterprise can not prove that the arrangement is entered into for reasonable commercial purposes, the taxation authority may, on the basis of information acquired by it, make tax adjustments and serve a Notice of Special Tax Investigation Adjustments upon the enterprise.

Article 96 A taxation authority, in carrying out general anti-tax avoidance investigations, may in accordance with the provisions of Article 57 of the Tax Administration Law require any party who makes preparation for a tax avoidance arrangement to provide relevant documents and evidence in a honest manner.

Article 97 General anti-tax avoidance investigations and adjustments shall be reported level by level to the State Administration of Taxation for approval.

Chapter XI Corresponding Adjustments and International Negotiations

Article 98 Where transfer pricing investigation adjustments are made with respect to any party to an related-party transaction, the other party to such an related-party transaction shall be allowed to make corresponding adjustments to avoid double taxation. Where such corresponding adjustments involve any associated party in a country (or region) to a tax treaty entered into with China, upon request by the enterprise concerned, the State Administration of Taxation shall carry out negotiations with the competent tax authority of the other party to the tax treaty in accordance with provisions of the tax treaty relating to procedures for mutual consultations.

Article 99 Where appropriate transfer pricing adjustments involve any associated party in a country (or region) under a tax treaty entered into with China, an enterprise may file a written application the State administration of taxation and the competent taxation authority, submit an Application for the Commencement of Mutual Negotiation Procedures and provide relevant documents such as a photocopy of the notice of transfer pricing adjustments made with respect to the enterprises or its foreign associated party.

Article 100 An enterprise shall, within three years of receipt of a notice of transfer pricing adjustments by the enterprise or its foreign associated party, file an application for corresponding adjustments. Where the enterprise files an application beyond the foregoing three-year period, a taxation authority shall not accept such an application.

Article 101 Where a taxation authority makes transfer pricing adjustments with respect to an enterprise , tax which has been withheld on interest, rental or royalties paid by the enterprise to its overseas associated parties shall not be adjusted correspondingly.

Article 102 Where the State Administration of Taxation accepts in accordance with the provisions of Chapter VI hereof an application submitted by an enterprise to conduction negotiations for and enter into a bilateral (or multi-lateral) arrangement for advance pricing, the State Administration of Taxation shall carry out negotiations with the competent taxation authority of the other party to the tax treaty in question in accordance with provisions of the tax treaty relating to procedures for mutual consultations.

Article 103 The State Administration of Taxation shall serve in writing the results of corresponding adjustments or mutual consultations upon the enterprise concerned via the competent taxation authority.

Article 104 The provisions of this Chapter for corresponding adjustments shall not apply to such interest expenses not allowed to be deducted in calculating taxable income and such interest expenses deemed as distributed dividends as are described in Chapter IX hereof.

Chapter XII Legal Liability

Article 105 Where an enterprise fails to submit a annual report on associated business transactions to a taxation authority or preserve same-period documents or relevant information, the relevant matters shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of Article 60 and Article 62 of the Tax Administration Law.

Article 106 Where an enterprise refuses to provide information on related-party transactions such as same-period documents, or provides false or incomplete information which is not able to genuinely reflect its associated business transactions, the relevant matters shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of Article 70 of the Tax Administration Law and Article 96 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law, Article 44 of the Income Tax Law and Article 115 of the Implementation Regulations for the Income Tax Law.

Article 107 Where a taxation authority makes special tax adjustments to an enterprise in accordance with the provisions of the Income Tax Law and its Implementation Regulations, the taxation authority shall charge interest on enterprise income tax levied additionally for any transaction entered into after January 1, 2008 on a daily basis.

  1. Interest shall be accrued on June 1 of the year following the one to which such tax is attributed until the date on which such tax paid additionally (or pre-paid tax) is turned over to the state treasury;

  2. Interest rate shall be the base interest rate on December 31 of the year to which such tax is attributed that is applicable to RBM loans of the People's Bank of China with a term equal to the period for which additional tax is levied (hereinafter referred to as the "base interest rate") plus five percentage points and daily interest rate shall be calculated on the basis of 365 days a year;

  3. Where the enterprise provide same-period documents and other relevant information in accordance with the provisions of these Measures, or although the enterprise, in accordance with the provisions of Article 15 of these Measures, is immune from preparing same-period documents, the enterprise, as required by the taxation authority, provides other relevant information, interest may be charged based on the base interest rate;

Where, although the enterprise, in accordance with the provisions of Item 1 of Article 15 of these Measures, is immune from preparing same-period documents, upon investigations by a taxation authority, the actual amount involved in related-party transactions entered into by the enterprise reaches the level where same-period documents must be prepared, and the taxation authority shall apply the provisions of Item 2 of this Article in charging interest on tax additionally levied; and

  1. Interest charged in accordance with the provisions of this Article shall not be deducted in calculating the taxable income.

Article 108 Where an enterprise prepays tax after a taxation authority has decided to make special tax adjustments and the enterprise pays additional tax after receipt of a notice of adjustments to additional tax, the year to which such prepaid tax is attributed shall be determined on the basis of the order of years to which additional tax payable is attributed and interest shall be charged separately before the date on which such prepaid tax is turned over into the state treasury.

Article 109 An enterprise shall pay additional tax levied as a result of special tax adjustments and interest accrued thereon within a period specified in a notice of adjustments issued by a taxation authority. Where the enterprise, due to special difficulties, is unable to pay such tax within the specified period, the enterprise shall, in accordance with the relevant provisions of Article 31 of the Tax Administration Law and Articles 41 and 42 of the Detailed Implementation Rules for the Tax Administration Law, apply to pay such tax within an extended period. Where the enterprise neither applies for an extension of the specified period nor pay such tax, the taxation authority shall, in accordance with the provisions of Article 32 of the Tax Administration Law and other relevant provisions, deal with the matters.

Chapter XIII Supplementary Provisions

Article 110 Taxation authorities may, after appropriate modifications, apply the relevant provisions of Chapter V of these Measures in carrying out investigation adjustment procedures with respect to other special tax adjustments other than transfer pricing administration and the administration of arrangements for advance pricing.

Article 111 State taxation bureaus and local taxation bureaus at all levels shall strengthen communication and exchanges with each other in the course of making special tax adjustments to enterprises and may, in light of actual requirements, form a joint investigation group to carry out investigations.

Article 112 A taxation authority and its personnel shall, in accordance with the provisions of the Tentative Measures of State Administration of Taxation for the Management of Tax-related Confidential Information of Taxpayers (Guo Shui Fa [2008] No. 93) and other regulations relating to the protection of secrets, preserve and employ information and documents provided by an enterprise.

Article 113 Where the last day of any period specified herein within a legal holiday, the day after the close of the legal holiday shall be the last day of any such period; where three consecutive days or more within any such period are within a legal holiday, any such period shall be extended by the number of the legal holiday.

Article 114 The terms "more than" ,"less than", "within the date", "on the date", "before", "below", "in excess of" as used in these Measures all include the number in question.

Article 115 Where any enterprise which is under investigation, during the period in which special tax investigation adjustments are made by a taxation authority, applies to change its business address or cancel its tax registration, the tax authority in principle shall not handle tax registration change or cancellation formalities before the conclusion of the investigation.

Article 116 Same-period documents to be prepared by an enterprise in accordance with the provisions of Chapter III hereof for related-party transactions entered into in the taxable year of 2008 may be deferred up to December 31, 2009.

Article 117 These Measures shall be interpreted and revised by the State Administration of Taxation.

Article 118 These Measures shall come into effect on January 1, 2008. The Rules of the State Administration of Taxation for the Administration of Tax on Business Transactions between Associated Enterprises (Trial Implementation) (Guo Shui Fa [1998] No. 59), the Circular of the State Administration of Taxation on the Revision of the Rules for the Administration of Tax on Business Transactions between Associated Enterprises (Trial Implementation) (Guo Shui Fa [2004] No. 143) and the Implementation Rules of the State Administration of Taxation for Advance Pricing with respect to Business Transactions between Associated Enterprises (Guo Shui Fa [2004] No. 118) shall be repealed simultaneously. Where there is any conflict between relevant regulations issued before the implementation of these Measures and these Measures, these Measures shall prevail.
