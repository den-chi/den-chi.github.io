Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods (Order of the General Administration of Customs No.219)

2014-03-12

The Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods have been passed at the executive meeting of the General Administration of Customs on February 13, 2014, and are hereby released for implementation as of the date of release. The Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods promulgated under the Order of the General Administration of Customs No.113 on February 26, 2004 and revised under the Orders of the General Administration of Customs No.168 and No.195 shall be repealed simultaneously.

Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 With the goal of promoting the healthy development of processing trade and regulating the administration of processing trade goods by Customs, the Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods (hereinafter referred to as the "Measures") have been formulated in accordance with the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Customs Law") and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 2 The Measures apply to the formalities of the establishment of the manual, import and export customs declarations, as well as the processing, supervision, verification, and writing-off of processing trade goods.

Processing trade and operation enterprises, processing enterprises and undertakers shall be subject to the supervision by the Customs pursuant to the Measures.

Article 3 The term "processing trade" herein refers to business activities in which an operating enterprise imports all or part of the raw or ancillary materials, spare parts, components, and packaging materials (hereinafter collectively referred to as materials) and after processing or assembly, re-exports the finished products, including both the processing of supplied materials and the processing of imported materials.

Article 4 Except otherwise provided by the State, where the imported materials under processing trade fall under the import restrictions as imposed by the State, the operating enterprise shall be exempted from submitting the import license to Customs.

Where the finished export products of processing trade fall under the export restrictions as imposed by the State, the operating enterprise shall submit the export license to Customs.

Article 5 In the event that the imported materials under processing trade fall under bonded supervision, Customs shall, after the export of the finished goods, complete the verification and writing-off according to the verified quantity of the goods actually processed and exported.

In the event that taxes have been collected on imported materials under processing trade in advance pursuant to the relevant provisions, Customs shall, after the export of the finished goods, return the collected taxes according to the verified quantity of the goods actually processed and exported.

Where export customs duties are to be levied on the exported products under processing trade, Customs shall collect the export customs duties pursuant to the relevant provisions.

Article 6 Customs shall carry out the guarantee system with respect to processing trade goods pursuant to the provisions of the State.

Without approval by the Customs, processing trade goods shall not be pledged.

Article 7 Customs shall implement classification supervision over processing trades, with specific administrative measures to be otherwise formulated by the General Administration of Customs (hereinafter referred to as "GACC").

Article 8 Customs may inspect the processing trade enterprises, which must render cooperation.

Customs inspections shall not influence the normal business activities of the enterprises.

Article 9 The establishment of the manual, import and export customs declarations, verification, and writing-off of processing trade goods shall be conducted in the forms of paper documents and electronic data.

Article 10 Processing trade enterprises shall, in accordance with the Accounting Law of the People's Republic of China and relevant provisions of Customs, establish account books, statements, and other relevant documents that comply with the requirements of customs supervision to record the information of import, storage, assignment, transfer, sale, processing, use, wearing-off and export relating to their processing trade goods and keep accounts and make assessments on the basis of legal and valid certificates.

Processing trade enterprises shall apply differentiated management to processing trade goods and non-processing trade goods. Processing trade goods shall be stored at designated places registered with Customs. Approval from Customs is required if the enterprises change the place of storage of the processing trade goods.

Chapter II Establishment of Manual of Processing Trade Goods

Article 11 An operating enterprise shall handle the formalities for the establishment of the manual of processing trade goods with the competent Customs in the area where the processing enterprise is located.

In the event that the operating enterprise and the processing enterprise are not within the jurisdiction of the same Customs directly under the GACC, the formalities for the establishment of the manual of processing trade goods shall be handled pursuant to the provisions of Customs on the administration of processing trade in different places.

Article 12 Unless otherwise stated, when handling the formalities for the establishment of the manual of processing trade goods, an operating enterprise shall faithfully report to Customs the trade method, unit consumption, ports of import and export, as well as the commodity names, commodity numbers, specifications and types, prices, origins and other information of the imported materials and finished export products and shall submit the following documents:

  1. valid documents as issued by the competent authority approving the operation of processing trade;

  2. the Certificate of Production Capacity of Processing Enterprise in Processing Trade as issued by the competent authority in case that the operating enterprise itself maintains the capacity for processing;

  3. contract of entrusted processing between the operating enterprise and the processing enterprise and the Certificate of Production Capacity of Processing Enterprise in Processing Trade of the processing enterprise as issued by the competent authority in case that the operating enterprise entrusts the processing to the processing enterprise;

  4. contract concluded by the operating enterprise with a foreign party; and

  5. any other certificates and materials that Customs deems necessary to be submitted.

Article 13 If an operating enterprise submits complete and valid document materials in accordance with Articles 11 and 12 hereof to apply for the establishment of the manual, Customs shall complete the formalities for the establishment of the manual of processing trade goods within five working days after receiving the application for the establishment of the manual from the enterprise.

In case that it is necessary to undergo the formalities for guarantee, Customs shall handle the formalities for the establishment of the manual only after the operating enterprise provides guarantee pursuant to the provisions.

Article 14 In the event of any of the following circumstances, Customs shall handle the formalities for the establishment of manual only after the operating enterprise has provided guarantee of the same value as that of the payable taxes or a letter of guarantee from a bank or a non-banking financial institution:

  1. being suspected of smuggling and Customs has placed the case on file for investigation and the case has not yet been concluded; and

  2. being required by Customs to make rectification as a result of poor management and the rectification has not yet been fulfilled.

Article 15 In the event of any of the following circumstances, Customs may require the operating enterprise to provide guarantee of the same value as that of the payable taxes or provide a letter of guarantee from a bank or a non-banking financial institution while handling the formalities for the establishment of manual:

  1. leasing of the factory or equipment;

  2. engaging in the processing trade for the first time;

  3. having applied for an extension of the manual of processing trade more than two times (inclusive);

  4. handling the formalities of processing trade in different locations; and

  5. being suspected of breaking rules and Customs has placed the case on file for investigation and the case has not yet been concluded.

Article 16 If an processing trade enterprise falls under any of the following circumstances, formalities for the establishment of the manual shall not be handled:

  1. the imported materials or finished export products fall within the scope of those prohibited by the State from import and export;

  2. the products to be processed fall within the scope of those prohibited by the State from being processed and produced within China;

  3. it is unsuitable to apply bonded supervision over the imported materials;

  4. the operating enterprise or processing enterprise is one that is not permitted to carry out processing trade as provided by the State; and

  5. the operating enterprise applies again to Customs for the establishment of the manual without reporting the manual of processing trade for verification, upon its expiration, to Customs within the prescribed time limit.

Article 17 In the event that the declaration contents and the documents submitted by the operating enterprise for handling the formalities for the establishment of the manual of processing trade goods are inconsistent with the facts, Customs shall handle the case pursuant to the following provisions:

  1. where the goods have not been imported, Customs shall write off the manual thereof; and

  2. where the goods have been imported, order the enterprise to return goods outbound.

In the circumstance specified in Item 2, Paragraph 1 of Article 17, the operating enterprise may apply to Customs for the solution of providing guarantee of the same value as that of the payable taxes or a letter of guarantee from a bank or a non-banking financial institution and continue to perform contract.

Article 18 An operating enterprise that has handled the formalities for the establishment of manual of the processing trade goods may apply to Customs for the sub-volume or follow-up volume of the manual of processing trade.

Article 19 In the event that there is any alteration of the contents of the manual of processing trade goods, the operating enterprise shall handle the formalities for alteration within the valid term of the manual of processing trade.

In case that it is necessary to report to the original examining and approving department for approval, such a report shall be made, save as otherwise stated.

Chapter III Import, Export, and Processing of Processing Trade Goods

Article 20 When an operating enterprise imports processing trade goods, it may either import them from foreign counties, areas under special supervision by Customs, or bonded sites under supervision, or transfer them into China by way of deep processing and transfer.

When an operating enterprise exports goods under processing trade, it may either export the goods to foreign countries, areas under special supervision by Customs, or bonded sites under supervision, or transfer them out of China by way of deep processing and transfer.

Article 21 An operating enterprise shall handle the formalities for import and export customs declaration of processing trade goods on the strength of the manual of processing trade, special customs declaration form for import and export of processing trade goods and other relevant documents.

Article 22 The goods imported and exported by operating enterprises by way of processing trade shall be included in customs statistics.

Article 23 If processing trade enterprises carry out deep processing and transfer, transfer-in enterprises and transfer-out enterprises shall apply to their respective competent Customs for handling actual goods receiving and delivery and customs declaration formalities. Specific administrative measures shall be otherwise formulated and released by GACC.

In the event of any of the following circumstances, the processing trade enterprise shall not handle deep processing and transfer formalities:

  1. not meeting supervision requirements of Customs, and being ordered by Customs to make rectification within a prescribed time limit and the rectification has not yet been fulfilled;

  2. not reporting the manual for verification upon its expiration; and

  3. being suspected of smuggling and Customs has placed the case on file for investigation and the case has not yet been concluded.

The processing trade enterprise which fails to receive and deliver goods in accordance with the provisions of Customs shall not handle formalities for deep processing and transfer again.

Article 24 An operating enterprise may undertake outward processing business and shall follow the provisions regarding the outward processing to handle filing formalities with Customs within three working days after being contracted out.

When processing trade is contracted out, the operating enterprise may not sell the processing trade goods to the undertaking enterprise. The undertaker may not subsequently contract the processing trade goods.

If the operating enterprise contracts out all processes, it shall provide guarantee of the same value as that of the payable taxes of outward processing goods or a letter of guarantee from a bank or a non-banking financial institution while handling filing formalities.

Article 25 Upon the approval of the competent Customs at the place where the enterprise is located, an operating enterprise does not necessarily transport processing trade goods such as the finished products, leftover materials, residual materials, inferior products and byproducts generated during the outward processing back to itself.

Article 26 If Customs implements supervision over processing trade goods, the operating enterprise and undertaker shall cooperate with the Customs.

Article 27 Materials for processing trade goods shall be used for their designated purposes only.

Exchange between enterprises' bonded materials, and between bonded and non-bonded materials may be conducted upon approval by the Customs, provided that such exchange of materials is allowed only within the same enterprise under the principles of the same variety, specifications and quantity and non-profit.

Exchange is not allowed between bonded materials imported with materials supplied by clients for processing.

Article 28 If an operating enterprise must use non-bonded materials due to the needs of processing techniques, it shall submit a faithfully declaration of the proportion, category, specification, type, and quantity of the non-bonded materials to be used to Customs in advance.

If the operating enterprise submits a declaration to Customs in accordance with Paragraph 1 of Article 28, Customs shall write off those non-bonded materials from the total materials consumed for the finished export products.

Article 29 In the event that an operating enterprise needs to return to or change the imported materials with the original supplier as a result of quality problem or non-conformity of the imported materials with the specification or type as stipulated in the contract and non-processing bonded materials are exported due to needs of after-sale services of processing trade goods exported, it may handle the customs declaration formalities directly with the port Customs.

Bonded imported materials that have already been processed may not be returned or changed.

Chapter IV Verification and Writing-off of Processing Trade Goods

Article 30 The operating enterprise shall process and re-export the imported materials within the prescribed time limit and shall report to Customs for verification and writing-off within 30 days from the day of export of the last batch of finished products under the manual of processing trade or the day of expiration of the manual of processing trade.

In the event that a contract concluded by an operating enterprise with a foreign party is terminated before expiration, the enterprise shall report to Customs for verification and writing-off within 30 days from the day of termination of the contract.

Article 31 An operating enterprise shall, when reporting for verification and writing-off, faithfully declare the information about the imported materials, the finished export products, leftover materials, residual materials, inferior products, byproducts and unit consumption and shall submit the relevant documents in accordance with the provisions.

If the operating enterprise reports to Customs for verification and writing-off in accordance with Paragraph 1 of this article and submits complete and valid documents, Customs shall accept the report for verification and writing-off.

Article 32 Customs may undertake the verification and writing-off via paper documents or electronic data and may go to the factories to conduct inspections if necessary and the enterprise shall render cooperation.

Customs shall complete the verification and writing-off within 30 days from the day of the acceptance of the report. In the event that it is necessary to extend the period due to special circumstances, it may be extended for 30 days upon approval of the director of Customs directly under the GACC or the director of the subordinate Customs authorized thereby.

Article 33 In the event that the imported materials or finished products under processing trade are instead sold on the domestic market due to certain reasons, Customs may, on the strength of the valid document of approval for domestic sale issued by the competent authority, collect taxes on the bonded imported materials in accordance with the law and collect additional interest for late tax collection, unless otherwise stated.

In the event that the imported materials fall within the import restrictions imposed by the State, the operating enterprise shall also submit the import license to the Customs.

Article 34 In the event that an operating enterprise returns the imported materials under processing trade out of China, Customs shall complete the verification and writing-off on the strength of the relevant certificate of returning.

Article 35 The leftover materials, residual materials, inferior products, and byproducts generated by the operating enterprise during the production process as well as bonded goods suffering losses from disasters shall be dealt with in accordance with the provisions of Customs regarding the administration of such materials and goods. Customs shall complete the verification and writing-off on the strength of the relevant documents.

Article 36 In the event that an operating enterprise loses its manual of processing trade, it shall report to Customs in a timely manner.

Customs shall write off the lost manual of processing trade after handling the case in accordance with the relevant provisions.

Article 37 In regard to a manual of processing trade that is, upon verification, allowed to be written off, Customs shall issue the Notification of the Conclusion of Verification and Writing-off to the operating enterprise.

Article 38 In the event that the operating enterprise has provided guarantee, Customs shall discharge the guarantee pursuant to the provisions after the conclusion of the verification and writing-off.

Article 39 The establishment of the manual of processing trade goods and the written-off documents shall be kept for three years from the day of conclusion of the verification and writing-off of the manual of processing trade.

Article 40 In the event that a processing trade enterprise is split, merged, or becomes insolvent, is dissolved or is involved in any other circumstance with normal operating activities ceased, it shall report to Customs in a timely manner and complete customs formalities.

In the event that the processing trade goods are sealed by the people's court or the relevant law enforcement department, the processing trade enterprise shall report to Customs within five working days from the day of the sealing of those goods.

Chapter V Supplementary Provisions

Article 41 In the event that smuggling, violations of provisions on customs supervision or other provisions of Customs Law are constituted as the Measures are violated, Customs shall deal with the case in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Customs Law and the Implementing Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Customs Administrative Penalties; if a crime has been constituted, criminal liability shall be prosecuted in accordance with the law.

Article 42 The following terms as used herein shall be defined as follows:

The term "processing of supplied materials" refers to business activities in which the imported materials are supplied by an overseas enterprise and the operating enterprise need not pay foreign exchange for the import, but carries out the processing or assembly in accordance with the requirements of the overseas enterprise and charges for the processing, with the finished products being marketed by the overseas enterprise.

The term "processing of imported materials" refers to business activities in which an operating enterprise pays foreign exchange for the imported materials and exports the finished products for marketing.

The term "processing trade goods" refers to imported materials and finished processed products under processing trade, as well as the leftover materials, inferior products and byproducts generated during processing.

The term "processing trade enterprises" includes those operating enterprises and processing enterprises which have been registered with Customs.

The term "operating enterprise" refers to the various kinds of import and export enterprises and foreign-funded enterprises that conclude contracts of processing import and export trade with foreign parties, as well as the foreign processing and assembly service companies that have obtained the license for the processing of imported materials upon approval.

The term "processing enterprise" refers to manufacturing enterprises that, upon entrustment by the operating enterprises, assume the processing or assembly of imported materials and maintain the status of a legal person, as well as factories without the status of legal persons established by the operating enterprises, but employing relatively independent accounting and having obtained the business certificate (license) of industry and commerce.

The term "unit consumption quantity" refers to the quantity of the imported materials consumed per finished export product by the processing trade enterprise under normal production conditions and is referred to as "unit consumption" for short.

The term "deep processing and transfer" refers to business activities in which a processing trade enterprise transfers the products processed by using bonded imported materials to another processing trade enterprise for further processing and re-export.

The term "undertaker" refers to the enterprise or individual that concludes the processing contract with the operating enterprise and undertakes the outward processing operations entrusted by the operating enterprise.

The term "outward processing" refers to the act of an operating enterprise to entrust an undertaker to process a certain operational procedure with respect to the processing of trade goods and transport of the processed goods back to the operating enterprise within a prescribed time limit for final re-export.

The term "verification and writing-off" refers to the act in which Customs, upon receiving the application by the processing trade operating enterprise reporting for verification by virtue of required documents after completing the formalities for re-export, domestic sale or others, lift the supervision after examination and inspection in accordance with the provisions.

Article 43 In the event that a bonded factory carries out processing trade, the provisions of Customs on the administration of bonded factories in the processing trade shall be followed.

Article 44 In the event that a bonded group in the processing of imported materials carries out processing trade, the provisions of Customs on the administration of bonded groups in the processing of imported materials shall be followed.

Article 45 In the event that a processing trade enterprise subject to computer network supervision carries out processing trade, the provisions of Customs on the administration of computer network control of processing trade enterprises shall be followed.

Article 46 In the event that a processing trade enterprise carries out processing trade in areas under special customs supervision, the provisions of Customs on the administration of areas under special customs supervision shall be followed.

Article 47 The declaration and verification of unit consumption shall be carried out in accordance with the provisions of Customs on the administration of unit consumption of processing trade.

Article 48 Separate provisions shall be formulated in regard to the administration of the tax refunds by Customs after the export of processing trade goods on which taxes have been collected upon import.

Article 49 The Measures shall be construed by the GACC

Article 50 The Measures shall come into force as of the date of release. The Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Supervision and Administration of Processing Trade Goods released under the Order of the General Administration of Customs No.113 on February 26, 2004 and revised under the Orders of the General Administration of Customs No.168 and No.195 shall be repealed simultaneously.
