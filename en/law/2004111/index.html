Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Administration of Leftover Pieces, Surplus Pieces, Defective Products, By-products and Disaster-damaged Bonded Goods in Processing Trade (Revised in 2014) Order of the General Administration of Customs No.218

2014/03/13

March 13, 2014

Article 1 In order to normalize the examination and approval of domestic market sales and the customs supervision of the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products, damaged bonded goods produced from the bonded imported materials and parts of processing trade, Measures of the Customs of the People's Republic of China for the Administration of Leftover Pieces, Surplus Pieces, Defective Products, By-products and Disaster-damaged Bonded Goods in Processing Trade (Revised in 2014) (hereinafter referred to as the "Measures") are hereby formulated in accordance with the Customs Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Customs Law") and other pertinent laws and administrative regulations.

Article 2 The meanings of the following terms used in the Measures are as follows:

Leftover pieces refer to the reasonable quantity of waste, small and leftover pieces produced by a trade processing enterprise engaged in the operation of processing for re-export in the course of processing within the unit consumption ratified by customs (hereinafter referred to as the "unit consumption"), which can no longer be used in the processing of the finished exported products under contract.

Surplus pieces refer to the remaining imported materials and parts in processing trade of a trade processing enterprise during the operation for processing for re-export, which can be used in the subsequent processing of finished goods.

Defective products refer to the products (including finished and semi-finished products) produced in the operation of processing for re-export by a trade processing enterprise, which have major defects in the production process or fail to meet the requirements of the export contract and cannot be re-exported.

By-products refer to one or more than one other products, produced in the course of processing and production of the finished products (namely the main products) prescribed in the export contract by a trade processing enterprise engaged in the operation of processing for re-export, and not required to be re-exported in the export contract.

Disaster-damaged bonded goods refer to the bonded imported materials and parts and finished products that cannot be re-exported due to the loss, lack, or damage caused by force majeure or other justifiable reasons examined and recognized by customs in the operation of processing for re-export by a trade processing enterprise.

Article 3 No enterprise, unit or individual may, without the permission of customs, sell or use for other purposes the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products and disaster-damaged bonded goods, which are subject to the supervision by customs, produced after the trade processing of bonded imported materials and parts.

Article 4 Where a trade processing enterprise applies to sell the leftover pieces in the domestic market, the department in charge of commerce shall exempt the enterprise from examination and approval and the enterprise shall report to the competent customs for direct ratification and handle the relevant formalities.

  1. After classifying the status of application for inspection on the leftover pieces for domestic sales made by the processing trade enterprise, the competent customs shall, according to the applicable interest rate and the finalized price for the leftover pieces, calculate and collect tax, and shall exempt the interest for tax delay.

  2. After the classification of the status of application for inspection on the leftover pieces for domestic sales made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, if the application is subject to the administration of the import license by the State Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Commerce, the Environmental Protection Administration and their authorized departments, the submission of import licenses shall be exempted.

Article 5 Where a trade processing enterprise declares to transfer the surplus pieces for use under another trade processing contract, the transfer shall be limited to the same operating enterprise, the same processing enterprise, the same imported materials and parts and the same form of processing trade. Any enterprise that meets the relevant conditions may undergo the procedures for cancellation of the contract as well as the transfer of the surplus pieces after the customs ratifies the unit consumption. For takeover contract of surplus pieces having been examined and approved by the department in charge of commerce, the relevant formalities will be dealt with by the original examining and approving authority through alteration. In case that the takeover quantity of surplus pieces will not cause the increment of total import quantity specified in the previously approved contract, the alteration formalities can be exempted; in case that the takeover contract is a new contract, the department in charge of commerce shall deal with the formalities in accordance with the current provisions on examination and approval for processing trade.

Where a trade processing enterprise that declares the transfer of the surplus pieces falls in any of the following circumstances, the customs shall grant its approval after the enterprise pays the risk security in an amount of not more than taxes payable for the bonded materials and parts to be transferred:

  1. the same operating enterprise declares to transfer the surplus pieces to another processing enterprise;

  2. the transfer-out amount of the surplus pieces reaches 50% or above of total amount of actual imported materials and parts under the processing trade contract; or

  3. the processing trade contract under which the surplus pieces are contemplated has been extended twice or above.

Where different competent customs are involved in transfer of the surplus pieces, the relevant formalities shall be handled with the two customs and the customs in the place to which the surplus pieces are transfer shall receive the risk security.

Where the operating trade enterprise that shall pay the risk security as specified in the preceding paragraph falls in any of the following circumstances, it shall be exempted from the risk security:

  1. it shall be subject to Type A administration of processing trade;

  2. the actually transferred amount of the contracts that use transfers through desk accounts is not less than the amount of taxes payable for the bonded materials and parts to be transferred; or

  3. where the original enterprise falls under any of legal circumstances such as relocation, merger, division, reorganization, reform of corporate system and equity change, if the enterprise succeeds the main rights and obligations or debtor-creditor relationships, the transfer of the surplus pieces will not be limited by the provisions on the same operating enterprise, the same processing enterprise and the same form of trade.

Article 6 Where a trade processing enterprise applies to sell the surplus pieces or the finished products produced by using the surplus pieces in the domestic market, it shall be handled according to the following circumstances:

  1. if the sum of the surplus pieces accounts for less than 3% (inclusive) of the actual total sum of the imported materials and parts under that trade processing contract, and the total sum is less than CNY10,000 (inclusive), the department in charge of commerce shall exempt examination and approval and the enterprise shall directly report to the competent customs for ratification; the competent customs shall verify and write off the surplus pieces after calculating and collecting the tax and the interest for tax delay in accordance with the relevant provisions. For those subject to the administration of import licenses by the State Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Commerce, the Environmental Protection Administration and their authorized departments, the submission of import license shall be exempted.

  2. if the sum of the surplus pieces accounts for more than 3% of the actual total sum of the imported materials and parts under that trade processing contract or the total sum is more than CNY10,000, the department in charge of commerce shall examine and approve in accordance with the provisions on examination and approval of sales in the domestic market, and customs shall, relying on the approval document issued by the department in charge of commerce, calculate and collect the tax and the interest for tax delay for all of the surplus pieces under that contract to be sold in the domestic market. For the surplus pieces subject to the administration of import licenses, the enterprise shall additionally submit the relevant import licenses to customs in accordance with the provisions.

  3. if the finished products produced by using the surplus pieces need to be sold in the domestic market, they shall be handled by the customs, based on the value of their corresponding imported materials and parts, in accordance with the provisions of Item 1 or Item 2 of the Article.

Article 7 In handling defective products to be sold in the domestic market by a trade processing enterprise based on the value of their corresponding imported materials and parts, Item 1 or Item 2 of Article 6 of the Measures shall apply mutatis mutandis.

Article 8 If the by-products that are produced in the course of processing and production or that can be recovered by a processing trade enterprise but have not been used for re-export, the processing trade enterprise shall declare the same truthfully to the customs at the time of establishment of the manual or verification and write-off.

For the by-products that the processing trade enterprise requires for domestic sale, the department in charge of commerce shall list the commodity names for domestic sale according to the actual condition of the by-products, and examine and approve in accordance with the relevant provisions on domestic sale in processing trade, and the customs shall, relying on the approval document of the department in charge of commerce, deal with the relevant formalities for domestic sale. For the by-products required for domestic sale, the customs shall, after classifying the status of application for inspection on the by-products for domestic sales made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, calculate and collect tax and the interest of delay tax according to the applicable interest rate and the finalized price.

The customs shall classify the status of application for inspection on the by-products for domestic sales made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, and if the by-products are subject to the administration of import license, the enterprise shall submit the relevant import license to the customs in accordance with the provisions.

Article 9 For disaster-damaged bonded goods (including leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products and by-products) in processing trade that encounter loss, lack or damage during the period of transportation, storage or processing, the trade processing enterprise shall report to the competent customs on a timely basis, and customs may send personnel to check and collect evidence according to the circumstances.

  1. In case of disaster-damaged bonded goods in processing trade caused by force majeure factors, after verification, for disaster-damaged bonded goods that have been lost or have not been lost but have been completely devalued and cannot be further used, the customs shall grant the verification and write-off of tax-exemption; for disaster-damaged goods that have lost the original value for use but can still be used, customs shall verify and write off them after calculating and collecting the tax and the interest for tax delay according to the finalized price of the disaster-damaged bonded goods and the applicable tax rate of their corresponding imported materials and parts. If the original imported materials and parts corresponding to the disaster-damaged bonded goods are subject to the administration of import licenses by the State Development and Reform Commission, the Ministry of Commerce, the Environmental Protection Administration and their authorized departments, the submission of licenses shall be exempted. When applying for verification and write-off within the prescribed period, the enterprise shall provide the following proof:

(1) the opinions endorsed by the department in charge of commerce;

(2) the notification of insurance compensation produced by the insurance company or the relevant certifications of inspection and quarantine produced by the department of inspection and quarantine; and

(3) other valid certifications recognized by customs.

  1. In case of loss, lack or damage of the processing trade bonded goods during the period of transportation, storage or processing owing to reasonable grounds that are examined and recognized by the customs other than force majeure factors, the customs, based on the opinions endorsed by the department in charge of commerce, the certifications issued by the relevant competent department, and notification of insurance compensation produced by the insurance company or the relevant certifications of inspection and quarantine produced by the department of inspection and quarantine, shall verify and write off them after calculating and collecting the tax and the interest for tax delay in accordance with the provisions. If the original imported materials and parts corresponding to the disaster-damaged bonded goods prescribed in the Paragraph are subject to the administration of import license, the enterprise shall be required to submit the relevant import license to the customs, provided that the submission of the import license is exempted as stipulated in Articles 4, 6 and 7 hereof.

Article 10 If a processing trade enterprise applies to return and transport the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products or disaster-damaged bonded goods out of the country for certain reasons, the customs shall handle the case in accordance with the relevant provisions on returning and transporting and shall handle the formalities of verification and write-off by relying on the relevant proofs for returning and transporting.

Article 11 If a processing trade enterprise cannot sell in the domestic market or return and transport the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products or disaster-damaged bonded goods with cause, it shall entrust an entity having legal qualifications to destroy them. The customs shall handle the verification and write-off formalities on the strength of the relevant documents, receipts and evidence of disposal issued by the entity that disposes of them.

The customs may send personnel to supervise the disposal and the processing trade enterprise and the relevant entity that disposes of them shall provide cooperation. The processing trade enterprise shall truly declare the incomes from disposal to the customs. The customs shall handle the formalities of levying duties with reference to administrative provisions on levying duties for domestic sales of the leftover pieces.

Article 12 For the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products and disaster-damaged bonded goods subject to the administration of import tariff quota, the following provisions shall be complied with:

  1. in the event that the leftover pieces are the commodities subject to the administration of tariff quota according to the classification of status of application for domestic sale inspection made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, the customs shall calculate and collect the tax as per the tax rate of tariff quota;

  2. in the event that the by-products are subject to the administration of tariff quota according to the classification of status of application for domestic sale inspection made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, if the enterprise can submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall calculate and collect the tax as per the tax rate of tariff quota; if the enterprise cannot submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall handle the case in accordance with the relevant provisions;

  3. in the event that the imported materials and parts corresponding to the surplus pieces and defective products are subject to the administration of tariff quota, if the enterprise can submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall calculate and collect the tax as per the tax rate of tariff quota; if the enterprise cannot submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall handle the case in accordance with the relevant provisions; and

  4. in the event that the imported materials and parts corresponding to the disaster-damaged bonded goods caused by force majeure factors are subject to the administration of tariff quota, the customs shall calculate and collect the tax as per the tax rate of tariff quota; in the event that the disaster-damaged bonded goods are caused by other reasonable grounds examined and recognized by the customs, and the corresponding imported materials and parts are subject to the administration of tariff quota, if the enterprise can submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall calculate and collect the tax as per the tax rate of tariff quota; if the enterprise cannot submit the relevant import quota license to the customs as stipulated, the customs shall handle the case in accordance with the relevant provisions;

Article 13 In case of additionally collected antidumping tax, countervailing duty, safeguard tax or retaliatory tariffs (hereinafter referred to as special tariffs), the following provisions shall be complied with:

  1. in the event that the leftover pieces are subject to special tariffs according to the classification of status of application for domestic sale inspection made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, the customs shall exempt the special tariffs required to be collected additionally;

  2. in the event that the by-products are subject to special tariffs according to the classification of status of application for domestic sale inspection made by the processing trade enterprise to the customs, the customs shall collect the special tariffs required to be collected additionally as required;

  3. in the event that the imported materials and parts corresponding to the surplus pieces and defective products are subject to special tariffs, the customs shall collect the special tariffs required to be collected additionally as required; and

  4. in the event that the disaster-damaged bonded goods are caused by force majeure factors, if the original use value has been deprived and the corresponding imported materials and parts are subject to special tariffs, the customs shall exempt the special tariffs required to be collected additionally; in the event that the disaster-damaged bonded goods are caused by other reasonable grounds examined and recognized by the customs, and the corresponding imported materials and parts are subject to special tariffs, the customs shall collect the special tariffs required to be collected additionally as required.

Article 14 When going through the customs clearance procedures for import and export of the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products, by-products or disaster-damaged bonded goods for domestic sale, the following provisions shall be complied with:

  1. for the domestic sale of surplus pieces, defective products and disaster-damaged bonded goods in processing trade, the enterprises shall report the names of original imported materials and parts in the processing trade; and

  2. for the leftover pieces and by-products in processing trade, the enterprises shall declare the status of application for domestic sale inspection to the customs.

Article 15 For the leftover pieces, surplus pieces, defective products and by-products produced in the processing of bonded imported materials and parts in processing trade by processing trade enterprises in bonded zones or export processing zones, the examination and approval for domestic sale and the customs supervision shall be conducted in accordance with the provisions on bonded zones and export processing zones.

Article 16 The customs shall handle the violations of the Customs Law and the provisions of the Measures which constitute smuggling or violations of customs supervisory provisions in accordance with relevant laws and administrative regulations including the Customs Law and Implementing Regulations of the Customs of the People's Republic of China on Administrative Penalties; if a crime is constituted, prosecution for criminal liability shall be undertaken according to law.

Article 17 The power to interpret the Measures shall remain with the General Administration of Customs.

Article 18 The Measures shall come into force as of July 1, 2004. The Measures for the Administration of the Leftover Pieces, Surplus Pieces, Defective Products, By-products and Disaster-damaged Bonded Goods in Processing Trade (Order of the General Administration of Customs No.87) promulgated on September 13, 2001 shall be repealed simultaneously.
