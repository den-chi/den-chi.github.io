Circular on Issues concerning the Registration and Administration of Dissolution and Cancellation of Foreign-invested Enterprises Gong Shang Wai Qi Zi [2008] No.226

2008-10-20

To the administrations of industry and commerce and the departments of commerce in all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities, separately planned cities and sub-provincial cities and the bureau of commerce of the Xinjiang Production and Construction Force,

The "Measures on Liquidating Foreign-Invested Enterprises" (hereinafter referred to as the "Liquidation Measures") are repealed by Decree of State Council No. 516, "Decisions on Repealing Certain Administrative Regulations". To clarify the laws applicable to dissolution, liquidation and deregistration of foreign-invested enterprises after the repeal of the "Liquidation Measures", regulate the procedures for approving dissolution and deregistration and enhance the mechanisms for withdrawal of foreign-invested enterprises, the matters regarding the administration of dissolving, liquidating and deregistering foreign-invested enterprises are hereby announced as follows:

I Applicability - According to the rules governing the application of laws under the "Practical Opinions Concerning the Laws Applicable to the Company Registration for Foreign Investments" (Gongshangwaiqizi No. 81 / 2006), dissolution, liquidation and deregistration of foreign-invested companies shall be bound by the "Company Law" and "Company Registration Regulations". Equity joint ventures shall also be bound by Articles 90 and 95 of the "Regulations on Enforcing the Equity Joint Venture Law". Cooperative joint ventures shall also be bound by Article 48 of the "Rules on Enforcing the Cooperative Joint Venture Law". Jointly or solely foreign-invested companies shall also be bound by the rules governing dissolution and liquidation of foreign-invested enterprises provided by Articles 72 and 73 of the "Rules on Enforcing the Foreign-Invested Enterprise Law".

II. Events of Dissolution and Approval Basis - Foreign-invested companies dissolved in the statutory events as specified in Article 181 of the "Company Law", Article 90 (i) (c) of the "Regulations on Enforcing the Equity Joint Venture Law", Article 48 (i) (c) of the "Rules on Enforcing the Cooperative Joint Venture Law" and other relevant provisions shall be respectively governed by the following rules:

(a) A foreign-invested company which dissolves as a result of the expiration of the business term as specified in the articles of association, court decision, revocation of its business licence, close-down order or being unqualified shall directly undergo the liquidation procedures without the need for such liquidationto be approved by the regulatory bodies.

(b) Under Article 90 (i) (b), (d), (e) and (f) of the "Regulations on Enforcing the Equity Joint Venture Law", Article 48 (i) (b) and (d) of the "Rules on Enforcing the Cooperative Joint Venture Law" or Article 72 (i) (b), (c) and (f) of the "Rules on Enforcing the Foreign-Invested Enterprises Law", a foreign-invested company which intends to dissolve before the expiration of the business term shall have its dissolution approved by the regulatory bodies.

(c) A party to an equity joint venture or a cooperative joint venture who wishes to unilaterally dissolve the joint venture before the expiration of the business term in reliance on Article 90 (i) (c) of the "Regulations on Enforcing the Equity Joint Venture Law" or Article 48 (i) (c) of the "Rules on Enforcing the Cooperative Joint Venture Law" shall acquire the approval of the regulatory bodies or an order of the people's court.

(d) A foreign-invested company which intends to dissolve under Paragraphs (b) or (c) as specified above shall file with the regulatory bodies the application form for early dissolution, resolutions in favour of the early dissolution passed by the authorities of the company, company certificate and business licence.

III. Liquidation Procedures - A foreign-invested company which is to dissolve shall establish a liquidation team in accordance with the relevant statutory requirements. The liquidation team shall file the names of the team members and leader with the companies registry within 10 days after the establishment of the team.

IV. Deregistration Procedures - During the liquidation, the liquidation team shall act in compliance with the "Company Law" to clear the creditor's rights and debts, handle and settle the unfinished business with the companies related to the liquidation, pay taxes owed by the company and pay taxes becoming liable in the course of the liquidation. Upon completion of the liquidation, the liquidation team shall prepare a liquidation report, which shall be approved by the authorities of the company or the people's court, and then make an application for deregistration to the companies registry.

V. Documents Required for Deregistration - Foreign-invested companies which make applications for deregistration shall submit the documents required by the "Notice on Altering Certain Forms and Documents for the Registration of Foreign-Invested Enterprises" (Gongshangwaiqizi No. 213 / 2005) and this Notice. In the "Documents Required for Deregistration of Foreign-Invested Companies", the 4th paragraph shall be changed from "liquidation reports lawfully filed and approved" to "liquidation reports approved at the members' meetings, general meetings (board meetings or general management meetings of equity joint ventures or joint venture companies limited by liability) or the people's courts" and from "the liquidation report shall include the sample announcement of deregistration in the newspaper" to "the liquidation report shall include the sample liquidation announcement by the liquidation team in the newspaper". "Evidence of tax payment issued by the taxation authorities and customs offices" provided in the 5th paragraph is no longer required. In the requirements of standard complete forms, "the liquidation report shall explain the payment of taxes owed by the company and taxes becoming liable in the course of the liquidation (including the payment and verification of customs duties and tax liability), return and revocation of the certificates and receipt of the related acknowledgements from the departments of commerce" has been inserted, as specified in the "altered registration forms and documents as well as the requirements" enclosed with this Notice.

VI. Dissolution, Liquidation and Deregistration of Foreign-Invested Enterprises not Operating in the form of a Company - Foreign-invested enterprises which do not operate in the form of a company shall dissolve, be liquidated and deregister under the "Legal Entity Registration Regulations", "Rules on Enforcing the Legal Entity Registration Regulations", "Rules on Enforcing the Cooperative Joint Venture Law", "Rules on Enforcing the Foreign-Invested Enterprises Law" and the relevant provisions. The materials required to support applications for deregistration shall remain unchanged.

All the local authorities shall strength the cooperation and communication with the taxation authorities, customs offices and other departments concerned, establish effective administrative mechanisms, regularly report on the progress and share information. New situations and new problems shall be identified in practice, and shall be well studied and reported.

Enclosure:Forms and Documents Required for Deregistration of Foreign-Invested Companies as well as the Requirements

No. Documents

1 "Application Form for Deregistration of a Foreign-Invested Company" signed by the liquidation team leader

2* Approval documents for the deregistration issued by the original regulatory bodies

3* Legally valid resolutions or decisions

4* Liquidation report approved by the authorities of the company or the people's court

5 Certificate of the Deregistration of the Subsidiary

6 Original business licence and copies

7 Other related documents

Requirements:

  1. The application form shall be completed properly and clearly with a black or blue pen or ball-pen.

  2. The documents listed above shall be original, unless it is expressed that copies are required.

  3. Should the documents listed above be written in a foreign language, the Chinese translations sealed by the translation firm shall be submitted.

  4. Item no. 2 above is not required where the company's business term expires, the court or arbitration centre decides to dissolve or liquidate the company, the administrative bodies order the company to close down, the company's business licence or certificate of incorporation is revoked or the company's registration of incorporation is cancelled.

  5. Item no. 3 is referred to as resolutions or decisions passed or made in compliance with the "Company Law of the People's Republic of China", "Equity Joint Venture Law of the People's Republic of China", "Cooperative Joint Venture Law of the People's Republic of China", "Foreign-Invested Enterprise Law of the People's Republic of China" and the articles of association and consistently in favour of the details provided in the application. Where the decision of dissolution or liquidation is made by the people's court or arbitration centre, close-down order is given by the administrative bodies or the company's business licence or certificate of incorporation is revoked, the documents in relation to the decision made by the court or arbitration centre, close-down order of the administrative bodies and decision on the revocation of the business licence or certificate of incorporation shall be submitted respectively. Where the company's registration of incorporation is removed by the companies registry on the ground of violating the "Company Registration Regulations", the decision of the companies registry on removing the registration of incorporation shall be submitted.

  6. Item no. 4 shall include the sample liquidation announcement by the liquidation team in the newspaper and explanation for the payment of taxes owed by the company and taxes becoming liable in the course of the liquidation (including the payment and verification of customs duties and tax liability), return and revocation of the certificates and the receipt of the related acknowledgements from the departments of commerce.
