Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning the Extension of the Period for the Declaration of Overdue Export Tax Rebate (Exemption)

Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2014] No 20

April 4, 2014

In order to actively launch the "Project of Facilitating the People in Processing Taxation Matters" in the process of export tax rebate, effectively solve the problems on export tax rebate of taxpayers, and standardize the law enforcement of the tax authorities, the State Administration of Taxation has made a decision that the period for declaring any export tax rebate (exemption) that fails to be declared within a time limit may be extended, and hereby announces relevant issues as below:

Article 1 Where the goods exported by an export enterprise or other entity before December 31, 2013, are under the circumstance specified in Item 18 of Article 2 of the Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning the Administrative Measures for Value-added Tax and Consumption Tax on Export Goods and Labor Services (Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2013] No.12) and an application for export tax rebate (exemption) fails to be filed with the competent tax authority before the deadline for declaring export tax rebate (exemption), the export enterprise or other entity may, before June 30, 2014, file an application with the competent tax authority, and provide relevant evidence production materials.

Article 2 With regard to an application for export tax rebate (exemption) filed by an export enterprise or other entity according to the provisions of Item 18 of Article 2 of the Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2013] No. 12 and Article 1 of this Announcement, the competent tax authority shall, within ten working days after the specified deadline for filing such application, complete the preliminary examination, and report the same level by level until to the State administration of taxation of a province, autonomous region, municipality directly under the Central Government or city specifically designated in the State plan (hereinafter referred to as a "provincial State administration of taxation") for reexamination; such provincial State administration of taxation shall, within ten working days, complete the reexamination, and provide to the competent tax authority the reexamination result which is informed by the competent tax authority to the export enterprise or other entity. Such export tax rebate (exemption) declared by an export enterprise or other entity shall be reviewed and handled as required if tax fraud is suspected to be committed or any other doubt is involved.

Article 3 With regard to the goods that an export enterprise or other entity is entrusted to export in 2013, if the client files an application for the issuance of the Certificate for Goods Exported upon Entrustment with the competent tax authority before March 15, 2014 according to the provisions of Article 11 of the Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning Value-added Tax and Consumption Tax on Export Goods and Labor Services (Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2013] No. 65), and the competent tax authority accepts such application but fails to enter the electronic data of the Certificate for Goods Exported upon Entrustment in the export tax rebate review system in a timely manner due to the reasons such as a festival or holiday, the competent tax authority shall, before April 10, 2014, enter the data in the export tax rebate review system, and review and handle the data as required.

Article 4 This Announcement becomes effective as of the date of promulgation.

It is hereby announced.
