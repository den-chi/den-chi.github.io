Circular on Issues Concerning Individual Income Tax on Enterprise Annuity and Occupational Annuity

Cai Shui [2013] No.103

December 6, 2013

The finance departments (bureaus), the departments (bureaus) of human resources and social security and the local taxation bureaus at all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government and cities specifically designated in the state plan, and the Financial Bureau and the bureau of human resources and social security of Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps,

In order to promote the development of multi-level endowment insurance system in our country and according to relevant provisions on Individual Income Tax Law, issues concerning individual income tax on enterprise annuity and occupational annuity are hereby notified in the Circular of Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security and State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning Individual Income Tax on Enterprise Annuity and Occupational Annuity (hereinafter referred to as the "Circular") as follows:

Article 1 Payment of individual income tax on enterprise annuity and occupational annuity

  1. For the part of enterprise annuity and occupational annuity (hereinafter referred to as the "annuities") paid by enterprises and public institutions (hereinafter referred to as the "entities") for their overall employees according to measures and standards stipulated in related national policies and regulations, no individual income tax is required to be paid by individuals temporarily when including the part into individuals' accounts.

  2. For the part of annuities paid by individuals according to relevant policies of the State that does not exceed 4% of the tax base of taxable salary, it shall be temporarily deducted from the current taxable income.

  3. For any part, either paid by entities or by individuals, exceeding the annuities required to be paid in Paragraph 1 and Paragraph 2 of Article 1 hereof, it shall be incorporated into current income from individual salary or wage and shall be subject to individual income tax in accordance with the law. The taxes concerned shall be withheld by the entities establishing the annuities, and shall be declared and paid to competent tax authorities.

  4. The tax base of individual salary contributed to enterprise annuity shall be the average monthly salary of the individual for the last year. The average monthly salary is calculated based on the items listed for computation of gross wage according to provisions of National Bureau of Statistics of China. For the part of average monthly salary in excess of 300% of the average monthly salary of the city with districts (where the employee works) for the last year, it shall not be included into the contribution base of individual salary.

The tax base of individual salary contributed to occupational annuity shall be the sum of employee's post wage and grade wage. For the part of the sum of employee's post salary and grade salary in excess of 300% of the average monthly salary of the city with districts (where the employee works) for the last year, it shall not be included into the contribution base of individual salary.

Article 2 Individual income tax in respect of investing and operating income from annuity fund

When the distribution of investing and operating income from annuity fund is recorded into individual's account, no individual income tax is required to be paid by the individual temporarily.

Article 3 Individual income tax when acquiring annuities

  1. When an individual reaches the retirement age regulated by the State, his or her annuity received on a monthly basis after the implementation of the Circular shall be fully subject to individual income tax at the tax rate applicable to the item of "wage and salary income"; and his or her annuity received on a year or quarterly basis after the implementation of the Circular shall be amortized evenly to each month and the amount received each month shall be in full subject to individual income tax at the tax rate applicable to the item of "wage and salary income".

  2. Where any entity or individual pays annuity before the implementation of the Circular and the individual receives the annuity after the implementation of the Circular, it shall be allowed to deduct the part of annuity paid by entity and individual before the implementation of the Circular and having been levied for individual income tax from the annuity that has been received by it. The balance after such deduction shall be subject to tax according to Paragraph 1 of Article 3 hereof. Where an individual receives annuity by stages, the taxable income for the current period may be written down at the proportion of the amount of annuity paid before the implementation of the Circular in the total amount that has been paid. The balance after such write-down shall be accounted for individual income tax according to the provision in Paragraph 1 of Article 3 hereof.

  3. For any individual account fund of annuity withdrawn on a lump sum basis by the individual for its settlement abroad, or any individual account balance of annuity withdrawn on a lump sum basis by the designated beneficiary or statutory successor of the individual due to its death, the receiver shall be allowed to amortized the individual account fund or balance of annuity withdrawn on a lump sum basis to 12 months and the amortized amount of each month shall be subject to individual income tax according to the provisions of Paragraph 1 and Paragraph 2 of Article 3 hereof. For any individual account fund or balance of annuity withdrawn on a lump sum basis for any other reasons, the above amortization method is not allowed; instead, the total amount received on a lump sum basis shall be subject to individual income tax as the wage and salary income for one month according to the provisions of Paragraph 1 and Paragraph 2 of Article 3 hereof.

  4. When an individual withdraws its annuity, its tax payable shall be withheld by the custodian entrusted by trustee on behalf of the trustor. The annuity account manager shall timely provide the custodian with a detailed statement on the payment of individual income tax relevant to annuity and corresponding individual income tax. The custodian shall, based on the materials specified by the trustee and provided by the account manager, calculate the tax payable that may be withheld from the current annuity attributable to the individual, and shall make declaration and payment to competent tax authorities where the custodian locates.

  5. An entity or any annuity custodian establishing annuity plan shall make declaration in respect of the payment on the basis of all personnel and full amount according to relevant provisions of Individual Income Tax Law and Law on the Administration of Tax Levying. The trustee shall be responsible for coordinating relevant officers to go through procedures of declaration and payment and provide relevant documents to tax authorities.

Article 4 The entity establishing annuity plan shall, within the first 15 days of the following month of establishing such plan, submit to competent tax authority where it locates the annuity plan, the letter of filling for the plan issued by department of human resources and social security, the plan confirmation letter and other relevant documents required by competent tax authority. Where there is any change in the annuity plan, the trustee and the custodian, the said documents shall be repeatedly submitted to its competent tax authority within the first 15 days of the month following such change.

Article 5 The finance, taxation, human resources and social security and other relevant departments as well as the annuity institutions shall strengthen their coordination and cooperation in order to well carry out various policies.

Article 6 For the purpose of the Circular, the term "enterprise annuity" refers to the supplementary pension insurance system voluntarily established by enterprises and their employees beyond the purchasing of basic endowment insurance, in accordance with the provisions of Tentative Measures for Enterprise Annuity (the original Order of the Ministry of Labor and Social Security No.20). The term "occupational annuity" refers to the supplementary pension insurance system established by public institutions and their staffs beyond the purchasing of a basic old-age pension, in accordance with the provision of Tentative Measures for Occupational Annuities of Public Institutions (Guo Ban Fa [2011] No.37).

Article 7 The Circular shall come into force as of January 1, 2014. Circular of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning the Imposition and Administration of Individual Income Tax on Enterprise Annuities (Guo Shui Han [2009] No.694) and the Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Supplementary Provisions on Issues Concerning Individual Income Tax on Enterprise Annuities (Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2011] No.9) shall be repealed simultaneously.
