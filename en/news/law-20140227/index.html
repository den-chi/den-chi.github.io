Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning Tax Rebate (Exemption) of Export of Goods of Foreign Trade Comprehensive Service Enterprise

2014-02-27

Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning Tax Rebate (Exemption) of Export of Goods of Foreign Trade Comprehensive Service Enterprise

Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation [2014] No.13

February 27, 2014

In order to further utilize the advantage of foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises in providing export services and to help small and medium-sized enterprises effectively explore the international market, upon consents of the Ministry of Finance and Ministry of Commerce, matters relating to tax rebate (exemption) of relevant export goods are hereby announced as follows:

Article 1 Where a foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise exports the export, in a self-supporting manner, the goods subject to the contracts entered into by and between domestic manufacturers and foreign companies or individuals and the following circumstances are met, the said foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise may declare tax rebate (exemption) according to the provisions on self-exports:

  1. the export goods are self-produced by the manufacturer;

  2. the manufacturer has sold the export goods to the foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise;

  3. the manufacturer has concluded and entered into export contracts with foreign companies or individuals, and agreed to export goods by the foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise to foreign companies or individuals, with the loans to be paid by foreign companies or individuals to the foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise; and

  4. the foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise exports goods in a self-supporting manner.

For the abovementioned export of goods, Item 3 of Article 2 of the Circular of the State Administration of Taxation and the Ministry of Commerce on Further Regulating the Order of Export Operation in Foreign Trade and Effectively Enhancing the Administration of Tax Rebate (Exemption) for Export of Goods (Guo Shui Fa [2006] No.24) and Item 7C, Paragraph 1 of Article 7 of the Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Value-added Tax and Consumption Tax Policies on Exported Goods and Services (Cai Shui [2012] No.39) do not apply.

Article 2 When declaring tax rebate (exemption) of export goods stipulated in Article 1 in the Announcement of the State Administration of Taxation on Issues Concerning Tax Rebate (Exemption) of Export of Goods of Foreign Trade Comprehensive Service Enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the "Announcement"), a foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise shall fill in Column 15 (business type) of the Declaration of Foreign Trade Enterprises for Detailed Report on Purchase of Goods for Export and Column 19 (business type subject to tax rebate (exemption)) of the Declaration of Foreign Trade Enterprises for Detailed Report on Tax Rebates for Exports with "WMZHFW".

Article 3 Foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises shall strengthen risk control and strictly review the operation and production capacity of foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises, so as to ensure the authenticity of the domestic purchase and export for the export tax rebate (exemption) declared. If there is any value-added tax deduction voucher made out falsely (including accepting the value-added tax deduction voucher that is falsely made out, except those accepted in bona fide the voucher for good), or any tax-related violations concerning gaining export tax rebate by cheating, the said enterprise shall be treated as the principal subject to liability in accordance with provisions.

Article 4 Tax authorities shall accept and handle the declaration for tax rebate (exemption) of foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises in compliance with provisions, and strengthen the alarm monitoring, examination, evaluation and analysis of foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises. Where there is any doubt about cheating tax rebate, it shall be handled according to prevailing provisions.

Article 5 Foreign trade comprehensive service enterprises in the Announcement refer to the foreign trade enterprises which provide services of logistics, customs clearance, credit and guarantee, financing, exchange, tax rebate and others.

Article 6 The Announcement shall come into force as of April 1, 2014. Where a foreign trade comprehensive service enterprise exports goods beyond the scope as set forth in Article 1, the prevailing provision for tax rebate (exemption) shall be followed.

It is hereby announced.
