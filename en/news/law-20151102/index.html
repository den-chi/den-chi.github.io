Circular on Improving the Policy on Extra Pre-tax Deduction of Research and Development Expenses

2015-11-02

Cai Shui [2015] No.119

November 2, 2015

The finance departments (bureaus), local offices of the State Administration of Taxation, local taxation bureaus and science and technology departments (bureaus) of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government and cities specifically designated in the state plan and the Financial Bureau and the Science and Technology Bureau of Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps,

In accordance with the relevant provisions of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise Income Tax and its implementing regulations, and in order to further implement the spirit of the Several Opinions of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China and the State Council on Promoting Further Systemic and Institutional Reforms to Accelerate the Implementation of Innovation-driven Development Strategies, better encourage enterprises to carry out research and development activities (hereinafter referred to as the "research and development activities") and standardize the implementation of the preferential policy on the extra deduction of research and development expenses (hereinafter referred to as the "research and development expenses") of enterprises, issues concerning the extra pre-tax deduction of research and development expenses of enterprises are hereby notified as follows:

I. Research and Development Activities and the Collection Scope of Research and Development Expenses

For the purpose of the Circular on Improving the Policy on Extra Pre-tax Deduction of Research and Development Expenses (hereinafter referred to as the "Circular"), the research and development activities refer to the systematic activities with specific goals constantly carried out by enterprises in order to gain and creatively apply new knowledge of science and technology, or materially improve technologies, products (services) and techniques.

(I) The research and development expenses entitled to extra deduction.

Where the research and development expenses that are actually incurred in the research and development activities of enterprises and do not constitute intangible assets are recorded into the current profit or loss, such expenses shall be deducted from the taxable income for the current year at 50% of the actual amount incurred in the current year and on an actual basis as required; if intangible assets are constituted, such expenses shall be amortized at 150% of the costs of the intangible assets before tax. The specific scope of research and development expenses includes:

  1. Labor costs.

Wages and salaries, basic endowment insurance premiums, basic medical insurance premiums, unemployment insurance premiums, work-related injury insurance premiums, maternity insurance premiums and housing provident funds of personnel directly engaged in research and development activities, as well as labor costs for external research and development personnel.

  1. Direct investment expenses.

(1) Expenses for materials, fuel and power directly consumed in research and development activities.

(2) Expenses for moulds used in interim tests and trial production, technique equipment development and manufacturing, expenses for purchase of samples, model machines and general testing means which do not constitute fixed assets and inspection fees for trial products.

(3) Expenses for operating maintenance, adjustment, inspection and repair of instruments and equipment used for research and development activities, as well as lease expenses for instruments and equipment leased under operating lease and used for research and development activities.

  1. Depreciation expenses.

Depreciation expenses for instruments and equipment used for research and development activities.

  1. Amortization of intangible assets.

Amortization expenses for software, patent rights, non-patented technologies (including licenses, proprietary technologies, design, and calculation methods) used for research and development activities.

  1. Design fees for new products, fess for formulation of procedures of new techniques, clinical test expenses for new drug development and field trial expenses for the exploration and development of technologies.

  2. Other related expenses.

Other expenses directly related to research and development activities, such as expenses for technology books and materials, fees for translation of materials, expert advice fees, insurance premiums of the high-tech research and development, expenses for retrieval, analysis, discussion, argument, verification, review, assessment and acceptance of research and development achievements, fees for application, registration and agency of intellectual property, travel expenses, and conference expenses. The total amount of these expenses shall not exceed 10% of the total research and development expenses subject to extra deduction.

  1. Other expenses specified by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation.

(II) The policy on the extra pre-tax deduction does not apply to the following activities.

  1. Conventional upgrade of enterprises' products (services).

  2. The direct application of a certain research and development achievement, such as direct adoption of public new techniques, materials, equipment, products, services or knowledge.

  3. Technical supports provided by enterprises for customers after commercialization.

  4. Repeated or simple modifications to existing products, services, technologies, materials or processes.

  5. Market research, efficiency investigation or management research.

  6. Quality control, test and analysis, and repair and maintenance as the industrial (service) process links or routine quality control, test and analysis, and repair and maintenance.

  7. Research on social science, arts or humanities.

II. Treatment of Special Matters

  1. The expenses arising from the research and development activities conducted by an external agency or individual that is entrusted by an enterprise shall be included in the research and development expenses of the enterprise at 80% of the actual amount incurred and be calculated for extra deduction, and the entrusted party shall not apply the extra deduction again. The actual amount of external research and development expenses shall be determined on the basis of the arm's length principle.

Where there is a related-party relationship between the entrusting party and the entrusted party, the entrusted party shall provide the entrusting party with details of expenditures of the research and development project.

Expenses incurred in research and development activities that are conducted by overseas agencies or individuals entrusted by enterprises shall not be subject to extra deduction.

  1. For a project jointly developed by enterprises, all partners shall calculate the respective research and development expenses they actually undertake for extra deduction.

  2. Enterprise groups may rationally determine the apportion method for the research and development expenses actually incurred in a project with high technical requirements and large investments and requiring concentrated research and development on the basis of the actual conditions of production and management and scientific and technological development and according to the principle that rights are in line with obligations and the cost expenditures match with the revenue sharing, and carry out apportion of the said research and development expenses among beneficial member enterprises, and relevant beneficial member enterprises shall calculate their respective expenses for extra deduction.

  3. Relevant expenses incurred in the creative design activities carried out by enterprises for obtaining innovative, creative and breakthrough products may be subject to extra pre-tax deduction in accordance with provisions of the Circular.

The creative design activities refer to the development of multimedia software and anime gaming software and design and production of digital anime and games; building construction engineering design (the evaluation standard for green buildings is three stars) and special design for landscape architecture projects; industrial design, multimedia design, anime and its derivatives design, and model design.

III. Accounting and Management

  1. Enterprises shall carry out accounting treatment of the research and development expenditures in accordance with the requirements of national financial and accounting systems, and meanwhile shall set assistant accounts for the research and development expenses enjoying the extra deduction policy by research and development projects, and exactly and collectively account for actual amounts of various research and development expenses subject to extra deduction in the current year. Where an enterprise conducts several research and development activities within one tax year, it shall respectively integrate the research and development expenses subject to extra deduction according to different research and development projects.

  2. Enterprises shall respectively account for research and development expenses and production and management expenses and integrate various expenditures exactly and reasonably and shall not implement the extra deduction policy for those with unclear division.

IV. Industries to Which the Policy on Extra Pre-tax Deduction does not Apply

  1. The tobacco manufacturing.

  2. The accommodation and catering industry.

  3. The wholesale and retail industry.

  4. The real estate industry.

  5. The leasing and business service industry.

  6. The entertainment industry.

  7. Other industries specified by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation.

The above industries shall be subject to the Industrial Classification and Codes for National Economic Activities (GB/4754-2011) and shall be updated accordingly.

V. Administration Matters and Collection and Administration Requirements

  1. The Circular applies to resident enterprises that have perfect financial accounting systems, pay tax on an actual profit basis and are able to exactly integrate research and development expenses.

  2. Where the integration of the actual amount of research and development expenses for each project is inaccurate and so is the calculation of the total amount, the tax authorities are entitled to reasonably adjust the pre-tax deductions or the extra deductions.

  3. Where the tax authorities lodge objections to the research and development projects for which an enterprise enjoys the preferential policy on extra deduction, the tax authorities may ask the competent administrative department of science and technology at or above the prefecture level to provide the expert opinion, and the said department of science and technology shall reply with the opinion in time. For the scientific research projects at or above the provincial or ministerial level undertaken by enterprises and the multi-year scientific research projects that have been appraised in prior years, no appraisal is required any longer.

  4. Where enterprises that conform to the extra deduction conditions for research and development expenses stipulated by the Circular fail to enjoy such tax preference after January 1, 2016 in a timely manner, then they may retroactively enjoy the same and go through the filing procedures, provided that the retroactive period shall be three years at most.

  5. The tax authorities shall enhance the follow-up management of the preferential policy on the extra deduction of research and development expenses and carry out regular inspection, with the annual inspection rate being not less than 20%.

VI. Implementation Time

The Circular shall take effect as of January 1, 2016. The Circular of the State Administration of Taxation on Issuing the Administrative Measures for the Pre-tax Deduction of Enterprises' Expenditures for Research and Development (for Trial Implementation) (Guo Shui Fa [2008] No.116) and the Circular of the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation on Policy Issues concerning Extra Pre-Tax Deduction of Research and Development Expenses (Cai Shui [2013] No.70) shall be repealed simultaneously.
