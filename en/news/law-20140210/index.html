Circular of the State Administration of Foreign Exchange on Further Improving and Adjusting the Policy for Foreign Exchange Control of Capital Accounts

2014-02-10

Circular of the State Administration of Foreign Exchange on Further Improving and Adjusting the Policy for Foreign Exchange Control of Capital Accounts

Hui Fa [2014] No. 2

January 10, 2014

The branches and foreign exchange control departments of the State Administration of Foreign Exchange in all provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government, and the cities of Shenzhen, Dalian, Qingdao, Xiamen and Ningbo, and all the Chinese-funded banks,

To further deepen the reform of foreign exchange control of capital accounts, simplify administrative examination and approval procedures, and promote the facilitation of trade and investment, according to the Administrative Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Foreign Exchange, the State Administration of Foreign Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the "SAFE") decides to further improve the method of foreign exchange control of capital accounts, and adjusts some measures for the foreign exchange control of capital accounts. Relevant issues are hereby notified as below:

Article 1 Simplifying foreign exchange control over external debts of finance lease companies

  1. Finance lease companies include three types of subjects, such as the finance lease companies established upon approval of the banking regulatory department, the foreign-invested leasing companies established upon examination and approval of the competent commercial department, as well as Chinese-invested finance lease companies jointly confirmed by the Ministry of Commerce and the State Administration of Taxation (hereinafter referred to as "finance lease companies").

  2. When a finance lease company or its project company carries out the external finance lease business, it shall, within 15 working days upon occurrence of external debts from finance lease, register with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where it is located by presenting the following materials, and such foreign exchange bureau shall review the compliance and authenticity of the transaction:

(1) application form, including but not limited to the basic information of the company and of the leasing project;

(2) the official reply of the competent department to approve the establishment of the finance lease company or project company and its business license;

(3) the audited financial report of the previous year and the financial statement of the last period; and

(4) the leasing contract and the materials certifying the transfer of the leased property (such as customs declaration documents, filing checklist, invoices, etc.).

  1. When a finance lease company carries out the external finance lease business, it is not subject to the current limit for the overseas lending quotas of domestic enterprises.

  2. A finance lease company may directly open a special account for overseas lending with the bank at the place where it is located for holding the incomes from external finance lease.

When the above foreign exchange fund is paid in the account, the bank shall review the fund source of such income. When the foreign exchange income in the account needs to be settled, the finance lease company may directly apply to the bank for going through the settlement.

  1. The foreign exchange bureau at the place where the company is located shall use the function of "Overseas Lending" in the capital project information system to register the contracting information on external claims for finance lease of finance lease companies and carry on the statistics of withdrawal information through reports and statements in paper.

When a finance lease company receives the rent income from external finance lease, it shall declare the same according to the declaration requirements relating to balance of payments, fill in the business number of such external claim in the column "Approval Document Number of the Foreign Exchange Bureau/Filing Table Number/Business Number", and shall monthly submit such information as the occurrence of the external claim for finance lease and the rent income to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where it is located. The bank shall, via the capital project information system, provide feedback on the rent income from external finance lease and other information. It shall gather relevant information according to new requirements after improving relevant module functions of the capital project information system.

Article 2 Simplifying foreign exchange control over domestic non-performing assets transferred to overseas investors

  1. The pre-administration of the SAFE over the approval of foreign exchange receipts and payments and remittance involved in the disposal of non-performing assets by finance lease companies is cancelled.

  2. The registration formalities for domestic non-performing assets transferred to overseas investors are simplified. Within 30 days after the relevant competent department approves a domestic institution to transfer its non-performing asset to an overseas investors, the overseas investor to which the domestic non-performing assets are transferred or the agent thereof shall, by presenting the following materials, go through the registration formalities for the domestic non-performing assets transferred to the overseas investor with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the main assets are located or the agent is located.

(1) Application form, and filling in the Registration Form for Domestic Non-performing Assets Transferred to Overseas Investors (see Appendix hereto);

(2) Approval or filing document showing that the relevant competent department approves the domestic institution to transfer non-performing assets overseas;

(3) The photocopy of the main clauses and terms of the transfer contract signed between the domestic institution and the overseas investor (without providing the data on transaction by transaction basis about the non-performing assets and guarantee matters);

(4) If the case is handled by the domestic agent, the agent agreement still needs to be provided; and

(5) Supplementary materials that need to be provided for the above materials.

  1. The approval of a foreign exchange bureau over the foreign exchange settlement of the income from non-performing assets disposed by a finance asset management company is cancelled, and a bank shall directly handle the formalities for foreign exchange booking or settlement.

After the domestic institution transferring non-performing assets receives the consideration from the overseas investor, such institution may, by presenting the following materials, directly apply to the bank for opening a foreign exchange account for holding foreign exchange incomes, or apply for the foreign exchange settlement of the foreign exchange income from the non-performing assets.

(1) Application form;

(2) The Agreement Handling Certificate (photocopy) for the capital project information system obtained at the time of transferring the non-performing assets to the overseas investor;

(3) The photocopy of the main clauses and terms of the claim transfer contract; and

(4) Supplementary materials that need to be provided for the above materials.

When the domestic institution opens a foreign exchange account for holding foreign exchange incomes, or handles the formalities for the foreign exchange settlement of foreign exchange incomes from the non-performing assets, it shall make a declaration according to the declaration requirements relating to the balance of payments, foreign exchange accounts, and foreign exchange settlement, and fill in the relevant business number for the registration of domestic non-performing assets transferred to the overseas investor in the column "Approval Document Number of the Foreign Exchange Bureau/Filing Table Number/Business Number".

  1. When the ownership of the assets registered by an overseas investor is changed or lost due to repurchase, sale (transfer), recovery, share transfer or other reason, the overseas investor or the agent thereof shall, within 30 working days after the change or loss of the ownership, go through the formalities for changing or cancelling the registration of domestic non-performing assets transferred to the overseas investor with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the assets register.

  2. The approval of a foreign exchange bureau over the foreign exchange payment for the proceeds from non-performing assets disposed by an overseas investor is cancelled, and handled by the bank.

With regard to proceeds obtained, through recovery, re-transfer or other means, by an overseas investor to which domestic non-performing assets are transferred, such investor may go through the formalities for external foreign exchange purchase and remittance with the bank by presenting the following materials.

(1) Application form;

(2) The Agreement Handling Certificate for the capital project information system;

(3) The photocopy of the Registration Form for Domestic Non-performing Assets Transferred to Overseas Investors;

(4) The document certifying the source of the proceeds from the non-performing assets disposed;

(5) If the case is handled by the domestic agent, the agent agreement still needs to be provided; and

(6) Supplementary materials that need to be provided for the above materials.

When an overseas investor goes through the formalities for external foreign exchange payment, such investor shall declare the same according to the relevant declaration requirements for balance of payments, and fill in the business number of the registration of domestic non-performing assets transferred to the overseas investor in the column "Approval Document Number of the Foreign Exchange Bureau/Filing Table Number/Business Number".

  1. The bank shall diligently review the business numbers of the registration of domestic non-performing assets transferred to overseas investors filled in when the bank opens foreign exchange accounts for holding foreign exchange incomes or handles foreign exchange settlement for the foreign exchange incomes from non-performing assets or when overseas investors go through the formalities for external foreign exchange purchase.

  2. Where the beneficiary of the original guarantee is change to an overseas investor due to the transfer of domestic non-performing assets to the overseas investor, such guarantee is not subject to the administration of external guarantee.

External guarantee occurred after the transfer of domestic non-performing assets to overseas investors is administered according to the current provisions on foreign exchange control over external guarantee.

Article 3 Further relaxing the administration of pre-stage expenses on foreign direct investment from domestic institutions

  1. The accumulated remittance amount of pre-stage expenses for foreign direct investment (hereinafter referred to as "pre-stage expenses") neither exceeds USD3 million nor 15% of the total investment amount of the Chinese party, the domestic institution may, by presenting its business license and organization code certificate, go through the formalities for registration of pre-stage expenses with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the institution is located.

  2. Where the accumulated remittance amount of pre-stage expenses exceeds USD3 million or 15% of the total investment amount of the Chinese party, the institution shall, in addition to submitting its business license and organization code certificate, provide to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the institution is located the written application and the documents certifying the relevant authenticity of the bidding, merger and acquisition, or joint venture project that the institution participates in, as submitted to the department in charge of foreign direct investment, to go through the formalities for registration of pre-stage expenses.

  3. Where a domestic institution does not obtain the approval or filing of the department in charge of foreign direct investment within six months after the remittance of pre-stage expenses, the institution shall report the use of pre-stage expenses to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the institution is located and return the remainder of the fund. If there is any objective reason, the domestic institution may apply to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the institution is located for extension of not more than 12 months.

Article 4 Further relaxing the administration of overseas lending from domestic enterprises

  1. The restrictions on the subjects of overseas lending from domestic enterprises are relaxed. Domestic enterprises are permitted to grant lending to the overseas enterprises that have equity relationship with such domestic enterprises. Domestic enterprises go through the registration of overseas lending amounts with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where they are located by presenting the overseas lending agreement and the financial audit report of the previous period, and the accumulated overseas lending amount of such domestic enterprises shall not exceed 30% of their owners' equity. If it is truly need to exceed the above proportion, the foreign exchange sub-bureau (foreign exchange control department) at the place where the domestic enterprises are located handles the cases by collective discussion.

  2. The restrictions on the two-year valid period of overseas lending amount are cancelled. Domestic enterprises may, according to the actual business needs, apply to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where they are located for the period of overseas lending amount.

  3. Where the principal and interest on overseas lending cannot be recovered due to objective reasons, the domestic enterprise may apply to the foreign exchange sub-bureau (foreign exchange control department) at the place where the domestic enterprise is located for cancelling such overseas lending, and the foreign exchange sub-bureau (foreign exchange control department) at the place where the domestic enterprise is located handles the case by collective discussion. After the principle and interest on overseas lending (including debt-to-equity swap, debt exemption, and performance security) are paid or overseas lending is cancelled, and overseas lending is no longer granted, the domestic enterprise may apply to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where it is located for going through the cancellation of overseas lending amount.

Article 5 Simplifying the administration of profit remittance of domestic institutions

  1. When handling the remittance of the profit equivalent to not more than USD50,000 for a domestic institution, it may no longer review the transaction documents in principle; when handling the remittance of the profit equivalent to more than USD50,000, the bank may no longer review the financial audit reports and capital verification report of such institution in principle, and shall review the profit distribution resolution (or partner profit distribution resolution) of the board of directors relating to the profit remittance and the original copy of the tax filing form thereof according to the principle of authentic transaction. After the remittance of each profit, the bank shall indicate the actual remittance amount and remittance date of such profit in the original copy of relevant tax filing form.

  2. The restriction that the amount of the profit disposed by an enterprise in the current year shall not exceed the sum of "payable dividends" and "undistributed profits" of the foreign party under the financial audit report of the previous period in principle is cancelled.

Article 6 Simplifying the administration of foreign exchange sale and payment of personal property

  1. The approval of foreign exchange purchase and remittance for the property transfer by an immigrant is subject to the examination and approval of the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the household register of such immigrant is located. The requirement that the total amount of property transferred equivalent to more than CNY500,000 shall be reported to the SAFE is cancelled.

  2. The requirement that the property transferred by an immigrant shall be remitted on multiple occasions is cancelled. After the applicant goes through the approval formalities for transferring the property of the immigrant with the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the original householder register of such applicant is located, the bank may remit relevant funds on one occasion or multiple occasions within the amount examined and approved in the approval document.

  3. The requirement that the property inherited by an heir from different ancestors shall be subject to separate application and remittance is cancelled. Where an heir inherits the property from different ancestors, the heir may choose the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the householder register of one of the ancestors is located to submit application materials, and remit relevant funds at the bank on one occasion or multiple occasions upon approval.

  4. The requirement that the relevant property right documents (such as house ownership certificate, real estate trading contract or removal, compensation and relocation agreement, work or lease contract or agreement, property transfer agreement or contract, and franchising agreement or contract) shall be notarized is cancelled; and the requirement that an authorized agency agreement or agent identity certificate shall be notarized is cancelled.

Article 7 Improving the administration of the Foreign Exchange Business Licenses in Securities Operations of securities companies

To operate the foreign exchange business, the securities company shall obtain the Foreign Exchange Business License in Securities Operations (hereinafter referred to as the "License") from the SAFE according to relevant provisions. Except that a securities company needs to apply for replacing the License in a timely manner according to relevant provisions due to such circumstances as the change of company name and the adjustment to the scope of the foreign exchange business, the securities company does not need to replace the License regularly from the date of implementation of this Circular.

Any securities company that has obtained the License to operate the foreign exchange business shall, prior to January 31 each year, submit the written report (the content of which includes specific situation of the foreign exchange business operated by the company, the type of the foreign exchange business, foreign exchange purchase and settlement and inward and outward remittance of funds, compliance of the foreign exchange business and relevant balance sheet of the foreign exchange business, etc.) on the operation of the foreign exchange business of the previous year to the foreign exchange bureau at the place where the securities company is located.

This Circular becomes effective as of February 10, 2014. In the event of any discrepancy between the previous provisions and this Circular, this Circular prevails. All the sub-bureaus and foreign exchange control departments are requested to forward this Circular to the central sub-branches and branches and the banks within their respective jurisdiction as soon as possible; and all Chinese-funded banks shall forward this Circular to their branches as soon as possible. Any problems identified during the implementation shall be reported to the Capital Project Administrative Department of the SAFE in a timely manner.
