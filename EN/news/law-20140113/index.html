Circular of the State Administration of Taxation on Implementing the Decision of the State Council on Cancellation and Decentralization of a Batch of Administrative Examination and Approval Items

Shui Zong Fa [2014] No. 6

January 13, 2014

The offices of the State Administration of Taxation and local tax bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities separately listed in the State plan, and the departments thereof:

The State Council released the Decision of the State Council on Cancellation and Decentralization of a Batch of Administrative Examination and Approval Items (Guo Fa [2013] No. 44, hereinafter referred to as the "Decision") on November 8, 2013, announcing the cancellation and delegation of seven tax administrative examination and approval items.

All the departments shall diligently implement the Decision, properly implement and coordinate the cancellation and delegation of tax administrative examination and approval items and speed up formulating follow-up administrative measures to attain the combination of delegation and administration. With regard to a project for which the follow-up administrative measures are not required to be released, such departments shall strengthen guidance and trace administration to guarantee the correct application of the basic-level tax authorities and taxpayers. The departments shall supervise and inspect the implementation of cancellation and delegation of tax administrative examination and approval items to prevent disguised examination and approval or seeming delegation. They shall continue to promote reform of the administrative examination and approval system, make public the catalogue of tax administrative examination and approval items, continue to promote the centralized acceptance, internal circulation and time-limited completion at the taxpayer service hall for administrative examination and approval items, push forward online examination and approval, implement the "sunshine" examination and approval, strengthen supervision over the administrative examination and approval power, strive to create a taxation environment that is unified, orderly and under fair competition, and increasingly improve the level of scientific and law-based tax administration.
