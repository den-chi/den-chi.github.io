Interim Provisions on Labor Dispatch -- Order of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security of the People's Republic of China No. 22

2014-01-24

Interim Provisions on Labor Dispatch

Order of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security of the People's Republic of China No. 22

January 24, 2014

The Interim Provisions on Labor Dispatch, considered and adopted at the 21st ministerial affairs meeting of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security on December 20, 2013, is hereby promulgated and shall come into effect on March 1, 2014.

Minister of the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Security: Yin Weimin

Interim Provisions on Labor Dispatch

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 For the purpose of regulating the Labor Dispatch, protecting the legitimate rights and interests of laborers, and developing a stable and harmonious labor relationship, these Provisions are formulated in accordance with the Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Labor Contract Law"), the Implementing Regulations of the Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Implementing Regulations of the Labor Contract Law") and other relevant laws and administrative regulations.

Article 2 These Provisions shall apply to the operation of Labor Dispatch business by Labor Dispatch entities and to the use of dispatched laborers by enterprises (hereinafter referred to as the "employer").

These Provisions shall apply to legally-established partnership organizations, such as accounting firms and law firms, foundations, private non-enterprise entities and other organizations in their use of dispatched laborers.

Chapter II Scope and Proportion of Labor Using

Article 3 Employers may use dispatched laborers only for temporary, auxiliary or substitutable positions.

For the purpose of the preceding Paragraph, temporary positions shall mean positions lasting for no more than six months; auxiliary positions shall mean positions of non-major business that serve positions of major business; and substitutable positions shall mean positions that can be held by substitute laborers for a certain period of time during which the laborers of the employers who originally hold such positions are unable to work as a result of full-time study, being on leave or other reasons.

An employer shall put forward proposals and suggestions after a discussion by the workers' general meeting or by all workers, conduct equal consultations with its labor union or workers' representatives to decide the auxiliary positions for which dispatched laborers are to be used, and announce the decision thereon internally.

Article 4 An employer shall strictly control the number of dispatched laborers which shall not exceed 10% of the total number of its workers.

For the purpose of the preceding Paragraph, the total number of workers shall mean the aggregate of the number of laborers who have signed labor contracts with the employer and the number of dispatched laborers used by the employer.

An employer that calculates the proportion of the dispatched laborers used shall mean an employer that may enter into labor contracts with laborers in accordance with the Labor Contract Law and the Implementing Regulations of the Labor Contract Law.

Chapter III Execution and Performance of Labor Contracts and Labor Dispatch Agreements

Article 5 A Labor Dispatch entity shall enter into labor contracts in writing with dispatched laborers for a fixed-term of at least two years in accordance with the law.

Article 6 A Labor Dispatch entity may agree on a probation period with a dispatched laborer in accordance with the law. Nevertheless, the labor dispatch entity shall only agree on one probation period with the same dispatched laborer.

Article 7 A Labor Dispatch agreement shall specify the following contents:

  1. the title and nature of the positions for which laborers are to be dispatched;

  2. the place of work;

  3. the number of dispatched laborers and the period of Labor Dispatch;

  4. the amount of labor remunerations as determined under the principle of equal pay for equal work and the method of remuneration payment;

  5. amount of social insurance premiums and the method of payment;

  6. working hours and matters concerning rest and leave;

  7. benefits for dispatched laborers in relation to work-related injuries, maternity or illnesses;

  8. matters concerning labor safety, health and job training;

  9. economic compensation and other expenses;

  10. the period of the Labor Dispatch agreement;

  11. methods and standards of payment for Labor Dispatch service fees;

  12. liabilities for breach of the Labor Dispatch agreement; and

  13. other matters that shall be included in the Labor Dispatch agreement as required by laws, regulations and rules.

Article 8 A Labor Dispatch entity shall perform the following obligations towards dispatched laborers:

  1. give true information to the dispatched laborers on matters prescribed in Article 8 of the Labor Contract Law, the rules and regulations to be observed and the contents of the relevant Labor Dispatch agreements;

  2. establish a training system to provide training and education for the dispatched laborers on job knowledge and safety;

  3. pay labor remunerations and the relevant benefits to the dispatched laborers in accordance with the law as required by State provisions and as agreed upon in the labor dispatch agreements;

  4. contribute social insurance premiums for the dispatched laborers in accordance with the law and go through the relevant formalities in relation to social insurance, as required by State provisions and as agreed upon in the labor dispatch agreements;

  5. supervise and urge employers to provide the dispatched laborers with labor protection, labor safety and health conditions in accordance with the law;

  6. issue certificates concerning the rescission or termination of labor contracts in accordance with the law;

  7. provide assistance in dealing with disputes between the dispatched laborers and their employers; and

  8. other matters required by laws, regulations and rules.

Article 9 An employer shall, according to Article 62 of the Labor Contract Law, provide dispatched laborers with benefits in relation to their positions, and shall not discriminate against dispatched laborers.

(Relevant articles: Q & A 1)

Article 10 A Labor Dispatch entity shall, in accordance with the law, apply for the determination of work-related injuries for dispatched laborers who have suffered accidents due to work with their employers, and the employers shall provide assistance in the investigation and verification for the determination of work-related injuries. The Labor Dispatch entity shall bear the liability for work-related injury insurance, provided that it may agree with the employers on the measures for compensation.

Where a dispatched laborer applies for the diagnosis or identification of occupational diseases, the employer concerned shall be responsible for handing matters concerning such diagnosis or identification, and provide true materials as necessary for the diagnosis or identification of occupational diseases, such as the occupational history of the laborer, his/her history of exposure to occupational hazards, the testing results of occupational hazard factors in the workplace, etc.; The Labor Dispatch entity concerned shall provide other materials as necessary for the diagnosis or identification of occupational diseases of the dispatched laborer.

Article 11 Where the administrative licensing obtained by a Labor Dispatch entity expires without renewal or where its Labor Dispatch Business Permit is withdrawn or revoked, the labor contracts it has lawfully executed with dispatched laborers shall be performed until the expiry thereof. The parties may rescind the labor contracts upon mutual agreement after consultations.

Article 12 An employer may send a dispatched laborer back to the Labor Dispatch entity concerned under any of the following circumstances:

  1. where the employer falls under any of the situations stated in Item 3 of Article 40 or in Article 41 of the Labor Contract Law;

  2. where the employer is declared bankrupt, has its business license revoked, or is ordered to be closed down or cancelled in accordance with the law, or where the employer decides to dissolve in advance, or discontinues operations upon the expiry of its operation period; or

  3. where the relevant Labor Dispatch agreement is terminated upon expiry.

During the period when the dispatched laborer who is sent back has no work to do, the Labor Dispatch entity shall pay remunerations on a monthly basis to the dispatched laborer in an amount not lower than the minimum wage required by the people's government at the domicile of the Labor Dispatch entity.

(Relevant articles:)

Article 13 If a dispatched laborer falls under any of the situations stated in Article 42 of the Labor Contract Law, the employer concerned shall not apply the provisions in Item 1 of Paragraph 1 of Article 12 hereof to send the dispatched laborer back to the relevant Labor Dispatch entity prior to the expiry of the period for Labor Dispatch. Upon expiry of the period for labor dispatch, the dispatched laborer shall not be sent back until the relevant situations disappear.

Chapter IV Rescission and Termination of Labor Contracts

Article 14 A dispatched laborer may rescind the labor contract by giving a written notice to the Labor Dispatch entity concerned 30 days in advance. A dispatched laborer may rescind the labor contract by giving a written notice to the labor dispatch entity concerned three days in advance within the probation period. The Labor Dispatch entity shall inform the relevant employer in a timely manner of the notice given by the dispatched laborer to rescind the labor contract.

Article 15 A Labor Dispatch entity may rescind the labor contract with a dispatched laborer who is sent back by the relevant employer under Article 12 hereof and who then raises objections to the new Labor Dispatch arrangements made by the Labor Dispatch entity under conditions that are the same as or higher than those agreed in the labor contract.

The Labor Dispatch entity shall not rescind the labor contract with a dispatched laborer who is sent back by the relevant employer under Article 12 hereof and who then raises objections to the new Labor Dispatch arrangements by the Labor Dispatch entity under conditions that are lower than those agreed in the labor contract, except where the dispatched laborer proposes to rescind the labor contract on his/her own accord.

Article 16 If a Labor Dispatch entity is declared bankrupt, has its business license revoked, or is ordered to be closed down or cancelled in accordance with the law, or if it decides to dissolve in advance or discontinues operations upon the expiry of the operation period, the labor contracts concluded thereby shall be terminated. The relevant employers shall consult with the Labor Dispatch entity on the proper placement of the relevant dispatched laborers.

Article 17 If a Labor Dispatch entity rescinds or terminates the labor contract with a dispatched laborer under any situation stated in Article 46 of the Labor Contract Law or Article 15 or Article16 hereof, it shall pay economic compensations to the dispatched laborer in accordance with the law.

Chapter V Social Insurance for Cross-regional Labor Dispatch

Article 18 If a Labor Dispatch entity dispatches a laborer to work in another region, it shall purchase social insurance for the dispatched laborer in the region where the employer is located, and contribute social insurance premiums in accordance with the requirements of the region where the employer is located. The dispatched laborer shall enjoy social insurance benefits according to State provisions.

Article 19 If a Labor Dispatch entity has established a branch in the region where the relevant employer is located, the branch shall handle the formalities to effect social insurance for the dispatched laborer and contribute social insurance premiums.

If the Labor Dispatch entity has not established any branch in the region where the employer is located, the employer shall, on behalf of the labor dispatch entity, handle the formalities to effect social insurance for the dispatched laborer and contribute social insurance premiums.

Chapter VI Legal Liability

Article 20 The provisions of Article 92 of the Labor Contract Law shall apply where a Labor Dispatch entity or an employer violates the provisions on labor dispatch under the Labor Contract Law or the Implementing Regulations of the Labor Contract Law.

Article 21 The provisions of Article 48 and Article 87 of the Labor Contract Law shall apply where a Labor Dispatch entity rescinds or terminates the labor contract with a dispatched laborer in violation of the provisions hereof.

Article 22 Where an employer violates Paragraph 3 of Article 3 hereof, the competent department of human resources and social security shall order the same to correct its activities and give a warning thereto, and the employer shall be liable for compensation in accordance with the law if such violation has caused damage to dispatched laborers.

Article 23 The provisions of Article 83 of the Labor Contract Law shall apply where a Labor Dispatch entity violates the provisions of Article 6 hereof.

Article 24 The provisions of Paragraph 2 of Article 92 of the Labor Contract Law shall apply where an employer sends back dispatched laborers in violation of the provisions hereof.

Chapter VII Supplementary Provisions

Article 25 Neither the use of dispatched laborers by resident representative offices of foreign enterprises and representative offices of foreign financial institutions in Mainland China, nor the use of international ocean seamen by seamen employers in the form of Labor Dispatch shall be subject to the provisions concerning temporary, auxiliary or substitutable positions and the provisions on the proportion of dispatched laborers used.

Article 26 The dispatching by an employer of its laborers to work outside Mainland China or to provide labor services for families or individuals shall not fall within the scope of Labor Dispatch as specified herein.

Article 27 Where an employer uses laborers in the form of Labor Dispatch under the name of hired work, outsourcing, etc., the provisions hereof shall apply.

Article 28 Where the number of dispatched laborers used by an employer uses prior to the implementation of these Provisions exceeds 10% of its total number of workers, the employer shall make a plan for the adjustment of such labor using, and reduce the said percentage to the required proportion within two years from the date of implementation hereof. However, if any labor contract or Labor Dispatch agreement legally executed prior to the promulgation of the Decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress on the Revision of the Labor Contract Law of the People's Republic of China will expire within two years after the date of implementation hereof, such contracts or agreements may continue to be performed until the expiry thereof in accordance with the law.

The employer shall submit the plan for the adjustment of labor using made by it to the local department of human resources and social security for record-filing.

The employer shall not use any additional dispatched laborers until it reduces the number of dispatched laborers used by it prior to the implementation hereof to the required proportion.

Article 29 These Provisions shall come into effect on March 1, 2014.
