Announcement on Issues Concerning Cross-border RMB Direct Investment(Announcement of the Ministry of Commerce [2013] No.87)

2013-12-03

Announcement of the Ministry of Commerce [2013] No.87-- Announcement on Issues Concerning Cross-border RMB Direct Investment

Announcement of the Ministry of Commerce [2013] No.87

December 3, 2013

For the purpose of promoting the facilitation of the cross-border RMB direct investment and improving the supervision measures, issues concerning cross-border RMB direct investment are hereby announced as follows:

Article 1 "Cross-border RMB direct investment" in the Announcement of the Ministry of Commerce [2013] No.87-- Announcement on Issues Concerning Cross-border RMB Direct Investment (hereinafter referred to as the "Announcement") shall refer to the direct investment activities carried out by foreign investors (including investors from Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan Region, also below) in China with RMB legally obtained overseas in accordance with the law including establishment of new enterprises, capital increase, participation or merger of domestic enterprises. Foreign investors shall go through relevant formalities to the cross-border RMB direct investment in accordance with the current laws, administrative regulations, rules and relevant policies of the State on foreign investment.

Article 2 The cross-border RMB direct investment and the reinvestment of the invested foreign-funded enterprises shall conform to the requirements of laws, regulations and relevant provisions on foreign investment and abide by relevant provisions of the industrial policy, safety review of foreign capital merger and acquisition and anti-monopoly review on foreign investment of the State.

Article 3 The foreign-funded enterprises shall not use any fund of cross-border RMB direct investment to make any direct or indirect investment in securities, financial derivatives (excluding the strategic investment in listed companies) or entrusted loans within China.

Article 4 Any and all competent commerce departments shall clearly indicate the word "overseas RMB investment", amount of RMB investment, and the requirements in Article 3 hereof in the reply to the cross-border RMB direct investment, and shall forward the reply document on cross-border RMB direct investment to the People's Bank, customs, taxation, industry and commerce, foreign exchange, and other departments at the same level in a timely manner.

Article 5 Where foreign investors apply for changing the original contribution currency to RMB, they do not have to handle the examination and approval for changes in contract or by-laws; instead, they may go through formalities for registration, opening an account, transfer of funds and others with relevant departments and banks in accordance with the requirements of laws, administrative regulations and relevant provisions on foreign investments.

Article 6 If foreign investors make direct investments with RMB profits that are obtained from the invested foreign-funded enterprises in China and have not been remitted to anywhere outside China as well as RMB obtained from share transfer, capital reduction, liquidation, advance recovery of investments, relevant provisions shall be abide by.

Article 7 The above measures shall come into force as of January 1, 2014. The Circular of the Ministry of Commerce on Issues Relating to Cross-border RMB Direct Investment (Shang Zi Han [2011] No.889) and the Circular of the General Office of the Ministry of Commerce on Issues Concerning the Administration on Cross-border RMB Direct Investments Implemented by Business System (Shang Ban Zi Han [2011] No.1171) shall be annulled as of the date of implementation hereof. In case of any unconformity between the provisions of the Ministry of Commerce on the cross-border RMB direct investments and the Announcement, the latter shall prevail.

Ministry of Commerce
